## Full Story / About Copy (order it appears, reconstructed from press verbatim)

### Origin / Background
The Zerowriter Ink is a collaboration between Zerowriter (Adam Wilk) and Soldered Electronics, makers of the Inkplate series of e-paper displays. It is a commercial evolution of the original open-source DIY Zerowriter project (2023), which used a Raspberry Pi Zero 2 W, a Waveshare 4.2-inch e-paper display, 40% mechanical keyboard, Python-based software, and a 3D-printed enclosure costing ~$50–$200 to build.

The commercial Zerowriter Ink replaces the Raspberry Pi with an ESP32 microcontroller running Arduino code and upgrades to a 5.2-inch Inkplate display.

"Zerowriter runs on [an Espressif] ESP32 [microcontroller], and is written entirely in Arduino."
— Adam Wilk, Hackster.io (pre-campaign coverage)

Zerowriter came to Soldered with an advanced prototype, which Soldered helped improve and finalize (designing in a better battery charging circuit) and then manufactured on their assembly line in Croatia.

### Software Modes
Two modes: drafting and word-processing; toggle back and forth on the fly. Optional session tools: word counts, writing time tracking, goal-setting. Files export as .txt.

### Hardware Specs (from campaign page via CNX Software, Liliputing, XDA)
- Display: Inkplate 5 v2, 5.2-inch, 1280×720 resolution
- Refresh rate: Full 1.02s, partial 0.19s
- Processor: ESP32-S3 with Wi-Fi 4 and BLE 4
- Keyboard: Hot-swappable 60% (61-key) mechanical, Kailh Choc low-profile switches
- Battery: 5,000 mAh LiPo, user-replaceable, weeks daily use / month standby
- Storage: microSD card; up to ~3,000 lines / ~24,000 words per file
- Dimensions: 295×180×15 mm (12×7.5×0.6 in)
- Ports: USB-C (charging, data transfer, programming)
- Shipping (campaign price): $8 US, $18 international
- Expected delivery at launch: February 2025
