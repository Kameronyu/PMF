## Launch Agency

Adam Leeb confirmed use of a crowdfunding marketing agency. Verbatim:
"a full service marketing agency that specialized in crowdfunding"
"generally known as the premiere IGG agency"

Agency name: NOT IDENTIFIED in the post. Agency is described as preferring Indiegogo and having
"a close relationship with the IGG team."

Key selection factors cited (verbatim from adamleeb.com/kickstarter-vs-indiegogo-a-creators-perspective/):
1. "Agency preference" — their marketing partner specialized in and preferred Indiegogo
2. Past Kickstarter experience — previous Clapboss campaign "underperformed, partly due to lack
   of promised support"
3. "Indiegogo support quality" — IGG offered "concrete" promotional commitments via contract
4. "Platform features" — Facebook pixel tracking, secret perks, multiple reward purchasing,
   campaign extensions
5. "Partnership potential" — relationships with Arrow and Ingram Micro for possible flash funding
6. "Experimentation" — first-time opportunity to test IGG

Indiegogo support: "both our campaign strategist and sales manager have stayed in close touch"
throughout, providing "weekly catch-up calls."
Source: adamleeb.com/kickstarter-vs-indiegogo-a-creators-perspective/ (retrieved 2026-05-24)

## Platform Choice: IGG vs KS

This was Astrohaus's second major crowdfunding campaign. First was Hemingwrite/Freewrite KS 2014.
Decision to use IGG for Traveler was deliberate and documented by founder.
Key insight: they partnered with an IGG-specialist agency and leveraged IGG's contractual
commitment model vs. Kickstarter's non-binding promotional promises.
Source: adamleeb.com/kickstarter-vs-indiegogo-a-creators-perspective/ (retrieved 2026-05-24)
