## Story / About Copy (in order of appearance — reconstructed from press captures)

### Opening Claim
"The world's thinnest E-Ink tablet" — 4.2mm, Guinness World Record certified (certification event: Dubai, October 14–15, 2025).

"40% lighter than iPad" (295g vs iPad baseline).

### Core Problem Framing
"People often write down deadlines in their notes but forget to check them again because notebooks aren't connected to calendars. Note-taking is only 'effective' if it leads to action."

### Three-Device Replacement Pitch
"Why juggle three devices when one can give you more? A voice recorder, eye-friendly E-reader, and drawing tablet—all in one sleek tablet."

### AI Feature Narrative (verbatim campaign-sourced language)

**Live Link Notes:**
"Live Link Notes feature connecting handwritten notes to audio recordings" — notes stay synced to the timestamp in the recording.

**AI Search by Circling:**
"If you remember a specific diagram or a keyword but can't remember which notebook it's in, you can use the AI search functions to locate it instantly."

**Meeting Workflow:**
"Meetings, notes, and to-dos integrate seamlessly into your schedule, with everything staying connected and context-aware."

**GPT-5 / ChatGPT Hook:**
Campaign title explicitly claims "The World's First GPT-5-Powered Paper Tablet." Campaign copy also references "ChatGPT 4o mini AI integration" and "ChatGPT 4o" for AI Search. A dedicated hardware ChatGPT button appears on device.

### Feature List (verbatim / near-verbatim from campaign copy)
- "15 Languages Transcription + 133 Languages Handwriting OCR"
- "Android & Google Play Ready"
- Real-time translation in 10 languages
- 80+ templates for meetings and projects
- Native handwritten mind maps
- Google Calendar integration
- "Smooth page turns, fluent typing, and natural handwriting" (<20ms latency)
- NOTE OS operating system

### Hardware Claims
- "4.2mm thin" — described as "paper-thin"
- Weight: 295g
- Display: 10.65-inch E-Ink, 1920×2560, 300 PPI, Mobius Carta flexible plastic panel
- Processor: RK3576 eight-core AIoT chip with Neural Processing Unit
- RAM/Storage: 4GB / 64GB
- Battery: 4,000 mAh; "90 days standby, 14 days light use" (campaign claim); ~3.7 days mixed use (reviewer measured)
- Stylus: Wacom 3rd-gen EMR, 19g, 4096 pressure levels, 6 brush styles, magnetic snap holder
- Microphones: four professional microphones (some sources say single mic — discrepancy noted in notes.md)
- No front light (design choice)
- Fingerprint scanner on power button
- Five POGO pins (keyboard folio compatibility implied)
- USB-C, Bluetooth 5.2, WiFi

### Theainote.com landing page hero (current retail positioning, likely mirrors KS campaign):
"Jot, record, transcribe, and auto-summarize — all on a paper-like E-Ink display."
Subhead: "AINOTE brings AI into your meetings, so you can focus on what matters."
Description: "AINOTE is an AI-powered note-taking e-ink tablet built for meeting-heavy, information-dense work. Capture everything, extract what matters, follow through with confidence."

Three-step workflow on LP:
1. "Capture: Use handwriting and voice transcription together to keep up with fast-moving conversations"
2. "Extract: Surface decisions, owners, and next steps so nothing important gets buried"
3. "Execute: Centralize follow-ups, sync across devices, integrate Google/Outlook calendars"

Tagline: "The first AI notebook built for business"
