## Pre-Launch Blog Post (modos.tech)

**Title:** "Modos Paper Monitor Pre-Launch on Crowd Supply"
**Author:** Alexander Soto
**Date:** May 20, 2024
**URL:** https://www.modos.tech/blog/modos-paper-monitor-pre-launch-on-crowd-supply

**Product description at pre-launch (verbatim from blog):**
"an open-hardware 13.3-inch, 1600 x 1200 monochrome or color e-ink monitor" with "60 Hz refresh rate" compatible with "HDMI, USB-C, and major operating systems."

Note: Pre-launch spec listed 60 Hz; shipped campaign (Aug 2025) listed 75 Hz — spec improved between pre-launch and live campaign.

**CTA (verbatim, appeared twice):**

**Motivation framing (verbatim from blog):**
Founder "began this venture in February 2021, driven by concerns about excessive screen time and its effects on health. The stated goal was addressing 'headaches, eyestrain, and distractions caused by our reliance on digital screens.'"

## Pre-Launch Timeline

- **Feb 2021:** Founder Alexander Soto begins the project (motivated by personal eyestrain, pandemic context)
- **Early 2022:** Modos Tech founded; announced e-ink laptop and monitor prototypes (per Good e-Reader, based on article published before campaign launch)
- **May 20, 2024:** Pre-launch blog post goes live; Crowd Supply notify-me page active
- **May 21, 2024:** Tom's Hardware covers pre-launch: "New open-source, high resolution, low-latency E-ink monitor announced"
- **May 22, 2024:** Liliputing covers pre-launch
- **~14 months later — Aug 4, 2025:** Campaign goes live on Crowd Supply
- **Sep 18, 2025:** Campaign closes, 179% funded

## Launch Agency

No launch agency (Jellop, BackerKit, LaunchBoom, Enventys, M+R) mentioned in any available sources. Campaign appears to have been self-run via Crowd Supply's platform with organic press coverage driving backers. NLnet/NGI0 Entrust Fund acknowledged as partial funder of Caster development.

## Gap: Good e-Reader "What Happened" Article

Good e-Reader published a skeptical "what happened" article (pre-campaign) stating the campaign "never launched and likely never will." This article appears to have been written during the ~14-month pre-launch gap (between May 2024 blog post and Aug 2025 campaign launch) and reflects real pre-launch doubt about whether the project would ship. The campaign launched 3–6 months after this article, contradicting its conclusion.
