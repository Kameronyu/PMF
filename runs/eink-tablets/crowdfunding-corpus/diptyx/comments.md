## MobileRead Forums Thread — Page 1 (Dec 13, 2025)
Thread: "Diptyx Open Source E-Reader"
URL: https://www.mobileread.com/forums/showthread.php?t=371112
Started: 12-13-2025, 11:56 AM

### Theme 1: Initial positive reception / curiosity
- retiredbiker (12-13-2025, 11:56 AM): "This looks really interesting: A two-page e-reader that folds open like a book, and is not only open source but repairable."

### Theme 2: Skepticism about utility of two-screen format
- Renate (12-13-2025, 01:20 PM): "That's kind of cute and obviates the need for a book cover. Unfortunately, unless it were running some dual language book, I don't see the utility of it."
- tsgreer (12-13-2025, 03:09 PM): "I think the idea is fun, but I personally feel that one of the advantages of ebook readers, is they are easier to hold than a book."

### Theme 3: Resolution / PPI as dealbreaker
- bustacap (12-13-2025, 02:58 PM): "could be interesting for manga, if theyre supported / edit: screens are only 640x480, so probably not a good idea after all... :P"
- patrik (12-13-2025, 03:21 PM): "That looks like what someone who has never actually used an e-reader would imagine how one should be...Too small screens with still a large footprint. Fail."
- DNSB (page 2): Kobo comparison — "960x648 vs. 1448x1072" — inferior pixel density versus current devices

### Theme 4: ESP32 performance concern
- salamanderjuice (page 1, exact date not captured): ESP32 processor "is far too slow" and questioned capability with complex content.

### Theme 5: Price/value vs. alternatives
- Multiple users questioned whether $230 justified the limitations compared to mass-market alternatives like the Boox Go6.
- Frogm4n (page 1): criticized omitted specs (size, weight, absence of frontlight) in marketing comparisons.

## Distinct Objection Themes Summary

1. 137 PPI / 640×480 resolution inadequate vs. modern e-readers (~300 PPI Kindle/Kobo baseline)
2. ESP32 too slow for complex EPUB rendering
3. No search functionality in firmware
4. Price ($230) unjustified vs. Boox Go 6 or similar at lower price with better specs
5. No frontlight
6. No PDF support (only EPUB)
7. Two-screen format: utility questioned (no clear advantage over single screen for most readers)
8. Extreme international shipping costs (e.g., Argentina: $250+ shipping + taxes)
9. Software maturity concerns (rendering artifacts in sample images)
10. Form factor: may be heavier / harder to hold than a conventional e-reader despite book metaphor
