The one thing I feel I absolutely need for something like this is a way to sync to my Dropbox, which is where I keep my work. You mentioned possible future support for Google Drive... but wouldn't you have to add a wi-fi connection to do that? Or is it a matter of plugging it into a computer that is connected to wi-fi and it syncing up that way? What are the plans there?

ZerowriterCreator
3 days ago

Hi Noah! Great question. I'll explain. The Zerowriter Fold has the guts that allow for wifi. They are disabled by default. If the user wants, they can enable it in the settings. Wifi will only be used for sending documents to a connected service. The only service I plan on building support for is google drive, but any could be added. Supporting cloud services is tedious and difficult because they change often, so my hope is by providing the structure via the most popular one, other developers can contribute and we can support other services.

Hope this makes sense. Basically: I wouldn't want anyone purchasing this to expect a highly connected ecosystem of syncing. That is what the others in this space are good at, and at a higher price. But, we can start simple.

Teclas en español...

KateCollaborator
3 days ago

Hello Juan,

Yes, the Fold supports Spanish, and we'll roll out keycaps for different languages (starting with common Latin languages like German, French, Italian, Scandinavian languages, etc...) after the campaign closes. Via Kickstarter - you will have the option to add to your pledge after the campaign closes as we will be adding keycaps for different languages. If the Spanish keycaps are not available at the time of shipping, your device will come with the stock QWERTY ENG/US keycaps and we can get you the Spanish language keycaps as they become available.

Also - any Kailh Choc Keycaps are compatible so you could source your own. Our Keycap partner (FKCaps) also produces print-on-demand keycaps that work with ZeroWriter, so we can facilitate a Spanish Keyboard for you.

Another route we are exploring is a standard keycap pack for major European languages that includes all necessary characters (umlauts, ß, accented vowels, ç, ø, œ, etc...).

In short - it won't be a problem re: Spanish-language keycaps. Just a matter of when.

Please let me know if you have any questions or are curious about anything. I'm happy to help :)

Sincerely,

Kate
