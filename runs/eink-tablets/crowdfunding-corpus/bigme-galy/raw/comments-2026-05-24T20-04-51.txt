Gonna leave my interview here. Overall this was a miss for me. I got the Galy as well as the Eyemoo color RLCD tablet. These days I find myself using the latter much more often. The reason, in a word: Speed.

We all knew Gallery 3 is slow, but it doesn't help that Bigme didn't put much post launch effort to work around that as best they could. It was promised that more optimization to shave off time is possible. It's wasn't explored (or not much came of it). Working with the UI is a chore. Dragging or zooming a screen becomes a guessing game. All of this in it's fast modes. Forget using the HD Gallery mode for anything more than next page functionality without it getting on your nerves. I compare it to the Eyemoo (which, being rlcd, is not as contrast) I still get plenty of color and reflectivity (use a matte screen protector) and I get the tradeoffs of high refresh. I'd imagine Kaleido 3 with graphic processor yields significantly better results along with solid color. Lesson learned in not always adapting a technology that hasn't had any successes just yet. As nice as some, particularly comic/graphic images look, I also needed just enough speed to switch between them, annotate them for the aesthetic boost to be worth it. Other techs (Kaleido/color RLCD) outshine it so far IMO.

Stefan Lickl
almost 3 years ago

I've been using the Galy for notetaking and reading comics for the last two weeks and I really like it.

Battery consumption has been improved to the point where I don't really have to worry about it during the day and I only have to remember to charge it at night or in the morning.

I'm really impressed with the colors, it's on par with color printed newspaper, which was about what I expected. Refresh rate is also about what I expected from an epaper device.

The Notes application is usually responsive and very usable, but sometimes it glitches out and generates lines from off-screen or big circles for no reason. Might be related to the Navigation Ball being activated and registering touch events at the same time as pen input.

Also, the blue colored pen is not really usable. Due to how the display generates the color blue (it starts with a light yellow and then transitions to blue), it visually lags when using a light background or is hardly readable when using a dark background. Cyan does not have same problem with the perceived responsiveness, since the pixels start out dark and cycle through a lighter color before ariving at cyan.

Having to scroll and zoom is not a great experience due to the display latency and refresh rate, but you have to scroll the side bar in the home screen and settings. It would be great if the scrolling could be replaced with a paging mechanism for the sidebar.

But overall, I'm using and enjoying the Galy more than I expected. So a big thumbs up from me.
