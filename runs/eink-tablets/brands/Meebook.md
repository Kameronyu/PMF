# Meebook — Phase 0 Per-Brand Record

- analyzed_at: 2026-05-20
- brand: Meebook (also branded "Mebook"/"MEEBOOK"; manufactured by Haoqing; lineage from Boyue/Likebook)
- official_url(s): No official DTC store. Sold via Amazon (B0DWS9K4WW, B0CGMZX5W7, B09Q37DFJ7) and AliExpress. Third-party listings: levaplus.com. Unofficial fan site: mymeebook.com
- panel_type: true E Ink electrophoretic (E Ink Carta HD, 300PPI mono; M8C color variant 150PPI)
- sources_used: Meta Ad Library dump (adlibrary/Meebook.txt), Amazon listing copy, levaplus.com listing copy, Good e-Reader reviews (M8, P78 Pro, M8C), eWritable brand page, Notebookcheck, the-ebook-reader.com

## Product
- Meebook M8 — 7.8" mono E Ink Carta HD, 300PPI, Android 14, octa-core 2.2GHz, 4GB+64GB, microSD, Wi-Fi 6, BT5.2, dual mic/speaker, ~265g. Price ~$259 (Amazon, pen extra ~$30) to ~$340 (third-party listings; ~$310 on sale at levaplus). One review cites $359.99.
- Meebook M8C — color variant of M8 (B&W 300PPI / color 150PPI), 4096-level EMR stylus.
- Meebook P78 Pro — 7.8" mono Carta, 300PPI, Android 11, quad-core, 3GB+32GB. ~$200. Spiritual predecessor of M8.
- Meebook P78 / older Likebook-branded units — legacy entry-level e-readers.
- M7 — smaller-format reader (per fan site).
- Format: e-ink e-reader / light note-taker. No own website; marketplace-only.

## Transformation(s)
- Primary: comfortable, eye-friendly long-form reading — "paper-like reading experience" with reduced eye strain; positioned heavily as a manga/comics reading device (Good e-Reader: advertising "heavily focused on manga reading solutions").
- Secondary: paperless note-taking ("Paper-Like Handwriting Experience") — but reviews rate this weakly; a tacked-on capability, not the core sell.
- My read: the real transformation Meebook sells is "read a lot, cheaply, without wrecking your eyes" — value-led, not focus/digital-minimalism-led.

## Niche
- Price-conscious readers and manga/comics consumers. Listing copy emphasizes comic/document format support (DJVU, CAJ, CBR, CBZ) and "delicate visuals for images and comics."
- Strong evidence of Asia-Pacific reader markets: third-party ad resellers target Cambodia (Khmer-language reading), Taiwan (Bearboss), and Latin America (Spanish-language).
- No identity/community framing (no "for students," "for minimalists," "for writers"). Niche is broad and generic: "anyone who reads on a screen and wants cheaper."

## Mechanism / UM
- mechanism(s) referenced: E Ink Carta HD electrophoretic display, 300PPI, 256 gray levels, 24-level warm/cold SMARTlight, EMR stylus (4096 pressure), self-developed refresh algorithm.
- UM type played: Feature UM (weak). Meebook does not differentiate the category or the problem — it competes inside an established Product UM (e-ink = eye-friendly paper-like reading) and gestures at feature specs (Carta HD, 256 grays, Android 14, octa-core, 1TB SD expansion). None of these is a proprietary or defensible mechanism; all are commodity panel/SoC specs. Effectively a spec-list, not a true UM.

## Marketing framing
- angles: "easy on the eyes during long reading sessions" | survival (health/eye protection) | pain. Secondary: "carry a whole library / read manga affordably" | belonging (reader identity) | desire. Price-as-angle: "race-to-the-bottom" affordability | status | pain (avoid overpaying).
- awareness level(s) targeted: solution-aware to product-aware — buyers already know e-ink readers exist and are comparing models on specs and price. Not introducing the category.
- hooks (from ads): no genuine Meebook-run ads exist. Reseller ads carrying Meebook use generic hooks: "¿Lees mucho en el celular y terminas con la vista cansada?" (VicorTech), "Meebook M8 — easy for reading books in Khmer" (GooShop Cambodia). These are retailer hooks, not brand hooks.

## Sophistication
- market sophistication stage this brand plays at: Stage 4 (feature-level competition). The Product UM (e-ink = eye-friendly paper-like reading) is long established; Meebook does not introduce a transformation, problem cause, or alternative mechanism. It competes on feature specs (Carta HD, 256 grays, Android 14 + Google Play, octa-core, expandable storage) and on price. It lacks the trust depth for Stage 5. Note: Meebook is largely a passive marketplace SKU rather than an active marketer — it competes more on listing specs and price than on any constructed message.

## Reviews / VOC quick-read
- review volume + rough sentiment: moderate expert coverage, mixed-to-negative sentiment. Good e-Reader on M8: "generic, cookie-cutter," "Pen is Terrible," "Drawing Experience is Abysmal," weak front-light; B&W M8 better than color M8C (better touch, cheaper, no Wacom-license cost). P78 Pro: "bare minimum," clunky/poorly-translated firmware. Consistent positives: Android 14 + Google Play, storage/expandability, affordability, decent 300PPI text.
- brand loyalty signal: weak. Reviews repeatedly flag poor customer support and minimal brand presence. An unofficial fan site (mymeebook.com) exists — small enthusiast interest, but no brand-cultivated community.
- hidden use cases / notable likes & dislikes: hidden use case = manga/comics reader (the M8's de facto strongest fit per reviewers) and a cheap Android-14 device for sideloading reading apps. Likes: current Android + Play Store, expandable storage, low price, crisp text. Dislikes: bad stylus/handwriting (no Wacom on M8), weak frontlight, poorly-translated firmware, generic design, weak support.
