## Mechanism / UM
- mechanism(s) referenced: E Ink Mobius flexible (plastic TFT) Carta panel; WACOM stylus layer; deca-core Helio X20 ("low latency"); 4G LTE; full Android + Google Play.
- UM type played: Feature UM — the brand's one genuine differentiator is "first E-Ink Android tablet with 4G LTE + WACOM + expandable storage all in one." It upgrades the recognized e-ink-tablet category on a feature stack (connectivity + inputs) rather than introducing a new transformation or a novel problem framing. Paper-feel ("friction," "pen-to-paper") is a generic Product-UM borrowing, not owned.

## Reviews / VOC quick-read
- review volume + rough sentiment: Low volume — a few pro reviews, no consumer review base. Sentiment mixed/negative. Good e-Reader 3.0/5 ("I want the E-Pad to succeed" but "serious bugs… before prime time"). NotebookCheck similar.
- brand loyalty signal: weak/none. No repeat product, no community. Backer comment threads on Indiegogo show frustration over delays/bugs.
- hidden use cases / notable likes & dislikes: Likes — strong build quality, excellent audio (stereo speakers + mic, unusual for e-ink), "lightning fast" PDF page turns, modern Android + powerful SoC. Dislikes — noticeable stylus lag (worse than Sony DPT / SuperNote / Onyx), phone-OS UI scaled up = pixelated/fuzzy navigation, poor battery life (75%→40% in 4 hrs), weak default reading app, resolution-error software bugs. Wisky publicly stated it was "going back to the drawing board" — signaling the product never reached a stable shipping state.
