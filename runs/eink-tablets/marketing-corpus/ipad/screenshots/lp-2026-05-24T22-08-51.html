    Mac and iPad.
    Best in class and beyond.

    Both are powerful on their own, and together they unlock even more possibilities.

    Super speed. Serious smarts.

    • A+ battery life.

      Work where inspiration strikes. With Mac and iPad, you get long battery life — so you can stay focused without needing to plug in.1

    • Tear through tough assignments.

      Mac and iPad help you take on whatever class throws your way with the incredible speed, responsiveness, and breakthrough performance of Apple silicon.

    • Take noteworthy notes with Apple Pencil.

      Tap into even more capabilities on iPad with Apple Pencil. Transform handwritten notes to text, mark up PDFs for class, and solve equations like you would on paper with Calculator and Math Notes.2

    • Work flows from app to app.

      Use window tiling to arrange windows side by side, so you can manage multiple tasks and move between apps effortlessly.

    • Edit with a click or tap? Say less. Or more.

      Writing Tools in Apple Intelligence can instantly make your email and notes more concise, rewrite them until the tone and wording are just right, and summarize selected text with a click or tap.3

    • Ready to get things done.

      Built-in apps like Notes, Keynote, Freeform, and Pages help you take notes, create presentations, collaborate on group projects, and crush your coursework.

    • Summarize study sessions in a snap.

      On iPad, Mac, or iPhone, tap record in the Notes app to capture audio recordings. Then quickly generate a summary with Apple Intelligence to reference later.3

    Reliability. Ace the test of time.

    • Built to last from class to career.

      Made with durable recycled aluminum, Mac and iPad are designed to stand up to whatever college throws at them. So you can rely on them long after graduation.

    • Optimized for on the go.

      Take Mac and iPad with you wherever your day takes you — from the dorm and the library to the café. And if your device ever goes missing, you can easily track it down with the Find My app.

    • Speed with staying power.

      Apple silicon delivers impressive speed and AI performance, while fast unified memory and storage enable quick access to files. Mac and iPad can handle what you need now and are ready for what’s next.

    • Free software updates keep you up and running.

      Regular macOS and iPadOS updates deliver new features and help ensure that your device runs smoothly and securely for years to come.

    • Handled with AppleCare+.

      Students get special pricing on AppleCare+, which offers one-stop support and service for your devices. Get accidental damage protection and coverage for lost or stolen devices and connect with an expert anytime.

    iPad, Mac, and iPhone. Calm, cool, and connected.

    • Use your iPhone. From your Mac.

      With iPhone Mirroring, see and use what’s on your iPhone from your Mac. You can also drag and drop files between devices.

    • Sync your schoolwork across devices.

      Control both your Mac and iPad using a single keyboard and mouse or trackpad with Universal Control. Create designs in Autodesk AutoCAD on Mac, then slide your cursor to iPad to make precise edits with Apple Pencil.

    • Quickly share with AirDrop.

      Share photos and large files via Bluetooth between your iPhone, iPad, and Mac, and with other students’ nearby Apple devices. No internet needed.

    • iPad + Mac. Teamwork on full display.

      With Sidecar, you can use your iPad as a display that extends your Mac desktop. So you can work in one app while you reference another or mirror the screens so they display the same content.

    • Yes. With Apple Intelligence, you can use Writing Tools to proofread your notes, emails, and papers on Mac and iPad.3 They’re available in most places you write, including third-party apps and websites.

      1. On Mac, select the text you want to refine, Control-click the selected text, then choose Show Writing Tools.

        Tip: To open Writing Tools in some apps — like Notes and Mail — you can hold the pointer over the selected text and click , or click in the toolbar.

      2. Click Proofread. Apple Intelligence checks your text for grammatical and spelling errors. All changes are underlined with a glowing line.
      3. Do any of the following:
        • Switch between the updated and original versions of your text: Click .
        • View changes and an explanation for each change: Click and .
        • Revert to the unedited version of your text: Click Revert.
      4. When you’re finished, click Done.

    • With Quick Note, you can jot down ideas and add links, no matter what you’re doing on your Mac. And you can customize the look of folders and files with colors, symbols, or emoji, or choose a custom icon for any file or folder.

      Create a Quick Note

      If you’re working in an app and want to write something down, you can easily create a Quick Note. Do one of the following:

      • Use the keyboard shortcut: Press and hold , then press Q.
      • Use hot corners: Move the pointer to the bottom-right corner of the screen (the default hot corner for Quick Note), then click the note that appears. To change or turn off the hot corner, see Use hot corners.
      • Use Safari: See Add Safari links to a Quick Note or Add content from Safari to a Quick Note.

      To close a Quick Note, click in the top-left corner of the note. To open the Quick Note again, use any of the methods above.

      To always create a new Quick Note (instead of opening the previous one), choose Notes > Settings, then deselect “Always resume to last Quick Note.”

      Learn more about Quick Notes on Mac

      Customize a folder
      1. On your Mac, select a folder, then click .
      2. Choose Customize Folder.
      3. Do any of the following:
        • Change the folder color: Click a colored circle.
        • Add a symbol: Select a symbol from the list.
        • Add an emoji: Click Emoji, then select an emoji.

      Note: Although you can customize the Home folder, you can’t customize other folders that come with your Mac, such as Applications, Library, System, and Users.

      Learn more about folders on Mac

      3. To start the recording, tap .
      4. To take notes while recording, swipe down from the top of the screen.
      5. When you’re done, tap .
      6. To see the transcript, tap .

      Note: Spoken words in supported languages are automatically transcribed. If you don’t see , your iPad either doesn’t meet the minimum requirements or doesn’t detect a supported language.

    • Yes. Use Image Wand in Apple Intelligence to turn a rough sketch into a related image that complements your notes.

      1. Go to the Notes app on your iPad.
      2. Open an existing note or tap  to start a new note.
      3. Tap , create a sketch using your finger or Apple Pencil, then tap Image Wand.
      4. Circle your sketch, enter a description, then tap .
      5. Swipe through the created images to find one you like.
      6. Tap  to choose a different style — Animation, Illustration, or Sketch.

      Note: Apple Intelligence is not available on all iPad models or in all languages or regions. To access the most recent available features, make sure you’re using the latest version of iPadOS and have Apple Intelligence turned on.

      Learn more about Image Wand on iPad

    • Minimize notifications and distractions with Focus. Set a Focus on Mac and it easily syncs across your other Apple devices.

      1. Go to the System Settings app on your Mac.
      2. Click Focus in the sidebar. (You may need to scroll down.)
      3. Do one of the following:
        • Add a provided Focus: Click Add Focus, then click a Focus, such as Gaming or Work.
        • Create a custom Focus: Click Add Focus, then click Custom. Enter a name, select a color and icon, then click OK. For example, you might create a Study Focus. You can create up to 10.

      4. Turn Intelligent Breakthrough & Silencing on or off.

        When Intelligent Breakthrough & Silencing is on, Apple Intelligence allows important notifications to interrupt you and silences notifications determined not to be important. Any notifications specifically allowed or silenced will always be allowed or silenced.

        If you added the Reduce Interruptions Focus, Intelligent Breakthrough & Silencing is already turned on (and can’t be turned off). See Summarize notifications and reduce interruptions with Apple Intelligence.

      5. Do any of the following:
        • Choose which notifications to allow
        • Schedule a Focus to turn on or off automatically
        • Customize app behavior

      If you keep Focus up to date across your Apple devices, the changes you make on your Mac are reflected on your other devices.

      • Learn more about Focus on Mac
      • Learn about Focus on iPad

    • Use Stage Manager to keep your desktop organized as you multitask. It keeps the app you’re working with front and center, with other apps neatly arranged along the left side of the screen for quick access.

      On your Mac, you can:

      • Switch apps: Click an app on the left side of the screen.
      • Arrange windows: Reposition, resize, and overlap windows to suit your workflow.
      • Group apps: Drag an app from the left side of the screen to add it to a group of apps in the center of the screen.
      • Ungroup apps: Drag an app to the left side of the screen to remove it from the group.

      If you turned off “Show recent apps in Stage Manager” in Stage Manager settings, the list of apps on the left is hidden. Move the pointer to the left edge of the screen to show it.

      • Learn more about organizing your Mac
      • Learn about organizing your iPad
