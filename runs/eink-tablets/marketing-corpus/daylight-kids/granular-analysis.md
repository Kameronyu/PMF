## 7. Transformation Framing — Per Transformation

#### Transformation 1: "Finally, a computer that's good for kids" / kids become happier, healthier, smarter

- **Niche this transformation targets:** Parents of children ages 3–14 (broad parental buyer)
- **Verbatim setup copy:** "As parents, it feels like we're trapped in a never ending tug-of-war with technology. And it's making our kids sicker, sadder, and more distracted." (landing-pages.md, LP1 Problem section)
- **Verbatim build copy:** "The first calm computer designed to help your child develop healthier digital habits. Daylight is a thoughtful rethinking of the role devices play in our children's lives. It's technology that actually puts your child's wellbeing, curiosity, and growth _first_." (landing-pages.md, LP1 Product intro); "Daylight is different than others tablets because it is utility based rather than dopamine based, so kids can focus most on playing outside in the backyard or riding their bikes with friends." (meta-ads.md, Ad 1 & 2)
- **Verbatim payoff copy:** "Calm technology designed to make kids healthier, happier and smarter." (landing-pages.md, LP1 Hero subheadline); "parents can finally take a much deserved sigh of relief when handing over tech to their precious littles." (meta-ads.md, Ad 1 & 2); "To the end of iPad Kids…and to the new era of Daylight Kids" (meta-ads.md, Ad 1 & 2)
- **Angles attached:** A-01, A-02, A-03, A-04, A-05, A-06 (see §8)
- **Site-vs-ads divergence:** Largely aligned. Both site and ads lead with the "low stimulation, non-addicting" mechanism and parent-relief outcome. Ad 3 adds an education-necessity framing ("If your child has to be on a screen for school, let it be one...") not prominent on the main LP — a divergence toward school-requirement use case.
- **Source artifacts:** landing-pages.md LP1, LP2, LP3; meta-ads.md Ads 1, 2, 3

#### Transformation 2: Kids can use technology without losing focus or sleep — school-safe screen time

- **Niche this transformation targets:** Parents whose children use screens for school (school-age, 8-14); educators
- **Verbatim setup copy:** "Traditional school devices emit blue light and flicker 24/7, disrupting sleep and concentration." (meta-ads.md, Ad 3); "Young brains are extra sensitive to artificial light and stimulation." (meta-ads.md, Ad 3)
- **Verbatim build copy:** "Daylight's paper-like display lets kids read, research, and learn without compromising their bedtime or focus." (meta-ads.md, Ad 3)
- **Verbatim payoff copy:** "If your child has to be on a screen for school, let it be one that supports healthy development." (meta-ads.md, Ad 3); "A learning hub, focused & distraction-free. Holistic support for your child's education and curriculum: do schoolwork, focused study, read textbooks, write notes, and use teacher-recommended classroom apps—all in one device." (landing-pages.md, LP1 Smarter section)
- **Angles attached:** A-05, A-06 (see §8)
- **Site-vs-ads divergence:** Ad 3 foregrounds the school-screen-necessity framing more sharply than the main LP, which leads with the happier/healthier/smarter triple-claim. The education LP (/landing/education) bridges these.
- **Source artifacts:** meta-ads.md Ad 3; landing-pages.md LP1 Smarter section, LP4 Education

#### Transformation 3: Parent peace of mind / freedom from constant monitoring

- **Niche this transformation targets:** Parents (specifically those who currently monitor device use anxiously)
- **Verbatim setup copy:** "it's making our kids sicker, sadder, and more distracted" (landing-pages.md, LP1); "I don't have the same level of concern and worry and I'm not having to constantly check on them" (Sandy Abram testimonial, landing-pages.md LP1)
- **Verbatim build copy:** "Hassle-free parental controls. No setup required." (landing-pages.md, LP1 Safety); "Simple parental controls and carefully curated apps" (meta-ads.md, Ad 1 & 2)
- **Verbatim payoff copy:** "parents can finally take a much deserved sigh of relief when handing over tech to their precious littles." (meta-ads.md, Ad 1 & 2)
- **Angles attached:** A-03, A-04 (see §8)
- **Site-vs-ads divergence:** Consistent across site and ads. The parent-relief payoff appears verbatim in both.
- **Source artifacts:** landing-pages.md LP1, LP2; meta-ads.md Ad 1 & 2; funnel-mechanics.md
