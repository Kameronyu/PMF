## Product
Single SKU: the "Bible Tablet" — a 10.1" Android LCD touchscreen tablet pre-loaded with Bible software. Same hardware platform as the company's "Senior Care Tablet" and "Senior Gamer Tablet" lines (a customized consumer Android tablet, not a purpose-built device).
- Hardware: 10.1" LCD touchscreen, 2GB RAM, 32GB storage, dual cameras (2.0 / 5.0 MP), 5-7 hr battery, Wi-Fi, Bluetooth 4.0.
- Pricing model: **one-time purchase, $249.95**, or 6 monthly payments of $41.66 at 0% interest via PayPal Credit. Not a subscription; software is pre-installed and free to use.
- Bundle includes: pre-installed Bible software, on-board user guide, monitored support site, 1-year warranty, 30-day return policy, free protective sleeve + stylus (valued $20), free tablet stand (valued $15), earbuds, cables, charger, gift box. Optional silicone cover add-on $12.95.
- Bible version: buyer picks one at checkout — KJV included, plus NKJV, NIV, ESV, NASB, NCV, NLT, Amplified, The Message, Spanish, CSB, or Strong's Concordance.

## Transformation(s)
- dominant frame: **No transformation in the framework sense.** The product is sold as a faster, easier-to-see way to access Scripture during church/study — an access-and-convenience promise, not a "your devotional life changes" outcome. Closest to a transformation: keeping older buyers engaged with Scripture they would otherwise struggle to read ("the Bible Tablet will keep you interested").
- secondary: Family togetherness — "'FAMILIES THAT PRAY TOGETHER...' 'STAY TOGETHER'" — invoked as a benefit but not built out as a frame.
- evidence: Hero copy is "Moses Would Have Loved This Tablet" / "The Gospel At Your Finger Tips" / "Get Book, Chapter and Verse in Just Three Taps" — all access/convenience, not life-outcome. No "be present," "deeper prayer," or "focused devotion" language anywhere on the site.

## Niche
- niche: **Older / senior churchgoers** (with secondary reach to all-ages church members and youth-group fundraising). The Bible Tablet shares its platform and copy with the company's senior-tablet line, and the FAQ explicitly addresses elderly buyers (cites a 92-year-old user, recommends a "tech buddy" for setup).
- targeted sub-niche: PMBD cluster = the aging believer whose eyesight and tablet-fluency are failing but who wants to stay active in church and study group — sold partly through churches as a fundraiser and a way to "modernize congregations."
- evidence: "Whether you are 20-something or 80-something, the Bible Tablet will keep you interested"; "No glasses needed" (adjustable font); "you can have your tablet read the Bible to you when your eyes are tired"; "GREAT FOR USE IN CHURCH SERVICE" / "GREAT FOR USE IN STUDY GROUPS" / "GREAT FOR FUND RAISERS"; FAQ targets seniors "with some computer experience and/or with local assistance available."

## Notes / gaps
- Decision-relevant read: In-Touch is NOT a premium devotional brand and does not occupy the "focused, present devotional life" lane our product targets. It sells **convenience and accessibility to seniors**, framing the problem as paper-Bible fumbling and eyestrain — never digital distraction, never "be present." It is functionally a senior-accessibility tablet with Bible software pre-loaded.
- Why ~$250 (LCD, not e-ink): the device is a low-spec generic Android LCD tablet (2GB RAM, $249.95) bundled with free software and accessories. The price is a hardware-cost-plus value play, not a premium positioning. The buyer here is a **value buyer** purchasing a practical gift — there is no premium narrative, no craftsmanship/heirloom angle, no mechanism story justifying spend.
- This does NOT prove a value-buyer ceiling for the whole faith market; it proves the **senior-accessibility sub-niche** is a value-buyer segment and that **nobody has offered a premium, focus-positioned faith device.** The premium / distraction-free / "be present with Scripture" lane is entirely vacant — In-Touch leaves it wide open. Caution: it also shows demand here skews older and gift-driven, and the only existing faith-hardware buyer is price-anchored around $250.
- Gaps: no public revenue/units; no aggregated review count; eBay store feedback total not retrievable (page timed out). Scale is best described as a long-running micro-business.
