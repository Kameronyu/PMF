## Mechanism / UM
- mechanism(s) — how/why it works: a proprietary reflective "Live Paper" display — "a new type of display that's like E Ink, but faster… runs at 60fps" — delivers paper-feel + full app speed; amber backlight emits "Campfire spectrum with zero blue light" so it doesn't disrupt circadian rhythm; "DC Dimming (No PWM)" = flicker-free, "easy on the eyes"; grayscale + Kids software "eliminates the notifications, doomscrolling, and addicting algorithms." The how-it-removes-distraction story is a hardware/software story (no notifications, monochrome, no addicting feed), not willpower.
- problem-mechanism (causal story, may be shared): screens are "exhausting, addictive, and distracting" by design — "Big tech do bad… because of the systematic forces they're beholden to, ie business model & shareholder mandate" (founder). Devices emit blue light that disrupts circadian rhythm "even in night mode"; they flicker (PWM) causing eye strain; their software is engineered for dopamine/addiction, not utility. The Kids ad sharpens it: "Young brains are extra sensitive to artificial light and stimulation. Traditional school devices emit blue light and flicker 24/7, disrupting sleep and concentration." This causal story is shared with the broader digital-minimalism/blue-light category.
- UM: **Product-UM** — "Live Paper," a named proprietary display positioned as the unique solution (paper-feel + 60fps + zero blue light + flicker-free in one panel). Secondary **Problem-UM** lean — "utility based rather than dopamine based" reframes the device category around the cause (engineered addiction), which is more sharply positioned than generic blue-light talk. Note: "paper-like feel" itself is now a saturated category trait, not a UM — the differentiation is specifically the *full-speed* paper display.

## Notes / gaps
- This is the closest positioning competitor to the faith market's "calm, focused, present" transformation — yet it sells that outcome on **secular, health-and-philosophy framing** (digital minimalism, circadian health, humane tech, anti-addiction). The faith lane is wide open: Daylight never says faith, prayer, Scripture, or devotion. A faith-framed device could borrow Daylight's exact transformation ("deep focus, distraction-free presence") and re-anchor it to "be present with God / undistracted in Scripture" — a niche Daylight structurally cannot serve without alienating its values-secular base.
- How Daylight justifies $729 without faith: (1) reframes the category — it's "a new kind of computer," not a tablet, so no like-for-like price anchor; (2) health stakes — sleep, eye strain, attention, children's brain development make price feel small vs. the harm avoided; (3) values/martyrdom framing — "a more caring computer company," "we make almost no money on it," PBC status; (4) authority — founder vision + named tech-founder endorsements + major press; (5) scarcity — sold-out batches. Price is sold as ethics + health, not specs.
- Panel caveat: "Live Paper" is a 60fps reflective LCD, not electrophoretic E Ink; Daylight blurs this ("E-paper," "paper-like") as a positioning choice.
- Data gaps: no flagship DC-1 Meta ads were running at scan time (Kids line only); adult-funnel paid-social hooks are unconfirmed. Warranty length not extractable from FAQ. Lifetime revenue and current run-rate not publicly reported. Funding figures conflict (~$12–20M).
