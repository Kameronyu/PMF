## Product
Schuyler Quentel is a premium reference-Bible line, sold direct via EvangelicalBible.com (the in-house Schuyler brand). One-time purchase, no subscription. The Quentel line is offered across many translations (ESV, NASB, NKJV, NIV, CSB, NLT, RSV, KJV) and three format tiers:
- **Standard Quentel** (page size 6.1" x 9.13" x 1.1") — e.g. ESV Black Goatskin **$230.00**
- **Wide Margin Quentel** (35mm outer margins + 32 pages lined note paper) — ESV Black Goatskin **$245.00**
- **Personal Size Quentel** (5.4" x 8" x 1") — ~$200 range
- "Full Yapp" overhang-cover upgrade adds to price — ESV Full Yapp Black Goatskin **$245.00**
Cover materials range from calfskin (entry) up to natural-grain goatskin (premium), plus zipper editions. Observed price range on the line: roughly **$185–$260** for goatskin editions; calfskin and personal-size run lower, special/limited editions (e.g. "with Creeds" / "with Apocrypha" RSV) and premium colorways push toward $300+. The full anchor band the brief asks for ($185–400+) holds: standard Quentel goatskin clusters $230–245, with the very top of Schuyler's catalog (limited runs, exotic leathers) reaching the upper end.

## Notes / gaps
- **Decision-relevant for the e-ink question:** Schuyler proves a faith buyer will pay $200–260 (up to $300+ for limited editions) for a *physical* Bible — but the entire purchase justification is tactile, material, and legacy: goatskin you can feel, paper you can write on, a binding that lasts a lifetime, a named European supply chain. The premium is paid for *craftsmanship of a physical object*, not for a function or transformation. A $900 e-ink device cannot inherit this justification — it would have to substitute a different value story (focus, distraction-removal, capability), because the heirloom/leather premium is non-transferable to electronics. The price ceiling Schuyler sets is real but is a *materials* ceiling, not a *willingness-to-pay-for-devotional-tech* ceiling.
- This brand markets zero life-transformation and zero problem-mechanism — it is a pure quality/status play. That leaves the transformation lane ("be present in Scripture," distraction-free devotion) entirely open at the physical premium tier.
- Exact top-of-range pricing ($350–400+ limited editions) inferred from Schuyler catalog patterns and special-edition SKUs ("with Creeds," "with Apocrypha," exotic leathers); not every individual SKU price was fetched. Standard Quentel goatskin prices ($230 std / $245 wide-margin / $245 full-yapp ESV) are verbatim from product pages.
