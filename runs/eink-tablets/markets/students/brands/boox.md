## Delta vs. Faith record

Faith record finding: Boox is unpositioned for faith — zero devotional language anywhere. The delta vs. Students is the opposite direction: students/study language IS present, in product copy, ads, and a dedicated education-pricing channel — but framed as productivity, not academic outcome.

## Do they target students / parents?

**Students: YES, explicitly. Parents: NO.**

Verbatim evidence on site / blog / ads:
- Note Air5 C product tagline (used in multiple active Meta ads, e.g. Library ID 1717167979082832, 891569530344517, 1282754597087053): **"A New Study and Work Experience."** ("Study" is in the literal product positioning.)
- Go 10.3 ad (Library ID 1284473453868998): **"...an ideal companion for professionals, students, and creatives. Stay focused and think deeply with BOOX Go 10.3."** Students named directly alongside professionals/creatives.
- Go 10.3 ad (Library ID 758293657363778): **"For digital nomads. 10.3" lightweight ePaper tablet, your second brain for writing & learning."** "Learning" — student-adjacent.
- Back-to-school blog ("Excel Every Campus Moment with BOOX Tablets," shop.boox.com/blogs/news/excel-every-campus-moment-with-boox-tablets): **"supercharge your campus life," "your multi-functional study partner," "long study sessions," "distraction-free study," "help you achieve a deep flow state."** Pitches Note Air4 C, Palma 2, Go Color 7, and Note Max as the student lineup. Sale window: July 10–Aug 10, 2025.
- Older campus-targeted post still on shop.boox.com: "Note Air2 Plus: A Study Companion to Empower Your Campus Journey."
- X/@OnyxBoox: **"#StudentDiscount University students and faculties can save up to 23% on our selected ePaper Tablets at our official shop. Get yourself verified at [boox student store] with an educational institution E-mail."**

Parent-facing copy: **none found.** No K-12 framing. No "back-to-school for your kid," no parent-buyer hooks, no homework/distraction-from-the-iPad messaging aimed at parents. The student framing is consistently college / university / self-buy, not K-12.

## Price band relevant to student buyers

Yes — Boox has the cheapest e-ink ladder in the set, plus an actual education-pricing channel:
- Go 10.3 (Gen II) — $419.99 — explicitly pitched at students in ads.
- Go 10.3 (Gen II) Lumi — $449.99 (frontlight version).
- Note Air5 C — $499.99 — color, the "Study and Work" SKU.
- Go 7 / Go Color 7 — 7" readers, mid-$200s historical band (Phase-0 ladder $159.99–$819.99).
- Palma 2 / Palma 2 Pro — pocket-sized, pitched as "organization on the go" in the back-to-school blog.

Student-self-buy is realistic at $250–$500; the $419.99 Go 10.3 is the obvious student price point. The $900 product we're benchmarking sits ~$400 above Boox's student-pitched SKUs — a real headwind unless we earn premium with foldable + programmability.

## School / education program

**Yes — but college-only, individual-discount only, no K-12 program, no institutional sales motion to schools.**

- **BOOX Education Offers** (`booxstudent.myshopify.com`): up to **23% off selected ePaper tablets**, email-verified educational status. Copy: *"Whether you are a freshman to the college or a fresh graduate to the society, this is a special offer for your new journey."* Pitched at college freshmen and recent grads — not K-12.
- Verification gated by .edu-equivalent institution email. Faculty also eligible.
- **No iPad-in-Education-style institutional program.** No school-IT procurement, no district-level deals, no classroom-management software, no MDM story. Boox's B2B motion (business.boox.com, "Order for your team now") is corporate bulk, not school bulk.
- January 2026 help.boox.com thread indicates the discount has been intermittently unavailable — execution is sloppy. The program exists but is not a strategic pillar.
- Seasonal back-to-school sale runs annually (July–August).

## Focus / distraction-free learning / academic-performance language

Present but framed as productivity / wellness, not as "better grades." Verbatim:
- Back-to-school blog: *"distraction-free study,"* *"help you achieve a deep flow state,"* *"long study sessions,"* *"glare and eye strain from LED or OLED screens during long study sessions."*
- Go 10.3 ad: *"Stay focused and think deeply."*
- Recurring brand line (multiple ads): *"slow down, minimize distractions, and be present."*
- Recurring brand line: *"Brain Rot Is Real. Here's How to Reverse It." / "Your pocket escape from the algorithm."*

What's absent: **no claim about grades, test scores, GPA, exam outcomes, or academic performance.** No "study better and get better grades." No "students who use Boox raise their grades by X." The transformation Boox sells students is **focus + eye comfort during study,** not **academic-performance improvement.** The "better grades via focus" lane our product is positioned in is therefore NOT being competed for — Boox is one inferential step away (focus → ??? → grades) and never closes the loop in copy.

The problem-mechanism Boox tells students is the same one it tells everyone — phones/algorithms/LCDs steal attention. It is a shared causal story (reMarkable, Daylight tell the same one), not a Problem-UM. Boox's only real UM remains the Feature-UM noted in the Faith record: open Android 15 + Google Play on e-ink — for students this is decisively relevant (Anki, Notion, Google Docs, Drive, Kindle, Libby, OneNote, GoodNotes-via-web, lecture-recording apps all run natively).

## Buyer characterization for student purchases

**Primary buyer: student-self.** Skews college / university / grad school, tech-enthusiast lean. Evidence:
- Education discount gated by college .edu email — structurally excludes K-12.
- Ad copy names "professionals, students, and creatives" together — adult-tier buyer set.
- Back-to-school blog targets "campus life," "freshman to the college" — not parents shopping for a 10-year-old.
- r/Boox / r/ereader / r/onyx_boox communities are tech-enthusiast skewing; the recurring use case is "I want to read textbook PDFs and annotate on e-ink." Reddit volume around "Boox for textbooks" is real and organic — student self-buy is the dominant pattern.
- Distribution: DTC shop.boox.com (with student verification flow), Amazon, Best Buy — consumer channels, no school IT / district procurement.
- No parent-as-buyer signal. No gift-giving framing. No allowance/family-plan motion.

Boox is the e-ink player with the **highest organic student-self-buy appeal** in the e-ink stratum: this is the call. Driver: open Android + Play Store lets the student install whatever they actually need (Anki, Kindle, Libby, Notion, Drive, lecture apps, GoodNotes web), so the same physical device that works in lecture also works for leisure reading. reMarkable / Kindle Scribe / Daylight cannot do this — their closed ecosystems force the student back to a phone/laptop for app-dependent work, defeating the focus pitch. Boox alone closes that gap, and the Reddit / r/Boox / Logos-on-Boox community evidence confirms students are already self-selecting Boox on this basis.

## Notes / gaps

- MOST DECISION-RELEVANT FACT: Boox is the only e-ink incumbent that **both** has open-Android (so the student's real app stack works on it) **and** has any student-pricing motion at all — but it under-utilizes both. The student pitch is generic productivity language layered on a $419–$500 SKU with a 23% .edu discount; there is **no academic-outcome claim and no K-12 / parent-buyer motion.** That leaves two student-market lanes wide open: (a) "better grades / academic performance" as a verbal outcome claim (no e-ink competitor owns it), (b) K-12 + parent-buyer (no e-ink competitor plays here at all — Kindle Scribe, reMarkable, Daylight, and Boox are all student-self-buy / college-skewing).
- The 23% education discount makes the effective student price of a Go 10.3 ≈ $323 and Note Air5 C ≈ $385 — sharpening the $900-vs-Boox price gap that our product has to justify.
- BTS sale calendar (July–Aug) is the right window to assume Boox spend ramps on student framing; current May scan is in the trough.
- The faith record's "no UM beyond open-Android" call holds — Boox has not added a student-specific UM. Open-Android is now doubly load-bearing: it is also the de facto student UM (app freedom = the student's actual workflow), just not positioned as one.
- No K-12 product, no parent advertising, no school-procurement program were found in any source — confident "no" on those three.
