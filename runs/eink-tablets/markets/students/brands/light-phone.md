## Product
- Light Phone II — minimalist E Ink phone (calls, texts, music, alarm, calendar, maps, directory). Current MSRP ~$299 one-time + optional Light service plan (~$30/mo).
- Light Phone III — launched March 27, 2025. Hardware: 3.92" matte black-and-white OLED (press calls it "near-e-ink"), aluminum frame, fingerprint power button, USB-C, 5MP rear camera, 5G sub-6, IP54, user-replaceable battery, 6GB RAM / 128GB storage.
- Pricing model: hardware one-time. Pre-order $599 (limited), then **$799 retail.** Optional Light service plan separate. No subscription required to use a phone you bought.
- Within ~$100 of our $900 e-ink tablet — closest minimalist hardware to the target price band in the entire competitive set.

## Buyer
- primary buyer (student-lens, where the device crosses into our market): mixed — student-self (college, "nosurf" community) + parent-for-teen.
- purchase context: self-buy (digital-detox / focus); secondary = parent-buying-for-teen-as-an-alternative-to-smartphone. Not a back-to-school promotional purchase — Light does not run that campaign.
- where bought: DTC only (thelightphone.com). Not on Amazon, not in carrier stores, not in Best Buy. That alone caps the parent-buyer addressable market relative to Gabb / Bark Phone (Amazon + carrier-style channels).
- evidence: support article explicitly addresses parent-buyer setup ("the parent can set up the Light account for the phone and control it from the Dashboard website"). Third-party kid-phone roundups (SafeWise, Wait Until 8th, techdetoxbox) consistently include Light Phone II in "best phones for kids/teens" lists — buyer-side recognition that Light does not echo in its own marketing.

## Features  (specs / attributes — NOT claims)
- Light Phone III: 3.92" matte OLED (often described as e-ink-like), 5G sub-6, aluminum frame, fingerprint ID on power button, USB-C, 5MP rear camera (no front camera), no video recording, IP54, user-replaceable battery, 6GB RAM, 128GB storage, MMS-style image messages.
- No web browser, no email, no social apps, no app store, no WhatsApp, no maps that show ads.
- Light OS (custom Android fork). Optional Light service plan.
- Parental control via Dashboard (parent owns the Light account, child cannot install tools without parent approval).

## Mechanism / UM
- mechanism(s): hardware-level removal of the distraction surface (no browser, no app store, monochrome display). The phone *cannot* do the distracting thing — that is the mechanism.
- problem-mechanism (causal story, shared): "your phone is designed to capture your attention; the way to reclaim it is to use a device that can't." Shared causal story with every minimalism / kid-safe player in the competitive set (Gabb, Bark Phone, Pinwheel, Wisephone, Daylight, reMarkable, Boox).
- UM: **none** — at the brand level it is a Product-UM ("a phone designed to be used as little as possible") but it is NOT positioned as a unique student-lens mechanism in any owned copy. Mark "none" for student-lens.
