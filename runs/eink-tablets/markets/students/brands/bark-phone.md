## Product

Two SKUs, both rebadged Samsung Android phones with Bark's monitoring/controls software baked in at OS level so it can't be uninstalled:

- **Bark Phone** — Samsung A16. 4GB RAM / 128GB storage, 5000mAh battery, 25W charge, IP54, 50MP triple camera, 6.7" screen. **$10/mo × 24 mo** device payment.
- **Bark Phone Pro** — Samsung A37. 6GB RAM / 128GB storage, 5000mAh, 45W charge, IP68, Gorilla Glass Victus+. **$19–$25/mo × 24 mo** device payment (pricing page lists $19; product page lists $25 — discrepancy, possibly promo).

Required separate wireless plan (subscription model — phone is not standalone):
- Starter: **$29/mo** (talk + text only, no internet / apps / app store)
- Advanced Wi-Fi only: **$39/mo**
- Advanced + 4GB: $49/mo
- Advanced + 8GB: $59/mo
- Advanced unlimited: $79/mo

All plans bundle "a Bark Premium subscription for the entire family" ($14/mo retail value). Total monthly OOP for a typical buyer = device payment ($10) + plan ($39–79) ≈ **$49–89/mo**, no activation fee.

## Mechanism / UM

- **mechanism — how/why it works:** monitoring + parental controls baked into the Android OS at root level by the carrier/OEM relationship, so kid cannot uninstall the agent or wipe the controls. Bark's AI scans message / app / browser content and flags "concerning content" to a parent dashboard. Granular per-app / per-time / per-contact rules.
- **problem-mechanism (causal story, shared):** smartphones expose kids to porn / predators / cyberbullying / sextortion / addiction, AND parental-control apps installed on a regular phone can be deleted or worked around by the kid. The fix has to be at the device level, not the app level. This causal story is shared across every kid-safe-phone competitor (Gabb, Pinwheel, Troomi all sell against the same problem) but Bark's version emphasizes the "kid can't disable it" angle more than walled-garden competitors.
- **UM: Feature-UM** — "the only monitored Android phone where the monitoring is OS-level / tamper-proof AND still allows graduated social + browser access." Honestly: this IS a real differentiator within the kid-safe-device category (no other competitor combines monitored-but-permissive + tamper-proof at OS level). Strong Feature-UM, not Product-UM (the category — kid-safe phone — pre-existed; Bark Phone is an upgrade within it). Not a Problem-UM (problem story is shared).
