# Finder Brief — Dumb Device Market · Competitive Set

You build the competitive-set roster for the Dumb Device market so analyzer
agents can study each entry. **One job: find, stratify, rank.** No in-depth
analysis.

## Market

- **Product (fixed):** $900 foldable, programmable e-ink tablet. Pipeline B.
- **Niche:** **LOOSE — discovery-pending.** This market is defined by buyer
  *behavior* — purchasing stripped-down/specialist utility tech or
  distraction-blocking tools to take more control over their tech consumption —
  rather than a fixed identity. Sub-niches will surface from the scan. Likely
  candidates: ADHD-identifying adults, digital minimalists (Cal Newport
  readers), deep-work / knowledge workers, recovering-tech-addicted, parents
  modeling less screen time. **Do NOT pre-assume a niche.**
- **Transformation:** **more control** over your relationship with technology.
  Less distracted, more present, more intentional / agency. Framed as digital
  minimalism / unplugging / taking back control. **NOT** productivity-productivity
  (don't conflate; "produce more" is a different transformation than "control
  your tech").
- **Problem-mechanism (hypothesis, likely shared):** the phone / addictive tech
  is the source of distraction and loss of agency. Almost certainly a SHARED
  causal story across this market — verify; differentiation will be a
  Product-UM, not a Problem-UM.

## Competitive set

The set is **anything the buyer purchases to take more control over their tech
or attention.** Six strata:

1. **Minimalist / dumb phones** — Light Phone, Punkt, Mudita Pure / Kompakt,
   Wisephone.
2. **Distraction-free writing devices** — Freewrite (Traveler, Smart
   Typewriter, Alpha).
3. **E-ink dedicated** — reMarkable, Boox, Daylight, Kindle Scribe, Supernote.
   reMarkable / Boox / Kindle Scribe / Daylight were analyzed with corrected
   briefs in the Faith run; **their records will be reused, not re-analyzed.**
4. **Physical app-blockers / phone-lockboxes** — Brick (magnetic block), kSafe
   (Kitchen Safe — timed lockbox), Yondr (consumer pouches), Unpluq.
5. **Software distraction-blockers** — Opal, Freedom, One Sec, ScreenZen, Cold
   Turkey, Forest. The biggest paid + free options.
6. **OS defaults (light)** — Apple Screen Time, Android Digital Wellbeing,
   Family Link. The free baseline; one representative note (like YouVersion was
   for Faith).

## Sub-niche capture — REQUIRED

The niche is loose. For each entry, note the **targeted sub-niche** the brand's
marketing aims at (ADHD-identifying, digital minimalists, deep-work workers,
parents, recovering tech-addicted, etc.) with verbatim evidence. This is a
primary deliverable of the scan.
