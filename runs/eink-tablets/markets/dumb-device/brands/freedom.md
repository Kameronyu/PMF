## Product
Freedom — cross-device app/website/internet blocker. Single product, one app surface across Mac, Windows, iOS, Android, Chrome / Firefox / Edge / Safari / Opera browser extensions. Founded 2011 by Fred Stutzman; bootstrapped four years; "Freedom Matters" is the brand blog/podcast arm.
Pricing model — freemium + subscription + one-time lifetime:
- Freedom Free: $0 (limited — short sessions, core blocking)
- Premium Monthly: $8.99/mo
- Premium Yearly: $39.99/yr ("$3.33/month", "Save 40%") — 7-day free trial
- Freedom Forever: $199 one-time (lifetime); homepage currently showing "$99.50 for life, 50% OFF" promo against the $199 anchor — "No subscription, no recurring charges."

The Forever ($199) tier is positioned as a one-time decision for committed users sick of subscriptions. Premium yearly ($39.99) is the primary subscription entry. ## Features  (specs / attributes — NOT claims)
- Block apps, websites, or the entire internet
- Cross-device sync — single session blocks Mac + Windows + iOS + Android + browser extensions simultaneously (core differentiator vs. mobile-only blockers)
- Advance + recurring scheduling
- Locked Mode (prevents user from quitting a session mid-way)
- Custom blocklists + preset blocklists
- Ambient sound / focus music library
- Session length range: 1 minute to 24 hours
- Website allowlist / exceptions ("block-all except specified sites")
- Browser extensions: Chrome, Firefox, Edge, Safari, Opera
- Unlimited devices on all paid plans
- **Forever / lifetime license at $199 one-time** — captured here as a feature/business-model angle (no-subscription option distinguishes it from every other blocker priced subscription-only)
- Free 7-day trial on Premium
- Freedom for Teams / Freedom for Schools institutional tiers
- Free e-book / blog content (Freedom Matters) as lead magnets

## Mechanism / UM
- mechanism(s) — how/why it works: Software-level network and app-level blocking running locally on each device, kept in sync via a cloud session controller — when you start a session on phone, the laptop and browser are blocked simultaneously. Locked Mode removes the in-the-moment escape hatch (you can't disable the blocker by re-opening the app), externalizing willpower into the tool. Session-based (1 min–24 hr) vs. permanent removal — buyer keeps full access outside of sessions, lowering the commitment barrier.
- problem-mechanism (causal story, may be shared): "Compulsive phone use," "information overload," "social media-induced cognitive decline," "late-night scrolling habits," "digital dependency." Same phone/addictive-tech causal story shared across the digital-minimalism category (Light Phone, Opal, Brick, Daylight all say versions of it). NOT uniquely owned by Freedom.
- UM: none. Cross-device synchronized blocking is Freedom's strongest feature differentiator vs. mobile-only competitors (Opal, One Sec, ScreenZen) and vs. desktop-only (Cold Turkey), but Freedom does not *position* it as a unique mechanism — it's presented as table-stakes ("Cross-device syncing is core to Freedom"). Locked Mode is similarly a feature, not a branded UM. They lean on academic citations + 4M+ user social proof instead of a named mechanism. Could plausibly upgrade cross-device into a Feature-UM but currently doesn't.

## Reviews / VOC quick-read
- review/rating volume + rough sentiment: iOS App Store 4.4★ across ~5,200 ratings — solid but not flagship-tier (Hallow has 365k ratings at 4.9★ as reference). Sentiment skews strongly positive on focus + ADHD support, mixed on UX friction.
- brand loyalty signal: High among committed users — the $199 Forever tier exists *because* users repeatedly choose to convert to lifetime. Founder explicitly built the lifetime SKU as a loyalty/anti-churn play. ADHD users describe it as a "must-have," signal of repeat-use stickiness.
- hidden use cases / notable likes & dislikes: Likes — cross-device sync ("phone, laptop, and desktop all distraction-free at once" is the #1 praised feature in third-party reviews); Locked Mode for users who can't trust themselves; session flexibility (1 min – 24 hr) avoids the all-or-nothing trap of physical blockers; ADHD-friendliness. Dislikes (typical for the category) — pricing complaints (subscription friction before the Forever option lands), occasional sync glitches, learnability of the scheduling system, some "I can still find ways around it" determined-bypasser complaints (less common than physical-blocker bypass complaints since Locked Mode addresses it).
