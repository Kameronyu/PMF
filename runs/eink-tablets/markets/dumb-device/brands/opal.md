## Product
Opal — iOS / Mac / Android freemium screen-time control app. One product; users buy access tiers. Features: Focus Blocks®, Focus Score®, Snooze®, Difficulty®, App Limits, App Lock, Deep Focus (irreversible), Focus Report, Block List, Leaderboard.
Pricing (publicly listed on /pricing — NOT hard-gated, but in-app onboarding paywall is the primary funnel):
- Free: $0 — Mac & iOS, basic blocking, 1 recurring focus session, blocklist blocking, today-only focus score.
- Pro Monthly: $19.99/mo (3-day free trial).
- Pro Yearly: $99.99/yr (≈ $8.29/mo, 7-day free trial). Default upsell tier.
- Pro Lifetime: $399 one-time.
- Teams Pro: contact sales (single invoice, account manager, training).
- Student discount: up to 50% off Pro via application.
- 30-day money-back guarantee for Stripe purchases (does NOT apply to App Store purchases — material friction).

## Buyer
- primary buyer: **Self-buyer** — adult who recognizes their own screen-time problem and pays for their own subscription. Mixed B2B/Teams Pro funnel exists but is small.
- purchase context: Most purchases happen via App Store in-app subscription after onboarding paywall (per Adapty paywall library reference). Lifetime ($399) is positioned for committed self-buyers. Web/Stripe purchases get the 30-day guarantee; App Store purchases do not — Apple's refund policy applies. Seasonal triggers (New Year — "Stop Scrolling in 2026"; Mother's Day; summer) used as buying moments.
- where bought: Apple App Store (iOS/Mac, primary), Google Play (Android), web via Stripe (small share, gets guarantee).
- evidence: All ads CTA to `ITUNES.APPLE.COM` or `START.OPALAPP.COM` (quiz funnel → onboarding paywall). Pricing page sets Yearly as default selection — typical self-buyer subscription pattern. "30 Day Money Back Guarantee for purchases made via Stripe" excludes Apple — explicitly self-buyer-app-store reality.

## Mechanism / UM
- mechanism(s) — how/why it works: **Friction + escalating difficulty** layered onto app launches: scheduled Focus Blocks prevent distracting apps from opening, Snooze/Difficulty add increasing friction (math problems, breathing exercises, partner-confirm, etc.) to bypass, and Deep Focus mode makes blocks unbreakable for the session. Pairs with gamification (Focus Score, Gems, Leaderboard) and social accountability (peer benchmarks, public leaderboards). The mechanism is *behavioral-design friction* + *self-binding commitment* — Ulysses-pact for screen time, with rewards layered on top.
- problem-mechanism (causal story, may be shared): **The phone is engineered to capture you, and Apple's built-in Screen Time isn't enough.** "5 to 6 hours. That's the average time you'll spend on your phone today — often without realizing." "You've tried Apple's Screen Time and the screen always wins." This is the SHARED causal story across the entire digital-minimalism / blocker category — Light Phone, Daylight, Brick, Freedom, ScreenZen all make the same move. Opal's specific addition is "Apple's Screen Time is too soft" — but that's a category-shared frame, not a uniquely owned one.
- UM: **none.** Opal does NOT own a unique problem-cause story or a unique mechanism. Friction-based app-blocking is the category default; difficulty-escalation and gamification are differentiated features but framed as *the best execution*, not as a uniquely-named UM. The ad copy positions Opal on **"#1 / market-leader / world's most-loved"** and **execution quality** — that's a Stage 4-5 leader plays, not a UM play. Trademarks (Focus Blocks®, Focus Score®, Snooze®, Difficulty®) are brand protection on execution language, not UM-level positioning.

## Offers / value models
- Freemium with a heavy onboarding paywall. Pricing is publicly listed on /pricing — NOT hidden — but the *primary* purchase funnel is the in-app onboarding paywall after a personalization quiz, which is the standard mobile-subscription mechanic. Funnel effect: free-tier discovery is real but the buying decision is anchored to the paywall presentation, not the website.
- Tiered: $19.99/mo monthly · $99.99/yr annual (≈$8.29/mo, 4.8x cheaper per month, default-selected) · $399 lifetime · Teams Pro custom.
- Free trials: 3-day on monthly, 7-day on annual. ("Start Free Week," "Start Free 3 Days.")
- Student discount: up to 50% off Pro via application — buyer-acquisition into the student sub-niche.
- 30-day money-back guarantee — Stripe only. App Store purchases follow Apple's refund policy (the bulk of buyers). Material asymmetry — the guarantee is real on the web tier and weaker on the dominant App Store funnel.
- Lead magnet / quiz funnel: `start.opalapp.com` "Social Media Detox Plan" 1-minute quiz drives top-of-funnel email capture. Personalization output ("Light / Moderate / Heavy / Very Heavy") feeds into the paywall.
- No bundle; no annual-discount counter beyond the monthly-vs-annual delta; no ongoing seasonal discount surfaced (seasonal hooks drive *acquisition*, not price cuts).

## Sophistication

Differentiation work happening: (a) **Feature-UM-flavored execution language** — Focus Blocks®, Snooze®, Difficulty®, Deep Focus, gamified Focus Score; (b) heavy **enhanced-claim layering** with quantified outcomes (1h 23m, 94%, 6 years, 43%); (c) **PMBD-segmented creative** — separate ad funnels for ADHD, parents, couples, remote workers, students, hosts; (d) **trust + bandwagon** ("#1," "4M users," "Fortune 500"); (e) **value-model leverage** (lifetime tier, student discount, quiz-personalized paywall).
