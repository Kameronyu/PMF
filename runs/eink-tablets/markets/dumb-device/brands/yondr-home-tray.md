## Product
**Yondr Home Tray** — single consumer SKU. A signal-blocking, lockable steel/leather-trimmed box with Faraday fabric (copper + nickel) lining the interior. Holds ~6 smartphones. Key dimensions: 10.5"W x 10.5"L x 4"H, 5.3 lb. Manual key-lock under a leather front tab. Optional rear cord-pass-through for charging (though signal-blocking only works when fully closed — can't charge and block simultaneously).
- Pricing model: **one-time, $249**, DTC only (Yondr website / Shopify). No subscription, no bundle, no warranty/guarantee surfaced. Shipping copy conflicts on the page ("Ships in 5 days" vs. "Estimated to ship in 2-4 weeks"); Shopify currently shows "Sold Out."
- Parent-company context (NOT analyzed here, only sets economics): Yondr's main revenue is the B2B magnetic phone pouch — ~3M students globally, $25–30/student for schools (LAUSD contract >$5M; reported $5M in US government contracts Q1–Q3 2024); concerts charge ~$2/head. The Home Tray is the consumer side hustle off a B2B-dominant business.

## Mechanism / UM
- mechanism(s) — how/why it works: **Physical removal + Faraday cage.** Phones go into a sealed metallic-lined box and a parent locks it. Two mechanism layers stack: (1) physical friction — the phone is out of arms' reach and behind a key; (2) signal blocking — even if the device is inside, no notifications, calls, or texts get through, so the phone can't pull anyone's attention even passively. The lock is the parent-control layer; the Faraday cage is the privacy/no-buzz layer.
- problem-mechanism (causal story, may be shared): **Phones are addictively designed and pull people out of presence; presence requires their physical removal.** Copy expresses this through outcomes ("Parents aren't tempted by work emails, and kids aren't TikTok-ing… the dinner table is lively again") rather than naming dopamine / attention-economy science. **This is the SHARED causal story across the entire digital-minimalism market** (Light Phone, Brick, Opal, kSafe all say a version of this). Mark as shared.
- UM: **none** for Problem-UM (cause story is shared with the category). **Arguable Product-UM** — Yondr is the only consumer SKU pairing *physical lock* + *Faraday signal block* in a household tray form factor. Brick uses NFC tag toggling (no physical containment). kSafe uses a timed lock (no signal block). Yondr could position "lock + Faraday cage" as a Product-UM, but the copy does not — it sells the outcome ("be present") and leaves the mechanism in the feature list. Honest call: **none uniquely positioned.** They have the differentiated mechanism on the shelf but don't market it as a UM.
