## Mechanism / UM
- mechanism(s) — how/why it works:
  1. **Single-function hardware** — the device has no browser, no email, no app store, no notifications. Distraction removal is enforced by what's *not* on the device, not by willpower. Marketed as a *"walled garden for focus."*
  2. **Drafting/editing separation** — Smart Typewriter is positioned explicitly as a drafting tool; cursor/edit navigation is intentionally limited. The friction *is* the feature. Editing happens later, on a real computer via Postbox sync.
  3. **Mechanical keyboard + E Ink + aluminum body** — sensory / craft cues that make extended drafting sessions tactile and easy on the eyes (frontlit E Ink, no blue light).
  4. **Cloud sync as safety net** — auto-backup removes the historical typewriter risk of losing the draft, enabling the dumb-device experience without the dumb-device anxiety.
- problem-mechanism (causal story, may be shared): **The modern multi-purpose computer is the cause of writer distraction.** Browsers, email, notifications, and the temptation of one-more-tab break drafting flow and cap your real word count. This is the **shared dumb-device causal story** (same root claim as Light Phone, Brick, Daylight, Boox, reMarkable in their distraction-free positioning) — Freewrite is older than most of them and applied it to writers specifically, but does not own it.
- UM: **Feature-UM — "single-function dedicated drafting device with mechanical keyboard + E Ink + cloud sync."** This is a real Feature-UM inside the recognized "distraction-free writing tool" category (vs. apps like iA Writer / Scrivener focus mode, vs. paper notebook, vs. an old typewriter). The combination — physically dedicated + cloud-backed + writer-craft aesthetic — is genuinely Freewrite-positioned and was the founding pitch ("Hemingwrite" Kickstarter, 2014). Smart Typewriter is the most "complete" expression of that Feature-UM (full-size mechanical keyboard, biggest screen, longest battery). **Problem-UM: none** — the "computer/phone is the distraction" cause is shared across the dumb-device market.
