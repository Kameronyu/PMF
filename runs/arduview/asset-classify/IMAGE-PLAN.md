# Arduview Landing Page — Image Plan
_Art direction: LP product-image set. All video frames are 4K extracts; timestamps are exact 5fps grid points from contact sheets._

---

## Image Set (7 images)

### 1. HERO — Full-device beauty shot, display on, PCB visible, clean bg

**Slot / job:** Above-the-fold hero. First impression. Must arrest scroll, prove the product is real and beautiful, and land the core premise — transparent shell, game on screen — in under 1 second.

**Claims proved:** `transparency_internals_visible` (LOAD-BEARING), `see_through_display_on` (LOAD-BEARING), `collectible_object`, `build_material_quality`

**Source:** `FRAME: arduview-vid-18 @ 00:01.40`

**Why this frame:** At 1s 40f into the wood-grain orbital, the device is presented nearly flat from above at a slight angle — the warm oak surface fills the background cleanly, the cyan display is fully illuminated and legible, and the PCB traces + components are unambiguously visible through the clear resin top shell. No hands, no motion blur. The composition is device-centered with the entire unit in frame, reinforcing collectible-object quality. This is the highest-aesthetic unobstructed device frame in the bucket.

**Target aspect / orientation:** 16:9 landscape for a full-width hero banner, or crop to 1:1 for square hero module. Device centered, crop tight enough that the wood grain provides minimal breathing room — the device should fill ~70% of the frame. Preferred: 4:3 crop from the 3840×2160 source to preserve full device top-to-bottom while keeping some surface context.

### 2. TRANSPARENCY PROOF — PCB + battery through the shell, close detail

**Slot / job:** Immediately below hero or in the first feature section. The single image that proves "you can actually see inside." This is the load-bearing transparency shot — no ambiguity about what you're looking through.

**Claims proved:** `transparency_internals_visible` (LOAD-BEARING), `build_material_quality`, `hackable_maker`

**Source:** `FRAME: arduview-vid-12 @ 00:03.00`

**Why this frame:** vid-12 is the only footage where the device is opened up mid-reveal, with the LiPo battery cell (yellow block) and PCB traces both in focus and clearly readable through the transparent enclosure, held in a hand against a neutral background. By 3 seconds the reveal is settled — the battery and board are fully exposed to camera, there is no motion blur, and the neutral bg does not compete. No other frame in the bucket shows the internals this legibly. The FPC ribbon cable connecting the display is also visible, reinforcing hackable/maker credentials.

**Target aspect / orientation:** 4:5 portrait (crop from the 2160×3840 source). Device fills the frame vertically, hand provides scale context. Crop toward the device center — eliminate excess grey ceiling, keep fingers for scale.

### 3. DISPLAY-ON GAMEPLAY — Active game pixels on transparent pane, in hand

**Slot / job:** Feature section: "See through. Play through." The single image that proves the see-through display claim with live game content running. Must show pixel art on a visibly transparent pane — not just a lit screen.

**Claims proved:** `see_through_display_on` (LOAD-BEARING), `retro_gaming`, `pocket_scale`

**Source:** `FRAME: arduview-vid-16 @ 00:07.20`

**Why this frame:** At 7s 20f in vid-16, the Pong gameplay is mid-rally — a paddle and ball are visible on the transparent display with the cyan pixel art clearly against the see-through pane. The neutral grey background comes through the display area, proving the transparency of the screen itself (not just the shell). The device is hand-held at a natural gaming angle showing D-pad and A/B buttons. This is the cleanest frame where game content + display transparency + controls are simultaneously legible.

**Target aspect / orientation:** 4:5 portrait (crop from 2160×3840). Keep hand for scale, device filling upper 2/3 of frame.

### 4. RETRO GAMING (KEEP PHOTO) — Full arcade game UI, mid-game, live

**Slot / job:** Gaming feature section. Proves this is a real playable game console with a rich game UI — not just a demo Pong ball. Reinforces the retro-arcade emotional register.

**Claims proved:** `retro_gaming`, `see_through_display_on`, `pocket_scale`, `collectible_object`

**Source:** `KEEP: arduview-0001`

**Why it survives:** This is the only frame in the entire bucket — photos and all 5 videos — where a full mid-game arcade scene is on the display: health hearts, coin counter, player sprite, platform/terrain, level elements. Every video frame shows either Pong paddles, a Start screen, a Game Over score, or a menu. None approach this content richness. The foliage background reads as lifestyle/outdoor, which is differentiating context (pocket game, taken outside). The PCB is visible through the shell behind the display, covering the transparency claim passively. This photo is irreplaceable by any video frame.

**Target aspect / orientation:** 4:5 portrait as shot. The device fills the center of the frame comfortably. Crop only if needed to remove extreme edge bokeh.

### 5. POCKET SCALE — Two-hand gaming grip, device dwarfed by hands

**Slot / job:** Scale proof section. Answers "how big is it actually?" in a single glance. Two hands holding it in a natural gaming grip makes the 85×43mm footprint immediately intuitive.

**Claims proved:** `pocket_scale`, `retro_gaming`, `build_material_quality`

**Source:** `FRAME: arduview-vid-08 @ 00:00.40`

**Why this frame:** The very opening of vid-08 — before the device is rotated — shows both hands holding the Arduview in a natural two-thumb gaming grip against a warm wood-grain surface. The START screen is lit on the display. The device fits comfortably in two adult hands with the thumbs resting on the D-pad and A/B positions — the size relationship is immediately readable. Clean neutral-warm background. This is the best scale-communication frame in the bucket because the hand-to-device ratio is unambiguous and the grip is natural, not staged.

**Target aspect / orientation:** 4:5 portrait (crop from 2160×3840). Crop to keep both thumbs and the device — trim excess wrist/arm above.

### 6. ORBITAL FULL-BODY — Device on desk, 3/4 angle, complete silhouette

**Slot / job:** Specs/dimensions section or a "what you get" module. Shows the complete physical form factor — both ends, all buttons, the full shell — so a backer can mentally model the object before committing.

**Claims proved:** `build_material_quality`, `collectible_object`, `transparency_internals_visible`, `pocket_scale`

**Source:** `FRAME: arduview-vid-13 @ 00:09.20`

**Why this frame:** By 9 seconds in vid-13's orbital, the camera has swung around to a 3/4 angle that shows the device lengthwise — the control cluster (D-pad left, A/B right), the display top, and the transparent shell edge all in a single composition. The desk background is light neutral grey-beige. The PCB is visible through the shell from this angle. No hands, clean surface. This angle communicates the complete physical silhouette better than the top-down frames and better than the hand-held frames where the grip occludes the edges.

**Target aspect / orientation:** 16:9 landscape (native from 3840×2160). Crop to center the device, trim excess desk. Can also crop to 3:2 for a specs-section tile.

### 7. MAKER / INTERNALS DETAIL — PCB traces + branded silkscreen through shell

**Slot / job:** "Hackable / Arduino-compatible" feature section. Speaks directly to the maker segment. Proves the circuitry is real, deliberate, and exposed by design — not hidden.

**Claims proved:** `hackable_maker`, `transparency_internals_visible`, `build_material_quality`

**Source:** `FRAME: arduview-vid-13 @ 00:14.40`

**Why this frame:** At 14s 40f in vid-13's orbital, the camera angle has come around to show the back/underside of the device where the PCB fill — IC chip, traces, solder joints — is front-facing through the clear shell. The "ArduView" silkscreen branded on the PCB is partially readable in several adjacent frames (confirmed in photo 0004/0005 stills showing the same mark). The desk surface provides a neutral, professional context. This frame makes the "hackable by design" claim visual without requiring any text overlay.

**Target aspect / orientation:** 16:9 landscape (native). Crop to fill with device; the desk surface should be minimal context, not dominant.

### Gaps

**`whats_in_box` — NOT COVERED.** No frame in the 5-video bucket shows packaging, a USB-C cable laid out, or any unboxing sequence. None of the 5 current photos covers this either. This slot requires a separate product flatlay or unboxing photo: unit + cable + any packaging, shot on a clean surface. **Reshoot required** for this claim.

**USB-C port closeup** — the USB-C connector is not prominently featured in any contact-sheet frame reviewed. It can be inferred from the PCB detail (Image 7) but is not the focal point. Low priority for a crowdfunding LP (backers care more about gameplay and transparency), but worth flagging if the Arduino/hackable copy leans heavily on the port.
