## PHASE 0 — THE STANDARD, DERIVED FROM THE LAW (committed before prosecuting the prompt)

What sound DR market-pick reasoning REQUIRES, quoted from the law:

**(S1) Anti-fluke / proven-spend floor — demand must be channeled, not invented.**
- "Profitable ecommerce channels proven market demand rather than attempting to manufacture new demand… 'You cannot create mass desire, you channel it.'" (ecommerce--mark-builds-brands.md)
- "Multiple competitors (not just one) meeting revenue thresholds" + "Competitor monthly revenue: $300K–$500K minimum" (ecommerce--mark-builds-brands.md, Validation Thresholds).
- The floor is anti-FLUKE, and one competitor is not proof: market-evaluation-criteria.md lists "Proven purchase signal on paid? (Competitor doing $500K+ — Mark's criterion)."
- Low competition is a trap, not opportunity: "Chasing 'untapped' products with zero competition when proven demand is a prerequisite ingredient" (Common Pitfalls).

**(S2) Durability of demand — steady/rising, never parabolic.**
- "Google Trends: 5+ year window showing steady/increasing trend, not parabolic spike" (ecommerce--mark-builds-brands.md). Repeated in product-research--spencer-origins.md ("Stable or growing (not declining spike)") and Triple-Method Validation.

**(S3) Differentiator REQUIRED on mechanism AND avatar — no-differentiator = do not run.**
- "Decision rule: 'No differentiator on mechanism AND avatar = do not run product'" (brand-building--spencer-origins.md, 5 Mechanism Types).
- The canonical failure is Hair Dryer: "No differentiator on any axis. 7 competitors, same mech, same market. Failed regardless of copy quality. No UM + no avatar diff = don't run." (differentiator-framework__2_.md).
- "5+ competitors saying the same thing = claim discounted regardless of copy quality" (brand-building--spencer-origins.md; definitions.md 5+ rule).

**(S4) Sophistication and Awareness are SEPARATE axes; sophistication = how many times the claim was made; read mechanically off claims.**
- "Sophistication measures how many competing offers have already been pitched to a market." vs awareness "how much education the customer needs." (consumer-psychology--carl-weische.md; differentiator-framework__2_.md "Concepts Still Being Defined").
- Five sophistication stages with explicit required moves (consumer-psychology--carl-weische.md table; definitions.md Stage→lever matrix). "99.9% of e-commerce operators are in Stages 3–5" (brand-building--spencer-origins.md). Genuinely-new = Stage 1 (early AirPods/Ozempic).

**(S5) Believability is a gate, judged FRESH against the stated mechanism for the claimed desire.**
- "Believability Test: is the mechanism plausible enough for the claimed desire?… If Believability fails: mechanism education required before desire claim." (brand-building--spencer-origins.md, Sub-Avatar Desire-Product Believability Test). Tiered believability also implied by trust-signal law (definitions.md).

**(S6) Price conditioning / economics — the market is conditioned to a price; premium must be JUSTIFIED by a differentiator, never by discounting in.**
- "Amazon — extract price anchor (what customers are used to paying)" (product-research--spencer-origins.md).
- Premium is built on perceived value, not won by undercutting: "building brand perception that justifies 2–3x competitor pricing" and "Launching at a discount with plans to raise prices later — audiences acquired at discount price points do not convert at premium prices" (pricing--mark-builds-brands.md).
- Margin floor: "Minimum 3× COGS AND minimum $20–$25 gross profit per unit." (ecommerce--mark-builds-brands.md); "80% gross margin… 5x cost of goods" (ecommerce--carl-weische.md).

**(S7) THE TRANSFORMATION AND AVATAR MUST BE VOC-GROUNDED — verbatim, single-individual, not asserted, not Frankensteined.**
- "Voice-of-Customer Research… The foundation of all copy, offer construction, and funnel messaging." (consumer-psychology--carl-weische.md).
- Desire must be verbatim: "Verify with Command+F — the words must appear in research, not be inferred. If 'hydrated' doesn't appear in research, don't put it in copy." (definitions.md, Desire; Command+F rule).
- Sub-niche/avatar validity is strict: "the co-occurrence must be observed in single individuals… Threshold: 5+ independent individuals… assembling traits across separate people produces a Frankenstein avatar." (definitions.md, Sub-niche).
- "Transformation… The outcome your product is marketed to produce" — but desire-mining "can surface hidden transformations the seller wasn't marketing" (definitions.md), i.e. the REAL transformation is a VOC finding, not a competitor-asserted claim category. NTPMA also orders Niche → Transformation FIRST, depending on the niche (offer-construction--carl-weische.md), and "Market validated the moment money is committed" — validation is an empirical act, not an assertion.

**(S8) Avatar quality criteria for the pick.**
- market-evaluation-criteria.md Avatar block: "Do they identify with the problem? / Have they bought for this problem before? / Unsatisfied with current solutions? / No brand loyalty? / VOC accessible?" — these are demanded inputs to the pick.

**(S9) Demand magnitude must be scored from actual signal, and "pain market vs identity market" is a real distinction.**
- Four core drivers incl. belonging/status (definitions.md; consumer-psychology--carl-weische.md Mass Market Desires — belonging and social-comparison are legitimate mass desires).
- Desire severity = "intensity × emotional charge × frequency" (market-evaluation-criteria.md). Identity/belonging cells have legitimate demand, but the magnitude still has to be MEASURED, not asserted.

So the tradeoff is DR-DEFENSIBLE under exactly two conditions: (a) the output stays PROVISIONAL and routes only to a dry test, never to MOQ/commitment; and (b) the asserted-vs-validated gap is surfaced, not buried. **Were the conditions met here?
