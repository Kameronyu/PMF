## PHASE 0 — THE STANDARD, DERIVED FROM THE LAW BEFORE READING THE PROMPT'S ARGUMENT

What sound DR reasoning REQUIRES of a collection/classification stage, quoted from THE LAW, committed before adopting any of the prompt's self-justification:

`definitions.md`: "Transformation — The outcome your product is marketed to produce." And the sharper warning: "Desire ≠ transformation … desire mining can surface hidden transformations the seller wasn't marketing." `differentiator-framework__2_.md`: "Concepts Still Being Defined" lists what's solid; the canon repeatedly insists the true transformation is found in customer language. `consumer-psychology--mark-builds-brands.md`: "The best raw data is customers' own words." `market-evaluation-criteria.md` solving order ends at "5. VOC research." STANDARD: a transformation label is sound only if it is either (a) explicitly scoped as the *claimed/asserted* transformation, OR (b) VOC-grounded. Asserting it as fact without either is a violation.

**S2 — Sophistication is a market property (niche × transformation), assessed by auditing competitors' most-saturated claim layer.**

STANDARD: a stage call is sound only if it counts real typed claims from real competitors *in that cell*, and the cell is a genuine niche × transformation.

**S3 — A claim is a verbal promise of an outcome; a feature/mechanism is not a claim and must not be counted as one.**
`definitions.md`: "Claim — The specific verbal promise the brand makes about what the product will do." "Enhanced claim ≠ UM. UM is why the product works (mechanism). STANDARD: typing must separate feature/spec/mechanism-as-spec from outcome-promises. Miscounting inflates the stage.

**S4 — UM ownability is a real differentiator only if the mechanism is a genuine mechanism, not a feature commodity, and "no differentiator = failure."**
`differentiator-framework__2_.md`: "No differentiator = failure (Hair Dryer)." "No UM + no avatar diff = don't run." `definitions.md`: distinguishes Problem/Product/Feature UM. STANDARD: clustering "mechanisms" and counting brands is sound only if what's clustered are actually mechanisms (how/why), and only if shared/unique tracks a defensible differentiator — not noise.

**S5 — Demand durability / fad-death is a legitimate kill-relevant signal.**
`consumer-psychology--spencer-origins.md` (DESIRE: Staying Power), `case-studies--spencer-origins.md` Dubai Chocolate boom-bust ("new mechanism … rapid saturation 6-month cycle"). STANDARD: a novelty-tech play must carry a durability read; an empty/guessed durability column is a real gap.

**S6 — Niche and market are precisely defined; a "cell" must be a real niche × transformation, not a constructed grouping.**
`definitions.md`: "Niche — A community or identity group that consumes content together … independent of any specific transformation. Venue test: is there a place (subreddit, FB group, forum, hashtag) where this group gathers." "Market — Niche × transformation. The unit of competition." **S7 — Saturation must never be pooled, and competitive-set membership must be honest.**
`brand-building--spencer-origins.md` Competitive Foreplay Board: "Aggregate ALL competitor AND alternative-solution ads." `angle.md` market test: "When you change the desire, does the competitive set change?" STANDARD: which brands count in a cell is a load-bearing honesty test; excluding brands changes the saturation read and the stage.

**S8 — Operator-note standard (the missing VOC tradeoff).** From the law: is building a belief chain on an ASSERTED transformation DR-permissible when speed is the priority? The law that bears: `definitions.md` "true transformation is VOC-found"; `case-studies` Hexclad/Ridge "desire-first" require articulated customer frustration; `consumer-psychology--mark-builds-brands.md` "no copy is written until the Necessary Beliefs Document exists" and that doc is VOC-derived. STANDARD: deferring VOC is DR-permissible ONLY IF the asserted transformation is (i) explicitly labelled as provisional/claimed and (ii) not used as if it were the validated buyer transformation downstream. If it is laundered into a committed belief chain, the tradeoff violates the law.

## PHASE 1 — BREAKING EACH DECISION

### DECISION 1 — Transformation stamped from competitor claims, VOC deferred (the operator-note charge)

- **RULE-SOUNDNESS: SURVIVED (with a strict, met condition).** The prompt does not assert the *true* transformation. It scopes itself explicitly: the run "surfaces the *claimed* transformations competitors assert; the *true* transformation … is a later VOC/review-mine job" (`arduview-pre-research-plan.md`). The output enforces this in machine-readable provenance: `space-map.json._provenance` = "Canonical transformations are claim-categories competitors ASSERT, not validated customer transformations — true transformation is a later VOC/review-mine finding." This is exactly the S1/S8 escape condition: VOC-deferral is DR-permissible because the asserted transformation is flagged as provisional, not laundered as validated. Quoted law it conforms to: `definitions.md` "Claim ≈ transformation stated as a promise … Count base claims across competitors to assess transformation-level saturation" — i.e., the law explicitly sanctions reading *claimed* transformations off competitors for the *sophistication* purpose. **The tradeoff is DR-defensible AT THIS STAGE.** The charge does not land here.
- **BUT — the condition is conditional, and the condition's enforcement is only a label.** The law permits deferral only IF downstream does not over-claim. The prompt itself flags the risk ("Whoever reads the output must not over-claim it past supply-side spend validation"). That guard is a *sentence*, not a mechanism. PHASE 2 is where this becomes the weakest join — the violation, if it exists, is downstream over-reliance, not the collection stamp. Held for Phase 2.
- **DATA-SUFFICIENCY: SURVIVED.** The chosen transformation `novelty-object-own` cites real claim data in `combos[].claims[]` (4 claims from playdate/pocket-operator/divoom). Not a naked assertion.

### DECISION 2 — `novelty-object-own` as a "transformation"

- **RULE-SOUNDNESS: VIOLATION (degrading, possibly fatal to the chosen-cell semantics).** S1/S6 test: "Transformation — the outcome your product is marketed to produce … state it as 'X happens to the buyer.'" Apply the test to `novelty-object-own`: what happens to the buyer? "Owning a novelty object" is not an outcome that happens to the buyer — it is *the act of purchase itself* (own the thing). `definitions.md` warns precisely against this: "Common confusion: Not a niche. Not a feature. Not a benefit list. The transformation is the single outcome the product promises." Owning a remarkable object is closer to a *purchase behavior / object-relationship* than a transformation (status? identity? the law says trace to a driver). The pre-research plan itself admits the transformation slot is OPEN and unknown ("status? hobby identity? belonging? cutting-edge signalling?") — yet the output collapses that open slot into `novelty-object-own`, which answers none of those. Quoting `definitions.md`: "Desire ≠ transformation." `novelty-object-own` reads as a *desire-to-possess* dressed as a transformation. The label smuggles the unknown (the real driver) under a placeholder that *looks* resolved. This is the "transformation in disguise" failure mode inverted: a non-transformation promoted to transformation.
- **DATA-SUFFICIENCY: CANNOT ASSESS → leaning VIOLATION.** The claims cited ("familiar but unlike anything you've ever seen," "bring personality … into your workspace," "a wall of sound in your pocket") are heterogeneous: a novelty claim, a workspace-decor claim, and an audio-capability claim. Clustering three unlike outcome-promises into one canonical "novelty-object-own" is asserted, not demonstrated. The missing datum: a stated rule for *why* these three roll up to one transformation rather than three (decor, music-making, novelty are arguably three different transformations / even three different markets per `angle.md`'s "does the competitive set change?" test — a Divoom pixel-art desk display and a Pocket Operator synth do NOT share a competitive set).

### DECISION 3 — `edc-aesthetic-collectors` as a "niche"

- **RULE-SOUNDNESS: CANNOT ASSESS (degrading).** S6 venue test: "Is there a place (subreddit, FB group, forum, hashtag) where this group gathers without your transformation being the reason?" The output provides no venue evidence for `edc-aesthetic-collectors` as a gathering community. `definitions.md` does list "EDC" adjacent identities are plausible niches, and the pre-research plan named EDC as a candidate territory — so it is not invented. But whether "edc-aesthetic-collectors" is ONE niche or a stapling of two (EDC-carry people + aesthetic-desk-object collectors, who gather in different venues) is unestablished. Missing datum: the venue/community evidence that makes this a single niche rather than a Frankenstein grouping (`definitions.md` warns against "Frankenstein avatar — a profile that doesn't describe any actual buyer," albeit that rule is stated for sub-niches; the principle — single real group — applies).
- **DATA-SUFFICIENCY: VIOLATION (degrading).** The niche label carries no `raw_variants` in the cell the way mechanisms and claims do. The prompt's own RULE — "Every canonical label must trace to raw variants actually present in the dumps. No invented categories" — is not visibly satisfied for the niche label in `space-map.json` (mechanisms_in_play and claims have raw_variants; the niche does not). By the prompt's own standard, the niche canonical is undertraced.

### DECISION 4 — Claim-typing (direct / enlarged / mechanism / enhanced)

- **RULE-SOUNDNESS: SURVIVED.** The typing schema maps cleanly to S3 and to `definitions.md` (claim vs enhanced claim vs UM) and `angle.md` sophistication stages. The FEATURE-vs-CLAIM TRAP inoculation ("a striking FEATURE is NOT a claim and is NOT typed … Miscounting a headline feature as a claim inflates the stage/saturation read") is exactly S3, correctly stated. The "mechanism alone is not a claim; it becomes claim_type:'mechanism' only when bound to an outcome" is `angle.md` verbatim-in-spirit. This logic is law-conformant.
- **DATA-SUFFICIENCY: VIOLATION (degrading) on the actual typing executed.** The single enlarged claim is the Pocket Operator blurb: "pocket operators are small, ultra-portable music devices, with studio quality sound and the flexibility to make music on the go. affordable for everyone…" typed `enlarged` with rationale "specifies quality level (studio quality) and condition (on the go)." Test against `definitions.md`: enhanced/enlarged claim = "base claim + qualifier" where the base is "an outcome promise." "Studio quality sound" and "make music on the go" are *feature/capability descriptions*, not a quantified/conditional *outcome* in the buyer's life. By the prompt's OWN FEATURE-vs-CLAIM TRAP, "studio quality sound" is arguably a feature-as-spec, not an outcome-claim — the typing may itself be the inflation the inoculation warns against. This is a self-inconsistency: the rule is sound (survives) but the application contradicts the rule (data-insufficient / mistyped). Degrading, not fatal, because the resulting stage is low regardless.

### DECISION 5 — Sophistication stage (mechanical: stage = highest claim_type tier 2+ live in-geo brands deploy; 5+ rule)

- **RULE-SOUNDNESS: SURVIVED.** The mechanical rule is a faithful operationalization of `angle.md`/`definitions.md`: "stage = the highest claim_type tier that 2+ LIVE in-geo brands deploy"; "5+ RULE: if 5+ live brands make the same base claim, that claim is discounted." But the artifact does not state it, so I cannot confirm the stage the pipeline will act on.)

### DECISION 6 — Mechanism ownability (shared 3+ / unique ≤2)

- **RULE-SOUNDNESS: VIOLATION (degrading) — "mechanism" is being used loosely against the law's definition.** S4 / `definitions.md`: "Mechanism ≠ UM. Mechanism is the underlying biological or causal pathway." UM is how a product is positioned around it. The space-wide `mechanisms_in_play[]` clusters include genuine mechanisms ("FPGA-based hardware-accurate (not emulated)", "RP2040 core") but ALSO non-mechanisms: "Long battery life" (raw: "1 month battery life") and "Premium high-density display" (raw: "3.5\", 615 ppi, LCD", "Gorilla Glass"). Those are FEATURES/specs — the very things the FEATURE-vs-CLAIM TRAP says not to treat as load-bearing differentiators. Calling a spec a "mechanism" and then computing ownability on it (S4) launders a feature into a UM-availability signal. Per `differentiator-framework__2_.md`, a feature that everyone can copy is not a UM; counting it as "unique" (brand_count:1) overstates the white space. Degrading: it inflates the count of "ownable mechanisms" with commodity specs.
- **DATA-SUFFICIENCY: SURVIVED on traceability.** Every mechanism cluster carries `raw_variants` tracing to per-pitch reads, satisfying the prompt's "must trace to raw variants" rule. The shared/unique counts are computed against visible brand attributions. The data is present even where the *category* (mechanism vs feature) is mis-assigned.
- **Cross-cell consistency flag:** space-wide, "Distinctive low-res / monochrome / pixel display" = `shared` (brand_count:4); in the chosen cell it is `unique` (brand_count:2, divoom+playdate). The prompt explicitly licenses this ("ownability recomputed PER CELL"). SURVIVED — but it means the chosen cell's "unique" white-space reads are an artifact of the cell being only 3 brands deep, not of genuine scarcity. See Phase 2.

### DECISION 7 — Bet type, "read off the page … WITHOUT needing to know the customer's true dream/desire"

- **RULE-SOUNDNESS: SURVIVED, narrowly.** The claim that structural bet is page-readable without knowing the true desire is defensible against `consumer-psychology--alex-hormozi.md` "Actions-Only Reality Filter" / "Ignore the Black Box": "focus on observable inputs and outputs," "would this be admitted to a court as evidence." A brand's leading on-page positioning IS court-admissible (the page says what it says); the customer's interior dream is not. So reading the *structural bet* off observable positioning, while deferring the *true transformation* to VOC, is a clean separation the law supports. Not a violation.
- **DATA-SUFFICIENCY: CANNOT ASSESS.** No `bet_types[]` array and no per-brand `bet_type` / `bet_type_basis` appear in the delivered `space-map.json`. The prompt mandates them ("Record a `bet_type_basis` that QUOTES/CITES the page signal"; "unify … into canonical `bet_types[]`"). The artifact omits them entirely. The decision's logic survives; its execution is unverifiable from the evidence — missing datum: the bet_type fields themselves. This matters because the pre-research plan says bet_type × niche × durability is the eventual decision crossing.

### DECISION 8 — Demand trend / fad-death durability

- **RULE-SOUNDNESS: SURVIVED.** Carrying a per-brand fetched Google Trends shape as the durability signal, and refusing to recompute or guess ("If a brand's demand_trend.shape is unknown, list it as unknown — never guess"), is exactly S5 + the Actions-Only filter. Correct DR logic for a novelty-tech play where the pre-research plan correctly identifies "fad-death as its primary risk."
- **DATA-SUFFICIENCY: VIOLATION (FATAL to the load-bearing durability validation as delivered).** The pre-research plan is unambiguous: "The durability signal (trend shape) is therefore the load-bearing supply-side field for this product — an empty durability column silently disables the most important validation." The delivered `space-map.json` contains **no `demand_trend` field anywhere** — not space-wide, not per-brand, not on the cell. The single most important supply-side validation for this exact product is, by the operator's own stated standard, silently disabled in the artifact. This is the gravest data-sufficiency failure in the segment: the kill-relevant signal the whole novelty bet hinges on is absent from the decision input. (The plan asserts "Trend source: confirmed present in fetch … so demand_trend is populated, not unknown" — but the *evidence I was given* does not show it populated. Per the hard prohibition I cannot fetch the brands.json to verify; on the evidence in front of me, it is missing.)

### DECISION 9 — Comparable-bet-seed exclusion + single-strong-comparable floor override

- **RULE-SOUNDNESS: SURVIVED with a noted hazard.** Keeping "they prove the bet, not the cell" separate (Nothing Phone et al. excluded from cell saturation) is logically clean — `definitions.md` market = niche × transformation; bet-evidence brands proving "novel-hardware-as-lead won durably" are not in-cell competitors, so excluding them from saturation is correct (S7: count the right competitive set). The single-comparable override is explicitly bounded: "proceed on a single strong validated comparable — but only to a DRY TEST, not to commitment … validation-before-MOQ (10–30 real purchase signals) is the backstop." This is the Tradeoff Framework done consciously (`consumer-psychology--alex-hormozi.md`: "Be conscious of what you're trading AND what you're trading it for"). The anti-fluke default of 2+ is consciously overridden with a stated safety net. Not a violation.
- **HAZARD (degrading): the exclusion logic interacts dangerously with the thin cell.** The chosen cell has only 3 brands, and the bet-evidence brands (the strongest proof of durable novelty-tech demand) are excluded from it by design. So the cell that the pipeline DECIDES ON (`_chosen_cell`) is simultaneously (a) thin and (b) stripped of its strongest demand evidence. The exclusion is correct in principle but means the chosen-cell saturation/ownability reads rest on playdate+pocket-operator+divoom — and `anti_fluke.qualifying_creatives:0`. A qualifying-creative count of zero in the decided cell is a red flag the artifact surfaces but does not resolve.
- **DATA-SUFFICIENCY: SURVIVED.** The provenance notes name exactly which brands are excluded and why (D-08 discipline cited), and the seed note is explicit. Traceable.

## PHASE 2 — PROSECUTING THE COMPOSITION

**Does the segment compose into justified grounds for the pipeline's conclusion (the chosen cell as the Market-Selection decision input)?**

The chain the segment must earn: *competitor claims → typed claims → sophistication stage + mechanism ownability + durability + bet type → a chosen niche × transformation cell that Market Selection can gate on.*

**The weakest join: the gap between what the prompt PROMISES the artifact contains and what the artifact ACTUALLY contains.** The prompt is an unusually rigorous, law-aligned specification — it correctly derives the standard for almost every decision (claim-typing, stage mechanics, mechanism clustering, exclusion discipline). On RULE-SOUNDNESS the segment is strong: 6 of 9 decisions survive the law cleanly, and the two most-charged moves (VOC deferral, page-readable bet type) are DR-defensible because they are honestly scoped as provisional.

But on DATA-SUFFICIENCY the delivered `space-map.json` does not contain the very fields the prompt declares load-bearing:
- **No `demand_trend`** — the field the pre-research plan calls "the load-bearing supply-side field for this product" whose absence "silently disables the most important validation." (FATAL)
- **No `sophistication_stage` + evidence line** on the chosen cell — the field the prompt calls "the load-bearing field for Gate 3 downstream." (Degrading→serious)
- **No `bet_type` / `bet_types[]`** — the input to the eventual bet × niche × durability crossing. (Serious)

The prompt argues eloquently for its own logic and would talk a careless reviewer into scoring the segment sound on the strength of that argument. That is the central trap, and it sprung here: **the reasoning is sound; the produced evidence does not instantiate the reasoning.** Internal coherence of the prompt is not a defense (anti-glaze rule). The artifact that actually flows downstream is missing the three signals the operator's own documents say are decisive.

**Second weakest join: the transformation label itself.** `novelty-object-own` is a non-transformation (Decision 2) standing in for an admittedly-OPEN slot. Downstream, Market Selection's Gate logic will read `run_transformation: "novelty-object-own"` as the transformation. The `_provenance` flag says "asserted, not validated" — but it does not say "this is not even a real transformation, it is a possession-desire placeholder." The over-claim guard ("must not over-claim past supply-side spend validation") protects against treating the *spend* as a *true-transformation proof*; it does NOT protect against the more basic error of treating a possession-desire as a transformation at all. This is where the missing-VOC tradeoff finally bites: VOC is what would have replaced `novelty-object-own` with a real driver (status / belonging / early-adopter signalling — the plan's own candidates). Deferring VOC is defensible; but the placeholder chosen to stand in its place is mislabeled against `definitions.md`, and that mislabel is carried as the decision's `run_transformation`.

**VERDICT: The segment EARNS its conclusion at the level of RULE-DESIGN but ASSERTS it at the level of DELIVERED DATA.** The logic is among the most law-faithful I could prosecute — the VOC-deferral tradeoff survives, the exclusion discipline survives, the stage mechanics survive. But the conclusion (`_chosen_cell` = novelty-object-own × edc-aesthetic-collectors as a sound Market-Selection input) does not survive intact, because: (1) the durability signal that the operator declares load-bearing for THIS product is absent from the artifact (fatal data gap); (2) the stage and bet_type fields the prompt declares load-bearing are absent from the chosen cell (serious gaps); (3) the transformation token is a possession-desire mislabeled as a transformation; and (4) the niche token is undertraced against the prompt's own "trace to raw variants" rule. A staff DR reviewer would not approve `space-map.json` as the "sole decision input" in its delivered state — not because the thinking is wrong, but because the artifact omits the thinking's load-bearing outputs.
