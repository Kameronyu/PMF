# Crowdfunded E-Ink + Adjacent Focus Devices (2022–2026)

Scout pass for PMF e-ink tablet project. Scope: e-ink + e-ink-adjacent focus
devices launched on Kickstarter / Indiegogo / Crowd Supply 2022–2026. Excludes
incumbent e-ink (reMarkable / Boox / Kindle Scribe / Daylight / Supernote)
which are already covered in the market scans. Daylight DC-1 is excluded
because it never ran a KS/IGG campaign — direct pre-order only.

HKD → USD converted at ~7.8 HKD = 1 USD (Kickstarter displays HKD for many
HK-incorporated campaigns; underlying pledges are typically USD). EUR → USD at
~1.08. Where the press cited the figure already in USD, used as-is.

Compiled 2026-05-23. Source tags inline: **[KS]** Kickstarter, **[IGG]**
Indiegogo, **[CS]** Crowd Supply, **[XREF]** secondary press, **[WS]** open
WebSearch synthesis.

## FOLDABLE E-INK — FLAGGED

Two crowdfunded foldable e-ink devices found in scope. Both meet the "screen
folds in the middle of the device" criterion (not just a folio cover).

### 1. Diptyx E-Reader — open-source DIY book-style foldable

- **Name / creator:** Diptyx E-Reader / Martijn den Hoed (Dutch indie hardware dev).
- **Platform:** Crowd Supply.
- **Year launched:** December 2025 (campaign live as of late 2025; deliveries
  scheduled to start May 2026).
- **Format:** Pure e-ink. **FOLDABLE — yes (book-style hinge, dual-panel).**
  Two 5.83-inch monochrome E Ink panels (648×480 ea, ~137 ppi) connected by a
  center hinge; folds shut like a paperback (4.7 × 5.9 × 0.6 in, 10.5 oz).
- **Raise (USD):** Pledge target $27,000. At reporting (early-campaign window
  per CNX Software / Liliputing coverage), had 32 backers in first few hours;
  final raise not yet captured — **flagged unverified, campaign mid-flight**.
  ESP32-S3 / 16MB flash / 8MB RAM / SD card.
- **Funding goal:** $27,000.
- **% Funded:** Unverified (in-flight).
- **Retail price:** $230 pledge tier. Free US shipping, +$12 ROW.
- **Ship status:** Campaigning; deliveries promised May 2026.
- **Positioning (verbatim/synthesis):** "An open-source, ESP32-powered,
  dual-screen e-book reader" (CNX Software); "fits in your purse like an old
  favorite novel" (creator framing). DRM-free, EPUB-only, fully open-source
  post-campaign.
- **Link:** https://www.crowdsupply.com/diptyx/diptyx-e-reader

### 2. Zerowriter Fold — clamshell e-ink word processor

- **Name / creator:** Zerowriter Fold / Zerowriter (creator of the earlier
  Zerowriter Ink, an open-source E Ink typewriter that shipped via Crowd
  Supply).
- **Platform:** Kickstarter.
- **Year launched:** Campaign live 2026 (per Kickstarter URL active in May
  2026). Pre-launch / waitlist marketing dates back to Feb 2025.
- **Format:** Pure e-ink. **FOLDABLE — yes (clamshell laptop-style hinge).**
  6-inch E Ink display + mechanical keyboard; clamshell closes flat.
- **Raise (USD):** $100,000+ raised from 450+ backers, 350%+ funded (per
  Hackster.io / Liliputing reporting; the press numbers may conflate Zerowriter
  Ink's earlier campaign with Fold's — **flagged: needs verification**). Funded
  in 42 minutes per multiple secondary sources.
- **Funding goal:** Unverified — implied <$30k given 350%+ funded.
- **% Funded:** 350%+ (reported, unverified).
- **Retail price:** First Wave $239–$269 (early bird, up to $90 off); retail
  ~$329–$359.
- **Ship status:** Campaigning / fulfilling — first orders ship late 2026
  (Zerowriter.ink product page); Q4 2026 estimate.
- **Positioning (verbatim):** "The E-Paper Word Processor for Writers" /
  "anti-AI laptop with one job: writing" (PCWorld). Distraction-free, 100-hour
  battery life, mechanical keyboard, user-controllable lighting, weeks of
  battery life.
- **Link:** https://www.kickstarter.com/projects/zerowriter/zerowriter-fold

### Non-crowdfunded foldable for reference (NOT counted in table)

- **Readmoo mooInk V** — first foldable color e-reader from Readmoo × E Ink.
  **NOT crowdfunded**: direct pre-order via Readmoo platform (Taiwan only as of
  Aug 2025 ship), NT$19,800 (~$615). 8" Gallery 3 ePaper bending over a single
  hinge (not dual-panel). Worth tracking but does not belong in a KS/IGG scan.
- **Lenovo/Hefei LCFC "Gemini" dual-screen e-ink concept** — iF Design Award
  2024 winner. No KS/IGG campaign per press; concept only. Skipped.

## NOTES

### Verification gaps
- **Zerowriter Fold raise** — multiple press cites "$100k+, 450+ backers, 350%+
  funded, funded in 42 minutes" but at least one source appears to conflate
  these numbers with the earlier **Zerowriter Ink** (Crowd Supply, 2024) which
  itself raised ~$100k+ with similar backer counts. The Fold KS page is
  Kickstarter-locked behind 403 to WebFetch; **the raise/backer numbers for
  Fold specifically should be re-verified from a fresh KS scrape before
  citing**. Either way: it is foldable, it is live, and it cleared its goal
  fast.
- **Diptyx raise** — campaign was mid-flight at last reporting (Dec 2025 /
  early 2026 press). Final raise not captured. Pledge target $27k; was on pace
  per CNX/Liliputing. **Re-verify before citing.**
- **DuRoBo Krono final raise** — one source (Kicktraq snapshot in WebSearch
  result) reports €17,546 of €20k goal (≈88%, technically under), while DuRoBo
  PR + Good e-Reader call it "fully backed." Likely closed at goal with late
  pledges or extended via in-demand. **Flagged: numbers conflict.**

### Definitional calls
- **Daylight DC-1** is the obvious "Live Paper / e-ink-adjacent focus tablet"
  but was NOT crowdfunded — direct pre-order only. Excluded per scope (also
  already covered in incumbent scans). Listed here only for the reader's
  awareness.
- **Mooink V** (Readmoo × E Ink): first true crowdfundable-shape foldable color
  e-reader, but launched as direct pre-order in Taiwan; no KS/IGG. Excluded.
- **Lenovo/Hefei LCFC Gemini**: dual-screen book-style e-ink — concept-only,
  iF Design 2024, no KS/IGG. Excluded.
- **Pomera DM250US** included even though the display is reflective LCD (not
  E Ink): the device positions in the same distraction-free / focus-device cell
  as Zerowriter and Freewrite, which the brief explicitly admits as
  e-ink-adjacent.
- **BLOOMIN8** included as adjacent ($1.53M raise is too informative to drop):
  it is a color-e-ink art frame, not a tablet, but it validates "non-screen
  / always-on / battery-decade e-ink" buying behavior — relevant signal that
  e-ink demand extends well beyond the reader-tablet category.
- **OKPad** is a hybrid IPS+E-ink convertible, not foldable in the
  fold-the-screen sense. The 360° hinge gives laptop/tent/tablet modes; included
  because the second display being e-ink is a focus-positioning choice.
- **Reinkstone R1** launched Jun 2021 — strictly outside the 2022+ scope, but
  it was still shipping and being reviewed deep into 2023, was widely
  cross-referenced by buyers comparing crowdfunded e-ink options in the scope
  window, and the press treated it as "in-market." Kept with the caveat.

### Vaporware / scam flags
- **None confirmed.** Minimal Phone was widely written off as vaporware before
  it started shipping in Jan 2025 (Android Police explicitly: "We were
  convinced the Minimal Phone was vaporware, but it's now started shipping to
  backers") — it cleared the gate. Bigme has a consistent shipping track
  record across 3+ KS campaigns. Mudita has shipped two prior KS campaigns
  (Pure, then Kompakt). iFlytek is a multibillion-dollar Chinese AI company —
  AINOTE 2 is in retail. Viwoods, Reinkstone, Bluegen, BLOOMIN8 all confirmed
  shipping per multiple press. No campaign in the list flagged as a scam.
- **Soft-flag: DuRoBo Krono** — first campaign by a brand-new company, sub-$20k
  raise, very small backer pool (68). Not vaporware (CES 2026 demo confirmed),
  but the smallest delivery risk on the list.

### Resemblance to incumbents
- **Bigme** (inkNote Color, Galy, B251) is the most consistent KS exploiter of
  Boox's playbook — color e-ink + Android + stylus, often beating Boox to market
  on a new E Ink panel (Gallery 3, Kaleido 2). Bigme is best understood as a
  "small-OEM Boox shadow" that uses KS as launch funnel + pre-order tool, not
  to validate demand.
- **Viwoods AiPaper** and **iFlytek AINOTE 2** are the AI-tablet wave — both
  ride the same Carta panel + RK3576 chip combo + ChatGPT/GPT integration.
  Direct overlap with reMarkable Paper Pro positioning (paperless productivity)
  but explicitly leaning on the AI angle that reMarkable has avoided.
- **MOAAN FLY True Color** is a Xiaomi-linked OEM Boox-Palma clone (pocket
  color Android e-ink).
- **Minimal Phone / Mudita Kompakt** sit in Cell E of the students profile
  (kid-safe / restricted device, parent-buyer) — but bought student-self / adult
  here. Light Phone is their incumbent comparator. None of these are in the
  $900 tablet conversation directly, but they validate "minimalist focus device"
  buyer behavior at $300–$500 phone price points.
