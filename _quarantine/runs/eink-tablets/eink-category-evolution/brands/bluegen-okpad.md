## Product
- **Model:** Bluegen OKPad — single SKU dual-screen convertible Android tablet.
- **Format:** 8.9" IPS LCD (1280×800) on top + 7.8" monochrome E Ink Carta (1872×1404) on bottom, joined by a 360° hinge (laptop / tablet / tent / stand modes). E Ink supports Wacom stylus (4,096 pressure levels). Both screens are touch.
- **Internals (cost-cut):** Qualcomm Snapdragon 600-series, 4GB RAM, 64GB storage, Android 10, 7,000 mAh battery, 8MP rear + 2MP front camera, USB-C, 3.5mm jack, Wi-Fi 5, Bluetooth 4.2.
- **Price points:**
  - Super Early Bird KS: $199
  - Standard KS tiers: $249–$299
  - Stated retail MSRP: $399
  - Average pledge HK$2,137 (~$274 USD)
- **Pricing model:** one-time hardware purchase. No subscription / no software upsell surfaced.

## Mechanism / UM
- **Mechanism — how/why it works:** two displays of different technologies (LCD for color/video/UI, E Ink for read/write) joined by a 360° hinge that lets the buyer pick the mode (tent / tablet / laptop / e-reader / stand) per task.
- **Problem-mechanism (causal story):** **absent.** Bluegen does not name *why* a single-display tablet falls short — no eye-strain story, no battery story, no distraction story, no glare story. The buyer must self-supply the rationale for wanting two display types. This is a **major positioning gap** — most other crowdfunded e-ink wins lean on a causal story (Mudita: "your phone owns your life"; Zerowriter Fold: "the modern laptop is anti-writer"; Daylight: "screens hurt").
- **UM:** **Feature-UM** — "hybrid LCD+E-ink in one device with 360° convertible hinge." The combination is novel in market (no other shipping device fuses LCD + E Ink behind a single hinge at this price). But Bluegen does NOT position it as unique — copy treats the dual-display as a self-evident feature. **The mechanism IS unique; the marketing does not claim uniqueness.** Honest call: Feature-UM by structure, "none" by Bluegen's own marketing emphasis.

## Evolution Anchor

- **Launch year:** 2024 (KS live July 4 – August 3, 2024; first units shipping target August 2024).
- **Main differentiation claim AT LAUNCH (verbatim):** "Double the Screens, Double the Potential."
- **What pre-existing claims competed with:**
  - Single-display LCD Android tablets at ~$200 price tier (Amazon Fire, Lenovo Tab, generic budget tablets) — Bluegen's claim is "two screen technologies vs. their one."
  - Single-display E Ink tablets at $300–$700 (Boox Note Air, reMarkable, Kindle Scribe) — Bluegen's claim is "you get color + E Ink in one device, half the price."
  - Existing dual-screen LCD+LCD convertibles (Lenovo Yoga Book 9i, Asus Zenbook Duo) at $1,500+ — Bluegen's claim is "1/5 the price, and the second screen is E Ink not redundant LCD."
  - No direct competing dual-screen LCD+E-ink shipping device at sub-$500 in 2024.
- **The form-factor question — hinge as lead, dual-display as lead, or both?**
  - **Dual-display (LCD+E-ink) dominates the headline.** "Double the Screens, Double the Potential" reads as display-stacking, not hinge-flexibility. Every secondary-press headline leads with "LCD and E Ink" or "dual-screen" — none lead with "convertible" or "360° hinge." The hinge is the *enabling* mechanism (without it the second screen is just a stuck-on appendage), but the displays are the *marketed* innovation.
  - The hinge gets framed as the unlock for use-mode variety (laptop / tent / tablet / e-reader) — i.e. the hinge sells the *utility* of the dual displays. So: **display hybrid leads; hinge is the supporting mechanism that makes the display hybrid useful**. Estimated weight in copy: 70% dual-display, 30% hinge/form-mode.
  - This is the inverse of Kam's foldable: a $900 single-foldable-screen device sells the FOLD as the headline (one device that becomes two sizes), with the display tech as the supporting feature. OKPad sells the displays as the headline, with the hinge as the supporting feature.
- **New claim / mechanism / feature introduced to the category — LCD+E-ink hybrid as positioning move:**
  - **The category-evolution move IS the LCD+E-ink hybrid display itself, packaged as "two devices in one at one device's price."** This is a meaningful positioning addition to the E Ink crowdfunding category — every other 2022–2025 crowdfunded e-ink device in scope (Bigme Galy, Bigme inkNote Color, iFlytek AINOTE 2, Viwoods AiPaper, MOAAN FLY, DuRoBo Krono, Mudita Kompakt, Diptyx, Zerowriter Fold) ships a **pure E Ink display** and positions on either color e-ink or AI or minimalism or focus. Bluegen is the only crowdfunded entry that says "actually, don't replace your LCD — *combine* them." That's a contrarian positioning move in a category whose narrative trajectory has been "pure E Ink for focus / distraction-free / less-screen."
  - **What Bluegen DIDN'T do that would have made it bigger:** add a focus / distraction / problem-mechanism story (e.g. "switch to the E Ink side when you need to read or think, switch to LCD when you need to watch / browse — the device decides which mode supports you"). The hybrid display is ideally suited to a "focus when you need to, color when you want to" narrative, and Bluegen left that money on the table. Result: a structurally-novel device positioned as a generic value play, which is why the raise was $119k (one-tenth of iFlytek AINOTE 2's $1.13M, one-fourth of Viwoods AiPaper's $275k).
- **Current evolution status; post-shipping reception:**
  - **Shipped (2024 H2)** with no widely-reported delay scandal but no post-ship marketing motor either. No retail / DTC web presence surfaced as of May 2026. No 2025–2026 owner-review corpus, no follow-up product, no Boox-Galy-style sequel announcement.
  - **Category influence:** **minimal.** No other crowdfunded brand has followed Bluegen into the hybrid LCD+E-ink shape (Diptyx, Zerowriter Fold both stayed pure E Ink). The hybrid-display category is empty behind Bluegen — either because the structural innovation is harder than it looks, because the positioning gap (no focus narrative) capped demand, or both.
  - **Net read for Kam's $900 foldable:** Bluegen demonstrates that "hybrid display as a feature" alone is not enough to drive scale — the form-factor / display-tech innovation has to be coupled to a transformation+niche the buyer can name. Kam's foldable, if it leans on focus / programmability / single-task discipline + a specific niche (analog-wellness organizer or ADHD-student tool per MEMORY), should outperform a "doubled capability" pitch at the same price. Form-factor novelty without a niche × transformation pitch caps at ~$120k KS in this category.
