## Product
- mooInk V foldable color e-reader.
- 8" foldable E Ink Gallery 3 panel. 300 PPI mono / 150 PPI color (~50,000 shades).
- Aluminum-magnesium alloy chassis. Hinge wrapped in soft-touch material.
  Two stable fold positions: 180° flat / 90° book-open. Magnets hold position.
- ~255 g. Octa-core, 4 GB RAM, 64 GB storage (microSD up to 1 TB). Android-based.
- 2,300 mAh battery. Wi-Fi, USB-C, Bluetooth 5.1. **No front light.**
- ePub / PDF, USB-C or cloud sync.
- Price: **NT$19,800 (~$615 USD).** Single SKU.
- Pricing model: one-time hardware purchase + Readmoo bookstore ecosystem.
- Pre-order: live Apr 2025 on Readmoo; ships Aug 2025; Taiwan-only.

## Transformation(s)
- **Dominant frame:** Reading on a device that "opens and closes like a real
  book" — restoring the physical-book experience to digital reading. Not
  productivity, not note-taking. Pure reading.
- **Secondary:** Pocket-portable 8" screen — get a larger reading surface
  without losing portability.
- **Evidence:** Readmoo CEO Pang Wenzhen: *"Continuously looking for lighter
  and more colorful reading solutions for readers has always been the team's
  goal. We are very happy to cooperate with E Ink to launch mooInk V."* Yanko
  Design headline: *"World's First Foldable E-Reader Tablet Opens And Closes
  Like A Real Book."* Gizmodo framing: *"turns an e-reader into a high-end
  paperback."* The press calls it a *"hardcover fantasy"* (513.toys).

## Niche
- **Niche:** Mandarin-reading book buyers in Taiwan (Readmoo's installed
  customer base). Within that — self-identified bookworms / heavy readers
  who already own multiple e-readers and value the *form* of a book.
- **Targeted sub-niche:** Premium/collector e-reader buyer who finds the
  "slab of plastic" form of conventional e-readers psychologically wrong —
  wants the device to *feel like a book*. Yanko explicitly addresses
  "bookworm demographic skeptical of reading on essentially a slab of plastic."
- **Evidence:** Pricing at NT$19,800 (~$615) is roughly 4× a basic Kindle in
  Taiwan; positioning + materials (alloy chassis, soft-touch hinge) confirm
  premium/collector pitch, not mass-market.

## Buyer
- **Primary buyer:** Self-buy adult, heavy Chinese-language reader, mid-to-upper
  income. Likely existing Readmoo bookstore customer (platform lock-in).
- **Purchase context:** Self-buy / status purchase / replacement upgrade to
  an existing mooInk device. Gift potential (high price + book-like aesthetic).
- **Where bought:** Readmoo DTC pre-order, Taiwan only. No retail partners,
  no Amazon, no global rollout announced.
- **Evidence:** Pre-order page is Readmoo.com (their bookstore site). Taiwan-
  only ship. No global English marketing. Price point + ecosystem lock-in

## Mechanism / UM
- **Mechanism (how/why it works):** A genuinely flexible Gallery 3 e-ink panel
  laminated across a single mechanical hinge; the panel bends rather than
  splits, so the display is continuous (no center bezel / no dual-panel seam).
  Magnets stabilize at 180° and 90°. Aluminum-magnesium alloy chassis carries
  the structural load.
- **Problem-mechanism (causal story, shared):** Conventional e-readers are
  "slabs of plastic" that fail the bookworm's emotional/tactile expectation
  of what reading is. Standard color e-ink tablets (rigid) sacrifice
  portability for screen size or vice versa. This problem-mechanism is shared
  by many "book-experience" brands (Hosanna Revival heirloom Bibles, Daylight
  DC-1 paper-like, Diptyx book-fold) — NOT unique to Readmoo.
- **UM:** **Product UM** — *the foldable flexible color e-ink reader that
  opens like a book.* Genuinely unique at the form-factor level: first commercial
  flexible-screen e-reader. Combines two mechanisms (flexible-display fold +
  Gallery 3 color) into a category that did not exist before. The buyer is
  asked to move the product into a new category ("foldable e-reader") that
  has no prior referent.

## Evolution Anchor

- **Launch year:** 2025 (announced Touch Taiwan, Apr 16, 2025; pre-order
  same month; ship Aug 2025).
- **Main differentiation claim AT LAUNCH (verbatim):** *"World's first
  foldable color e-reader"* / Readmoo CEO: *"lighter and more colorful
  reading solutions for readers."* / Yanko / Gizmodo: *"opens and closes
  like a real book."*
- **What pre-existing claims competed with:** As the first foldable color
  e-reader, **no direct prior claims existed in this exact category.** It
  competes structurally with rigid color e-ink — **Bigme inkNote Color**
  (2022 KS), **Bigme Galy** (2022 KS "World's First Color E Ink Gallery™ 3
  Tablet"), and **Reinkstone R1** (2021 "Ultimate True Color DES E-Paper
  Tablet"). Those all hold the *color* claim on rigid form factors. mooInk V
  doesn't try to beat them on color — it adds **fold** as the new axis,
  reframing the category from "what color tech" to "what shape." It also
  competes against **Diptyx** (book-fold dual-panel mono, open-source) and
  the broader minimalist e-reader cell — but Diptyx is dual-panel + mono +
  hobbyist, and Bigme Galy is rigid + color, so mooInk V occupies an empty
  cell between them.
- **New claim / mechanism / feature introduced:** **Flexible-screen fold +
  Gallery 3 color**, combined in one device. Specifically: a single Gallery 3
  color e-ink panel that *bends* across a mechanical hinge (rather than two
  panels with a bezel between them, as Diptyx and the Lenovo Gemini concept
  use). This is the *display* doing the folding, not just the chassis. The
  Product UM is "foldable flexible color e-reader."
- **Where it slots in the foldable e-ink evolution thesis:**
  - **Bluegen OKPad (2024 KS, $119k raise)** — convertible hybrid (360°
    hinge, IPS top + E Ink bottom). Solves "multi-mode" via convertibility
    of a *rigid* device; the screens themselves do not fold. Form-factor
    bet: hinge as posture/mode switcher.
  - **Diptyx (2025 CS)** — book-fold dual-panel mono. Two separate rigid
    panels joined at a hinge, like a paperback. Form-factor bet: imitate
    book-spine geometry with off-the-shelf rigid panels. Cheap, open-source,
    hobbyist.
  - **mooInk V (2025)** — flexible-display fold, single panel that bends.
    No center bezel. Color. Industrial-grade. Form-factor bet: the *display
    itself* should be the thing that folds, and color is now table stakes.
  - **Trajectory:** Bluegen and Diptyx are *workarounds* — they fold the
    chassis or fold around a seam. mooInk V is the first to fold the
    *display*. This is the form-factor "graduation" — the moment foldable
    e-ink stops being a chassis trick and starts being a real flexible-screen
    product category. The thesis position: **foldable flexible color is now
    a real cell**; competitors will follow because the panel exists.
- **What E Ink Corp's direct partnership signals about the form factor thesis:**
  - **E Ink validates the form factor.** The sole manufacturer of every e-ink
    panel in the world picked Readmoo to launch the flexible color panel
    commercially. This means (a) E Ink has flexible Gallery 3 in production
    inventory and is willing to commit it to a shipping product, not just
    a Touch Taiwan demo; (b) E Ink is doing platform-development by funding
    a real customer use case rather than spec-sheet announcements; (c) the
    durability story (200,000 folds, 9-year R&D, alloy chassis) is co-signed
    by E Ink, which is far more credible than a Bigme / Bluegen / Diptyx
    durability claim would be. **The form factor has crossed the validation
    threshold.** Foldable flexible color e-ink is now a real product category,
    not a concept. Every other e-ink OEM (Boox, Bigme, Onyx, MOAAN, iFlytek,
    Viwoods) will get access to the same panel within 12–24 months. The
    competitive window for "first" claims is closing; the next claim will be
    *"best foldable for [use case]"* — which is where our $900 thesis enters.
