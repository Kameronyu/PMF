## Product
RIDI Paper is a line of dedicated e-ink e-readers built in-house by RIDI Corporation, locked to the Ridibooks store. Line history:
- RIDI Paper (2015) — 6"
- RIDI Paper Pro (Dec 2017) — 7.8", 300ppi (1404×1872), 250g, 1GB RAM / 8GB storage + microSD, Wi-Fi, hardware page-turn buttons, Android 4.4. Launch price 249,000 KRW (~$190 USD). South Korea's first 7.8" e-reader.
- RIDI Paper 3 (2018) — 6"
- RIDI Paper 4 (released June 2022, pre-order Apr 2022) — 7", 300ppi (1264×1680), Carta 1200 E Ink, Exynos 850 octa-core, 3GB RAM / 32GB storage, IPX8 waterproof (2m / 60min — first in Korea), USB-C, Bluetooth 5.0, Android 10, black/white. Launch price 329,000 KRW (~$250 USD).
- RIDI Paper 4 reported discontinued ~2024; no successor "Paper Pro" 2024+ model confirmed. NOTE: the brief's "Paper Pro / Paper 4" appears to conflate two distinct devices five years apart — Paper Pro is the 2017 model, Paper 4 the 2022 model. Imported units on Amazon/eBay run ~$200–280.

Reading-only device. No stylus / no handwriting / no note-taking — this is an e-reader, not a writing tablet.

## Transformation(s)
Better, more comfortable reading. Verbatim (translated from RIDI Paper 4 press release): "최적의 독서 경험" (an optimal reading experience); reading "보다 다양한 환경에서 여유롭게" (in more diverse environments, at leisure — i.e. bath, pool, travel via IPX8). Your read: the transformation is convenient immersive reading of Korean e-books/webnovels, not focus/digital-minimalism. Eye strain and blue light are not foregrounded as the pitch; the implicit benefit is platform convenience (your whole Ridibooks library on a purpose-built device).

## Niche
Existing Ridibooks customers — Korean readers of e-books, web novels, and (to a lesser extent) webtoons who already buy on the platform. Narrow and captive: the device only reads content from the Ridibooks store, cannot install other bookstores' apps. Effectively a retention/lock-in device for the installed base, not a tool sold to a new identity community. Imagery and copy are Korean-language only; no Western-market targeting.

## Mechanism / UM
- mechanism(s) referenced: E Ink display (paper-like reading), IPX8 waterproofing, hardware page-turn buttons, Carta 1200 panel (faster refresh / better contrast vs predecessor)
- UM type played: Feature — IPX8 waterproofing is the headline differentiator ("first e-reader in Korea with IPX8"). This upgrades an already-recognized category (e-reader) via a specific feature. Carta 1200 is a secondary feature claim. No Problem UM (no "backlit screens wreck your sleep" framing) and no real Product UM beyond generic e-ink; differentiation is feature-level + platform lock-in.

- awareness level(s) targeted: most-aware / product-aware. Marketing is aimed at the existing Ridibooks customer base who already know e-readers and the platform; it's a feature/upgrade announcement, not category education.

## Sophistication
- market sophistication stage this brand plays at: Stage 4 — feature-level competition. The e-reader category is mature in Korea (Kindle limited there, Kobo/crema/Yes24 compete); RIDI competes by upgrading a known category with specific features (IPX8 waterproof, Carta 1200, hardware buttons, 7–7.8" size) and by platform lock-in to its own store. Not introducing a new transformation (not Stage 1), not establishing a Product UM (not Stage 2/3).
- revenue_est: hardware revenue unknown — not separately reported. Company-level: RIDI Corporation reported record revenue of 235.4B KRW (~$161M USD) in 2024, +7.2% YoY, with an operating loss of 12.9B KRW (~$8.8M). That figure is overwhelmingly digital content (e-books, webtoons, web novels), NOT device sales. Devices are a small retention/lock-in line item. Confidence: Reported (company-level only); device-level revenue = unknown, do not infer.
- social_proof_volume: Ridibooks app ~30,600+ reviews (Android, AppGrooves) — but that is the content app, not the device. Device-specific review volume is low: Amazon/eBay import listings carry only a handful of ratings each (niche Korean-language import). Korean community discussion exists on namu.wiki, blogs (Brunch, Nyan101), and forums but no large aggregated rating count.
- distribution_channels: ridibooks.com, Naver Smart Store, 29CM, 11st, Coupang (Korea); secondary/import via Amazon, eBay, Bunjang (used). Korea-only official distribution.

## Reviews / VOC quick-read
- review volume + rough sentiment: low device-specific volume in English/Western channels; moderate Korean-language coverage. Sentiment in Korean reviews/forums: generally positive on the reading experience, screen, IPX8, and one-handed ergonomics; the device is regarded as a competent "strong competitor" in the Korean e-reader market.
- brand loyalty signal: loyalty is to the Ridibooks content platform, not the hardware. Device exists to keep heavy platform readers inside the ecosystem; switching cost is the user's purchased library.
- hidden use cases / notable likes & dislikes:
  - Likes: IPX8 waterproof (bath/pool/travel reading), Carta 1200 screen quality, USB-C, hardware page-turn buttons, lightweight 7" form factor, minimalist design.
  - Dislikes / complaints: hard store lock-in (cannot install other bookstores or Kindle/Google — major limitation cited in reviews); a known hardware fault where fast chargers can damage the PCB (workaround: charge at ≤10W); Korean-language-only OS makes it near-unusable for non-Korean readers; line reportedly discontinued ~2024 with no successor, raising long-term support concern.
