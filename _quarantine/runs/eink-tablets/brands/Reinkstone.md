## Product
- **Reinkstone R1** — 10.1" color DES e-paper Android tablet / e-note. 2232x1680, 280 PPI grayscale / 140 PPI color; 4096-level EMR stylus with eraser; Android 11; 1.8GHz quad-core, 4GB RAM, 64GB ROM; 4500mAh battery (claimed 100 days standby / 300 hours use); 0.68cm thick.
- Crowdfunding price ladder: Super Early Bird $329 → Early Bird $379 → Kickstarter regular $429 → stated MSRP $549.
- **Status as of 2026:** The R1 tablet is delisted from reinkstone.com. The site now sells only color e-ink **phone cases** (Reink Case C1/C2/C3/X1/Knight, $49.99–$79.90) and a **Reink Tag** MFi e-ink locator ($34.90). The brand has effectively pivoted out of the tablet category covered by this study.

## Transformation(s)
- Verbatim framing (launch PR): solves eye fatigue from light-emitting tablets, sunlight readability, and unifies "reading, writing, working, and entertainment"; "simply lets you do more than other E-readers" and "streamlines your day."
- My read: classic e-ink transformation stack — reduced eye strain / screen fatigue + paper-feel note-taking — with an added "do-it-all in color" angle. Color was the headline transformation differentiator: a paper-feel device you no longer have to accept in grayscale.

## Niche
- Targeted readers, note-takers, and "do more" knowledge workers fatigued by backlit tablets. Broad, not identity-tight — no specific community (students/writers/minimalists) called out.
- Imagery and feature mix (Android 11 + color + stylus) signal a general "productivity + reading + light entertainment" buyer, i.e. the Onyx Boox-style prosumer e-note shopper. Niche read: broad prosumer e-note buyer; weak community identity.

## Mechanism / UM
- mechanism(s) referenced: DES (Dot Eutectic Structure) color e-paper "electronic slurry" module; EMR stylus layer; Android 11 openness.
- UM type played: **Feature UM** (primary) — upgrades the recognized color-e-paper category via a specific proprietary panel ("DES slurry, not micro-cup / micro-capsule, not E Ink"). Secondary **Product UM** lean — color e-paper as a category-defining solution ("true color, no eye strain"). The UM text: DES slurry delivers higher color saturation/definition than existing micro-capsule e-paper.

## Sophistication
- market sophistication stage this brand plays at: **Stage 4 (feature-level competition)**, with a Stage 3 undertone. By 2021 a Product UM (color e-paper) already existed (E Ink Kaleido on Onyx/PocketBook); Reinkstone could not introduce color e-paper as new, so it competed on a feature-grade differentiator — a proprietary DES panel claimed superior to incumbent micro-capsule tech. The "world's first book-sized true-color Android tablet" claim is feature/format one-upmanship, characteristic of Stage 4.
- revenue_est: **~$1.0M reported (one-time, 2021 crowdfunding)** — Reported: Kickstarter $1,019,677 / 2,424 backers (campaign goal $100k, 1020% funded); additional undisclosed Indiegogo InDemand raise after KS. Ongoing tablet revenue: effectively $0 — product delisted, widely reported as undelivered. Current phone-case business revenue: unknown (no proxy data; on-site review counts too thin to model).
- social_proof_volume: Trustpilot 66 reviews (3/5); on-site phone-case reviews ~3 (4.7); no meaningful follower/UGC volume surfaced; some YouTube hands-on coverage from e-reader channels circa 2021–2022.
- distribution_channels: originally Kickstarter + Indiegogo + own Shopify store (reinkstone.com). Today: own store sells phone cases/Tag only. No Amazon presence found for the R1. Tablet effectively no longer distributed.

## Notes / gaps
- **Major flag: the R1 tablet is a dead / failed product.** It is delisted from reinkstone.com, never reliably delivered to backers, and the brand has pivoted to color e-ink phone cases. For an e-ink tablet market map, Reinkstone is a cautionary data point, not an active competitor.
- Treat all R1 marketing copy as 2021 crowdfunding-era positioning, not a live funnel. There is no current tablet landing page, quiz funnel, or advertorial to analyze.
- Not on Meta — zero ad-library footprint for the tablet or the current phone-case business.
- DES panel classification: vendor explicitly distinguishes DES "slurry" from E Ink Corp micro-capsule and from micro-cup tech. It is still electrophoretic color e-paper (true e-ink class, not RLCD/NXTPAPER), so it belongs in the true-E-Ink bucket, but it is a non-E-Ink-brand panel — note this if the study segments by panel vendor.
- Biggest data gap: no current/active marketing funnel exists for any Reinkstone product, and there is no usable revenue signal for the post-pivot phone-case business — only the one-time 2021 ~$1.0M crowdfunding figure is solid.
- Indiegogo InDemand raise total after Kickstarter is undisclosed — would add to the ~$1.0M reported figure if found.
