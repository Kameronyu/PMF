## Product
- **Supernote Manta** — 10.7" flexible E Ink, 300 PPI, 6.0mm thick (3.6mm at edges), ~467g with Half Folio. Ceramic NeverReplace Nib. Price ~$459–$505 USD.
- **Supernote Nomad (A6 X2)** — 7.8" E Ink, 1404×1872 (300 PPI), 266g, 6.8mm. RK3566 quad-core, 4GB RAM, 32GB + microSD (to 2TB), 2700mAh replaceable battery, Chauvet OS (Android 11-based). $329 USD. White / Crystal editions.
- Accessories: Push-Up Standard Pen, Supernote × LAMY special pen set, folios.
- Format: dedicated handwriting-first e-notebooks (note-taking + PDF/ebook annotation + reading). Not multimedia tablets.

## Transformation(s)
- Verbatim: "(re)gain stolen creative focus and think deeply for breakthrough results"; "Dive into Mind Flow"; "Regain stolen creative focus."
- Read: distraction-free deep focus + paperless note-taking. Secondary transformation = digital minimalism / sustainability ("go paperless while reducing e-waste", "the most sustainable travel size e-notebook"). The promise is cognitive (flow, focus, depth), not just utility.

## Niche
- Primary identity: "For Those Who Write" — writers and people who think on paper. Tagline literally names the niche.
- Copy/imagery target a broad professional spread: writers, engineers, students/teachers, knowledge workers, healthcare, sales, management; explicitly calls out left-handed users and fountain-pen lovers.
- Nomad adds an outdoor/traveler sub-niche ("the outdoor writer's dream device" — hikers, wildlife sketchers).
- Cross-cutting identity layer: digital minimalists and sustainability-minded buyers (modular self-repair, e-waste reduction, "Buy Manta, Save Manta" conservation donation).
- Net: moderately broad niche unified by a "writer / deliberate thinker" self-image rather than a single occupation.

## Mechanism / UM
- mechanism(s) referenced: FeelWrite 2 self-recovery soft film (writing surface); Ceramic NeverReplace Nib + Wacom EMR stylus, 4096 pressure levels; modular self-repairable design (user-replaceable battery, SD, motherboard); offline on-device handwriting recognition; no frontlight/backlight by design; Chauvet OS.
- UM type played: **Feature UM** (primary) — upgrades the recognized e-notebook category on specific named features: FeelWrite 2 film, never-wearing ceramic nib, user-modular hardware. Secondary **Product UM** — the device as a deliberate distraction-free / no-subscription / repairable system ("not a consumable, a notebook that stays with you for years"). Not a Problem UM; copy assumes the buyer already accepts e-ink and is choosing between e-ink notebooks.

## Reviews / VOC quick-read
- review volume + rough sentiment: 3,423 own-site reviews at 72% 5-star — positive but not euphoric (notably lower 5-star share than some DTC peers). Strong third-party review coverage (eWritable, eReadersForum, Wirecutter, YouTube teardown channels).
- brand loyalty signal: high — dedicated r/Supernote community with official-team co-creation, owners citing it as "the one" replacing all paper journals; durability/anti-subscription/repairability messaging fosters long-hold loyalty. Fountain-pen-enthusiast crossover is a distinct loyal segment.
- hidden use cases / notable likes & dislikes:
  - Likes: lowest writing latency in class (~17ms vs reMarkable ~26ms, Boox Go ~40ms per eReadersForum); never-wearing ceramic nib; modular self-repair; no subscription; clean distraction-free OS; PDF/ebook annotation.
  - Dislikes / trade-offs: FeelWrite 2 surface feels smoother/glossier than reMarkable's paper texture (divisive — some prefer reMarkable feel); slower processor and fewer apps vs Boox; no color, no frontlight (deliberate but a real objection); premium price; software pace can lag.
  - Hidden use cases: outdoor field journaling / hiking logs (Nomad), fountain-pen-user migration, eReader/PDF annotation, sustainability-driven purchase.
