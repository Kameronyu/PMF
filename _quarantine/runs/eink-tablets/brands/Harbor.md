## Mechanism / UM
- mechanism(s) referenced: RLCD (Reflective LCD) — no backlight, no frontlight, uses ambient light; "Zero Blue Light"; 60Hz refresh; full-color LCD subpixels.
- UM type played: **Product UM** — the brand differentiates on the solution itself, a named display category (RLCD) positioned as a third option distinct from E Ink and LCD. It is also partly a **Problem UM** in the eye-care blog content (blue light / backlight flicker as the cause of strain and migraines). Primary play is Product UM: "Works with ambient light, not against it" = the unique delivery mechanism. Not a Feature UM — RLCD is framed as a whole new display class, not a spec upgrade to an existing category.

## Sophistication
- market sophistication stage this brand plays at: **Stage 3–4**. A Product UM for eye-care displays (E Ink) already saturates this market, so Harbor cannot introduce the transformation (rules out Stage 1–2). It positions RLCD as an *alternative* to the dominant E Ink mechanism — classic Stage 3 ("a Product UM already exists; needs an alternative"). The ad copy also competes feature-by-feature (60Hz, color, weight, "no ghosting") which edges into Stage 4. Net: Stage 3 primary, with Stage 4 ad tactics.
