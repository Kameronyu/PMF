## Product
N10 e-note line — true E Ink note-taking tablets with EMR active stylus:
- N10 (orig. 2022, 2nd-gen 2024 "Flagship") — 10.3", 1872×1404 (300 PPI), RK quad-core, 2GB/32GB (2024 gen bumps RAM), Android 11, 6,000 mAh, 374g / 5.5mm. ~1,999 yuan (~$300). 2024 Flagship bundles a lapel mic and scanner tool.
- N10 Touch — 10.3" capacitive-touch variant of the e-note.
- N10 Pro (2024) — 10.3" Carta 1200 (300 PPI), screen stack reduced 31% for clarity, octa-core 6 TOPS NPU, 4GB/64GB, Android 14, 6,500 mAh, 8192-pressure pen, 4-mic array. ~2,999 yuan / ~$419.
- N10 Mini — 7.8" Carta HD (300 PPI), RK3566, 2GB-4GB / 32GB-64GB, 240g / 5.3mm, 3,500 mAh. $399.99 (Good e-Reader Store); ~1,899 yuan on JD.
- N10 Max — 13.3" E Ink (16-grayscale), RK3566, 8GB/128GB + microSD, Android 11, 480g / 6.6mm, 4,000 mAh. $869.99 (Good e-Reader Store).
- C10 — adjacent 9.7" e-note, ~1,999 yuan pre-sale on JD.
Format: hardware-only, one-time purchase; bundle includes case + pen (some SKUs add mic/scanner). No subscription.

## Transformation(s)
- Paperless note-taking with a paper-feel writing experience. N10 Max copy (verbatim): "natural and realistic writing experience" via "flexible screen micro-suspension design," compared to "writing on a thick stack of paper."
- Capture-and-convert productivity: handwriting/PDF/image → editable digital text (OCR), plus record-and-transcribe meetings. The N10 Pro positions transcription + handwriting + recording running simultaneously.
- Reading without screen fatigue (e-reader / audiobook use is secondary).
- My read: the marketed outcome is "do all your knowledge-work capture on one distraction-free E Ink device" — paperless productivity, not primarily focus/minimalism.

## Niche
- "Business professionals or students" — stated target per reviews of the N10 Mini and N10 Pro. Knowledge workers who take notes, sign contracts, annotate PDFs, transcribe meetings.
- Secondary use cases surfaced in reviews: contract signing, academic grading, PDF annotation, ebook/audiobook reading.
- Broad, generic professional/student niche — no identity-led community positioning (no "for writers," "for ADHD," "for digital minimalists"). Niche is broad and weakly defined; Hanvon competes on hardware, not on tribe.

## Mechanism / UM
- mechanism(s) referenced: in-house Hanvon EMR active stylus (4096/8192 pressure levels, in-house developed); flexible-screen micro-suspension design for paper-feel writing; reduced screen stack (-31%) bringing E Ink layer closer to surface; in-house OCR engine; octa-core 6 TOPS NPU for on-device AI transcription/recognition.
- UM type played: Feature UM — Hanvon does not argue a new problem or a novel category. It upgrades the already-recognized E Ink e-note category with named feature specs (micro-suspension stack, -31% layer reduction, 8192-pressure in-house pen, 6 TOPS NPU). It is feature-vs-feature competition against Onyx BOOX et al., not mechanism-storytelling. No Problem UM and no real Product UM in the copy.
