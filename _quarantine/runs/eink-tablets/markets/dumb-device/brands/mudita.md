## Product
Mudita is a Polish minimalist-tech brand (founded by Michał Kiciński, CD Projekt co-founder). Two phones in market, both with E Ink displays:

- **Mudita Pure** — 2.84" E Ink (600×480, 270 PPI, 16-grayscale) feature-phone form factor, 140 g, custom MuditaOS (non-Android), Bluetooth 4.2 off-by-default, no mobile browser, no app store, built-in meditation timer. Retail price **$369** (outlet "very good" condition ~$310). Original Kickstarter early-bird $369 / backer $258.
- **Mudita Kompakt** — 4.3" E Ink (800×480) Android-fork "MuditaOS K" (no Google services), 3 GB RAM, 32 GB + microSD, 8 MP camera, 3,300 mAh (up to 6-day standby), IP54, USB-C + wireless charging, 3.5 mm jack, fingerprint reader. Hardware kill-switch cuts GSM modem + mic at hardware level; camera/Wi-Fi/BT killed at software level. Retail **$439** ($349 on dumbwireless 15%-off sale; sold out at retailer as of capture). Colors: Charcoal Black, Pebble Gray, Natural White. Carriers (US): T-Mobile, AT&T, MVNOs (Noble Mobile, Mint); Canada: Rogers, Telus.
- Also sells: Harmony 2 + Bell 2 e-ink alarm clocks, Element/Radiant Swiss-made watches (adjacent SKUs, not in scope here).
- Pricing model: one-time hardware purchase. No subscription. Sold DTC via store.mudita.com, Amazon (Kompakt B0FFN6N5KP), and minimalist-phone resellers (dumbwireless, minimalgoods).

## Transformation(s)
- dominant frame: **More control / more presence** — buyer moves from a phone-overwhelmed, distracted, notification-driven state into a calmer, more intentional, "present" life. Framed as more life, not more productivity.

(2) **Lower EMF exposure / safer device** — Pure-specific; reduce radiofrequency exposure for users who view ambient EMF as a health risk. (3) **Privacy** — Kompakt-specific; hardware-level disable of mic / modem / camera.

## Mechanism / UM
- mechanism(s) — how/why it works:
  - **E Ink display** — reduces blue light, restricts UI to text/grayscale, makes "scrolling" inherently unsatisfying → lowers habitual phone use.
  - **No app store / no browser (Pure) or curated sideload (Kompakt)** — removes the supply of attention-hijacking software at the device level. You can't doomscroll what isn't installed.
  - **Hardware kill-switch (Kompakt)** — physically severs RF/mic to enforce privacy moments.
  - **Patented antenna + custom shielding (Pure)** — engineering route to ultra-low SAR.
- problem-mechanism (causal story, may be shared): **The smartphone itself — its notifications, apps, and the supply of digital noise — is the cause of distraction, lost presence, and overwhelm.** "Society's reliance on smartphones has reached a tipping point — affecting focus, well-being, and personal connections." This is the SHARED digital-minimalism causal story (Light Phone, Punkt, Brick, Daylight all say a version of it). Not unique to Mudita.
- UM: **Product-UM (Pure only, EMF/SAR sub-thesis)** — "ultra-low SAR via patented antenna + internal shielding, lowest head SAR of any phone on the market." Within the minimalist-phone stratum, Light Phone and Punkt do NOT position on EMF. Mudita Pure uniquely names a *cause* (radiofrequency exposure as a health risk) and ties a proprietary mechanism (patented antenna) to addressing it. This satisfies the Product-UM test: a mechanism positioned as uniquely differentiating against alternatives, with novel information (SAR numbers, lab test results) educating the buyer. It is arguably a **Problem-UM as well** (EMF as the unnamed cause of phone-related harm) but Mudita stops short of medicalizing EMF claims — they lead with the engineering, not a Problem-UM narrative.
  - For Kompakt: **UM = none.** "E Ink phone with hardware kill-switch and no Google" is differentiated execution, but the mechanism (e-ink + curated OS) is the category default for the new minimalist-phone wave — same playbook as Light Phone III / Punkt MP02. No uniquely-named cause or proprietary positioning.
  - On distraction itself: UM = none for both products. The "phone is the distraction" problem-mechanism is shared category-wide.
