## Explicit "control / digital minimalism / unplugging" frame? — **NO (adjacent, not explicit).**

reMarkable does NOT market digital-minimalism, "unplug," "take back control," "less screen time," or addiction/dopamine vocabulary. They market **focused work / better thinking** — a productivity outcome, not a tech-relationship outcome.

Verbatim evidence of what they DO market (productivity / focus, not minimalism):
- "The Paper Tablets for Focused Work" (homepage hero)
- "helps you think better, with smart tools"
- "Close the gap between how you think and how you work"
- "a paper tablet made for thinking" / "Your place to think" (Paper Pure)
- "reMarkable Paper Pro: for a focused, organized, and paperless workplace"
- "Distraction-free for deeper focus. Try it now risk free"
- "No multitasking, just deep focus"
- "Give your team a smarter way to work" (B2B)

Closest adjacency to the dumb-device transformation:
- "Distraction-free for deeper focus" — anti-distraction angle (overlaps)
- "35% less stress" / "20% more focus" (vs. PC) — stress-relief from connected devices (overlaps)
- "It's not your typical tablet… just a focused space to think" (influencer) — overlaps
- One ADHD influencer creative: "As a left-brain creative personality type with a strong dose of ADHD, it's easy for me to get distracted by social media..." — incidental, not positioned

What's missing for a true control frame: no "phone is the problem," no "unplug," no "intentional tech," no "reclaim attention," no "digital detox / minimalism" language. Distraction is framed as a workplace/productivity friction, not a life-design problem.

## Problem-mechanism framing (verbatim)

Cause is positioned as **connected multitasking devices**, framed as workplace cognitive load — NOT the phone-as-addictive-tech story the dumb-device market shares.

- "No bright, flickering lights that cause eye strain" (mechanism — emitted-light screens)
- "No multitasking, just deep focus" (mechanism — app-switching cost)
- Paper Pure cites "35% less stress" and "20% more focus" **vs. a PC** (comparison target is the PC, not the phone)
- B2B copy positions the problem as scattered teams/tools, not addiction

Notable: the phone is essentially absent as a named antagonist. Light Phone / Brick / Daylight name the phone explicitly; reMarkable does not. This is the cleanest tell that they're not playing the dumb-device frame.

This problem-mechanism is a **shared causal story** within the e-ink-focus category (Boox, Supernote, Kindle Scribe, Kobo all name distraction from connected devices). Mark UM as **none** for the dumb-device market — they own a Product-UM ("the paper tablet") but not a Problem-UM here.

## Sub-niche targeted (in dumb-device context)

**Deep-work knowledge workers / "think better" professionals** — Cal-Newport-adjacent, but reMarkable never names Deep Work / Newport. Mapped to the brief's candidate list:

- Deep-work knowledge workers / writers — **PRIMARY.** Direct hits: "focused work," "deeper focus," "made for thinking," meeting/document imagery, B2B segment.
- Digital minimalists (Cal Newport readers) — **adjacent, not explicit.** No minimalism vocabulary. A Newport reader could self-identify in, but reMarkable does not court them.
- ADHD-identifying adults — **incidental only.** Single influencer creative, not a positioned segment.
- Parents / recovering tech-addicted / dopamine-fasting — **not targeted.** Zero signal.

Net: reMarkable targets the **status / mastery** flavor of attention-control (do better work) — not the **survival / recovery** flavor (escape addictive tech) that defines the dumb-device niche.

## Price point + buyer in dumb-device context

- Price: device $399 (Paper Pure) → $629 (Paper Pro) base; bundles to ~$908; +Connect $3.99/mo ($39.90/yr).
- Buyer (this market): self-buy white-collar professional / knowledge worker buying a productivity tool — often expensable / B2B. Gift / B2B secondary. NOT the digital-minimalist self-buyer who frames the purchase as a lifestyle / addiction intervention (Light Phone, Brick buyer).
- Distribution: DTC, Staples (select), remarkable.com/business B2B.

## Positioning overlap/distance vs. Kam's $900 device

**Overlap:**
- E-ink mechanism (reflective, no blue light, no notifications by design)
- Single-purpose / dedicated-device structural argument
- Anti-distraction angle ("Distraction-free for deeper focus") — partial
- Stress/eye-strain relief angle — partial
- Price band: Kam $900 lands above reMarkable's base ($399–629) but inside the bundle ceiling (~$908). Premium-tier positioning is comparable.

**Distance (where Kam can play that reMarkable does not):**
- reMarkable owns "focused **work** / paperless workplace." Kam can own "**control over your relationship with the phone**" — the explicit minimalism / unplug frame reMarkable refuses.
- reMarkable never names the phone as the problem. Kam can.
- reMarkable's buyer = productivity buyer (status/mastery). Kam's potential dumb-device buyer = recovery/intentionality buyer (survival/pain pole) — a different driver entirely.
- reMarkable's "paper tablet" Product-UM is saturated; Kam's foldable + programmable form factor is unclaimed in this market and lets Kam avoid head-to-head spec war on "paper-like feel."
- Sub-niche openings reMarkable leaves: ADHD adults (only incidental), digital minimalists (adjacent but uncourted), recovering tech-addicted, parents modeling less screen time.

**Collision risk:** Kam collides with reMarkable on **mechanism** (e-ink single-purpose device) and on **angle** ("distraction-free / deep focus") if Kam leads with productivity framing. Kam does NOT collide if Kam leads with control / minimalism / phone-replacement framing — that lane is empty at reMarkable.
