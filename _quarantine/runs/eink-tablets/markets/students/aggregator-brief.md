# Aggregator Brief — Students Market · Market Profile

**One job:** roll up per-competitor records into one market profile. You do NOT
analyze new competitors and you do NOT score — you assemble.

## Inputs

- All competitor records in `/home/kyu3/PMF/runs/eink-tablets/markets/students/brands/`
- The roster: `/home/kyu3/PMF/runs/eink-tablets/markets/students/competitive-set.md`
- For e-ink incumbents: the Students-specific deltas in `students/brands/` PLUS
  the full Faith records at
  `/home/kyu3/PMF/runs/eink-tablets/markets/faith/brands/` (reMarkable, Boox,
  Kindle Scribe, Daylight).
- Context: our product is a $900 foldable, programmable e-ink tablet. Market =
  students × better grades via focus.

## Procedure

1. **Read all records** (incl. Faith records for the e-ink incumbents).

2. **Cluster into market cells = niche × transformation.** Likely cells for
   Students: school-issued workspace, parent-bought study device,
   student-self-bought study tablet, kid-safe/restricted device, parental-control
   software add-on. A competitor sits in one primary cell.
   **Saturation counts WITHIN a cell only** — never pool across cells.

3. **Per cell, aggregate** — frequency counts for claims (base + enhanced),
   mechanisms / UMs, angles, features, targeted sub-niches, buyer types. Plus
   price band, sophistication stage, who runs paid acquisition.

4. **Buyer-side synthesis** — Students is structurally buyer ≠ user (mixed). A
   primary deliverable. Surface:
   - Which cells are parent-buyer dominated, student-self-buyer dominated,
     school-institutional.
   - What each buyer type pays, where they buy, why they buy.
   - Where the $900 price point lands for each buyer type — parent gift to
     K-12, parent purchase for college, student-self-buy for college, etc.

5. **Cross-cell synthesis:**
   - **Differentiation whitespace** — what transformation / lane is unclaimed.
   - **Price-band reality vs $900** — every cell's price band laid against our
     target; name ceilings.
   - **Proven-spend picture** — where money demonstrably flows.
   - **Problem-UM vs problem-mechanism** — is "device is the distraction" a
     unique UM or a shared causal story across this market? (Likely shared.)

6. **Light trend research** — WebSearch for: student-device / education-tech
   adoption trends, ADHD diagnosis trends in school-age population, parental
   spending on study tools, college device-purchasing patterns, "phones in
   schools" policy trends.

7. **Gate 1 evidence dossier** — assemble under the four variables.
   **Do NOT score.** Evidence only.
   - **Desire to Solve** — proven spend, core-driver proximity, severity,
     frequency.
   - **D2C Feasibility** — believability (can a $900 e-ink device credibly
     deliver better-grades-via-focus?), economics (price-band fit at $900 for
     parent-buyer at K-12 vs college, vs student-self-buyer).
   - **Market Sophistication** — claim / enhanced-claim counts, competitor
     sophistication, revenue, distribution, UM availability.
   - **Market Growth** — trend velocity, why-now, adjacent signals.

Write `/home/kyu3/PMF/runs/eink-tablets/markets/students/students-market-profile.md`
