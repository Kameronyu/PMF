## Does Daylight target students / parents in copy or ads?

**Split brand. The flagship and the Kids line are aimed at totally different buyers.**

**Flagship (`daylightcomputer.com`, DC-1 adult positioning) — NO student framing.**
WebFetch of the home page returned ZERO verbatim hits for "student," "students,"
"college," "school," "academic," "class," "homework," "study," "grades,"
"education," "edu," "campus," or "dorm." The FAQ likewise has no education or
back-to-school section. The adult funnel is "deep focus and wellbeing,"
"digital minimalism," "circadian health" — never academic performance.

**Daylight Kids (`kids.daylightcomputer.com`) — YES, explicit school / education positioning, parent-buyer.**
Verbatim evidence:
- "Holistic support for your child's education and curriculum: do schoolwork,
  focused study, read textbooks, write notes, and use teacher-recommended
  classroom apps — all in one device."
- "A learning hub, focused & distraction-free."
- "Works with educational tools that schools and teachers already use."
- "Daylight is a more humane way to bring technology to the education system."
  — Frank Edelblut, Former NH Commissioner of Education (endorsement).
- Bundle SKUs explicitly named "Back-to-School Edition," segmented "Age 3-7"
  and "Age 8-14" (i.e. K-8; no college framing anywhere).
- Named curated apps: **Google Classroom, IXL, Chrome, Khan Academy, Kindle,
  Epic! Books** — the explicit homeschool / classroom-software stack.
- Comparison table vs iPad: lists "Parental controls," "Curated apps," "Low
  eye-strain," "Tantrum-free" as Daylight ✓ / iPad ✘ — this is a parent
  comparison, not a student one.

Meta Ads (re-confirmed from Faith artifacts):
- Flagship `daylight_adv.txt`: 0 active ads. Adult/wellbeing funnel is PR +
  podcasts + influencer, not Meta paid social.
- `daylight-kids_adv.txt`: 3 active ads, ALL parent-targeted. Hooks: "To the
  end of iPad Kids," "You can't blame kids for struggling to focus,"
  "Introducing, Daylight Kids — happier, healthier, and smarter children."
  Ad #3 (Library ID 2358490071322776) explicitly references school devices:
  "Traditional school devices emit blue light and flicker 24/7…If your child
  has to be on a screen for school, let it be one that supports healthy
  development." This is the closest the brand gets to a "school" hook —
  framed at parents of K-8 kids forced onto screens for schoolwork, NOT at
  college students or K-12 self-buy.

**Quantified Daylight Kids lean:** brand is structurally bifurcated. Flagship
page = 0 student/school keywords, 0 Meta ads. Kids line = explicit "Back-to-
School Edition" SKU, ESA-funding callouts, education-system endorsement,
100% of active Meta ads. Daylight is actively running the parent/kid play —
it's not a side experiment, it's the entire current paid-acquisition surface.

## Price band relevant to student buyers

- Flagship DC-1: **$729** one-time, no student discount, no EDU verification,
  no financing surfaced. Same price for everyone.
- Daylight Kids "Back-to-School Edition" bundle: **$799 list → $729 with
  SOLSTICE code** (adds protective case + stylus or sleeve + stylus). Bundle
  with accessories effectively matches flagship price. Not a student discount
  — a parent-buyer SKU.
- ESA (Education Savings Account) funding accepted in **AZ, AR, LA, MS, MT,
  NH, TN, UT, WV, WY** (10 states); also approved for **OpenEd reimbursement
  in qualifying states**. This is the financing story for the parent-buyer:
  state-funded school-choice dollars cover the $729, not a discount off it.

No college-student / .edu / university-store pricing. No back-to-school
promo at the flagship level. Price floor for students is the full $729 retail
unless the parent has access to ESA funds.

## School / education programs

- No bulk classroom / school-IT / district-purchase program surfaced.
- No "Daylight in Education" portal analogous to Apple/Google for Education.
- Education play is **funding-channel only** (ESA + OpenEd) targeted at
  homeschool / school-choice parents, not at schools-as-buyers. Endorsement
  from a former state Commissioner of Education is positioning, not a
  program. Inquiries route to `hello@daylightcomputer.com`.

## What they say about distraction-free learning, academic performance, or focus

Flagship (adult): "deep focus and wellbeing," "A distraction-free space for
learning & creativity," "A sacred space for focused writing without
distraction." Focus is framed as a *life* outcome, not a *grades* outcome —
no verbatim mention of academic performance, GPA, test scores, or grades.

Daylight Kids: focus framed as developmental + behavioral, not academic-
performance. "Smarter Children" header but the proof points are "thoughtfully
curated apps" and "learning hub, focused & distraction-free" — not test
scores or grades. The strongest academic-adjacent claim: "Daylight's paper-
like display lets kids read, research, and learn without compromising their
bedtime or focus" (ad copy). Closest to our target transformation ("better
grades via focus"), but Daylight hedges short of claiming the grade outcome
and stays on the bedtime / focus / development side.

## Buyer characterization for student purchases

- **Primary buyer: parent.** Unambiguous on the Kids line — every testimonial
  is a parent (Notion co-founder, nurse practitioner / author, oncologist,
  author/mother), every CTA assumes parent-as-purchaser ("a much deserved
  sigh of relief when handing over tech to their precious littles"), every ad
  is parent-guilt + parent-relief ("You can't blame kids for struggling…").
- **Purchase context:** parent-as-gatekeeper for K-8. "First device to give
  your children." Funded by parent wallet or by state ESA dollars routed
  through the parent. NOT class requirement, NOT school-IT procurement, NOT
  college-student self-buy.
- **Where bought:** DTC only (`kids.daylightcomputer.com`). No Amazon, no
  retail, no school channel, no campus store.
- **Age band targeted:** explicitly **Age 3-7 and Age 8-14** — K-8 only.
  College / high-school self-buyers are not in the SKU lineup or copy.
- **Buyer for the flagship $729 DC-1:** self-buy adult — digital-minimalism,
  writer, biohacker, founder-adjacent. NOT a student buyer.

## Most relevant analog to OUR product (programmable e-ink at premium price)

Daylight is the closest e-ink incumbent to our product on three dimensions:
premium price ($729 vs our $900), focus/wellbeing transformation, and
"reinvented computer" category framing. Its actual go-to-market signal for
the student/parent question:

- The **adult $729 unit sells on values + health + founder authority** — not
  on student/academic framing. There is no evidence Daylight has tried and
  abandoned student self-buy; it never tried.
- The **parent-buyer K-8 play is where Daylight is putting paid acquisition
  dollars right now** — same hardware, repositioned with curated software +
  parental controls, sold at the same effective price ($729) but with ESA
  funding as the close. This is the real-world signal that a parent will pay
  $700+ for an e-ink device when the pitch is "save your kid from iPad" +
  state-funded payment path.
- The college / K-12 student self-buy lane is **structurally empty in
  Daylight's playbook.** No evidence collected that premium e-ink sells direct
  to a student wallet at this price.

## Strongest signal on premium-price e-ink into student/parent buyer

**Parent of K-8: YES, validated.** Daylight is currently running an entire
parent-funded sub-brand at our exact price band with three concurrent Meta
ad creatives and a school-choice / ESA financing path. The "save my kid from
the iPad" parent will pay $700+ for an e-ink device IF the pitch is health,
focus, and developmental harm-avoidance, AND IF a state-funded payment route
exists. Endorsement from a former state Education Commissioner suggests the
homeschool / school-choice channel is the leading edge.

**College student or K-12 student self-buy: NO signal.** Daylight has no
student-discount SKU, no .edu verification, no campus / dorm copy, no
back-to-school-for-the-student framing. The closest premium e-ink incumbent
to our product is, in practice, not selling into a student wallet at all.

## Notes / gaps

- The Kids bundle "Back-to-School Edition" name is the most directly
  student-adjacent SKU in the e-ink category at the $700+ tier — worth
  watching for what the SOLSTICE code becomes after summer 2026.
- ESA / OpenEd financing is a meaningful precedent for our product: in 10
  states, parents can pay for a $700+ "educational" device with state funds.
  This is a financing channel we could borrow if we lean parent-buyer.
- Daylight does not split K-12 from college. Its split is **K-8 (parent-buyer)
  vs. adult self-buy**, with high-school / college as an uncovered middle.
- No academic-performance / grades claim found anywhere — Daylight stops at
  "focus" and "smarter." A grades-outcome promise is open positioning.
- No Daylight Kids screenshot artifact captured here beyond the existing
  Faith-run `daylight-kids_adv.png` / `daylight-kids_adv.txt`.
