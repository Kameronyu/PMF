# Competitive Set — Faith / Devotional Market

**Market:** Christian faith / devotional buyers · transformation = a focused, present
devotional life (undistracted Scripture + prayer).
**Fixed product:** $900 foldable, programmable e-ink tablet.
**Competitive set = everything the buyer could use to get focused devotional time.**
Four strata. Per stratum: ranked by market relevance + size/scale. ★ = must-analyze.

Compiled by FINDER agent · 2026-05-22. Roster only — no in-depth analysis.

## Key finding — Does any e-ink player target faith?

**No.** Evidence:
- No major e-ink manufacturer (reMarkable, Boox, Kindle Scribe, Supernote, Daylight,
  Kobo, or any of the ~30 brands in the eink run) markets to Christians or runs
  faith-based campaigns. Confirmed via search — only third-party marketing agencies and
  user-generated content connect e-ink to Bible use.
- The only *faith-targeted dedicated devices* — In-Touch "Bible Tablet" (~$120–200) and
  Sharper Image e-Bible (~$120–160) — are **LCD Android tablets, not e-ink.**
- Faith on e-ink exists only as a **third-party content cottage industry**: Keystone
  Study Bible (e-ink-optimized KJV), reMarkable/Boox/Supernote Bible PDFs and journaling
  templates sold on Etsy and niche sites.

**Implication:** A faith-targeted e-ink device would be a category-of-one on the
hardware side. The competitive pressure is from substitutes — free apps (YouVersion),
$25–60 journaling Bibles, and cheap $120 LCD "Bible tablets" — not from a faith e-ink
incumbent.

## Summary

- **Roster counts:** Physical 8 · Apps 7 · General tablets + software 7 · E-ink 6 = **28 entries**, 12 marked must-analyze.
- **Physical price spread:** ~$7 (single-book Scripture journals) / ~$23–26 (cheap journaling Bible) up to **~$230 Schuyler Quentel** and $300–400+ for top Cambridge/Allan's heirloom editions — roughly a 17x+ spread within one stratum.
- **E-ink targets faith?** **No** — no e-ink player markets to faith; faith-dedicated devices are cheap LCD; Bible-on-e-ink is third-party PDFs/templates only.
- **Surprising:**
  1. A faith-dedicated *hardware* sub-niche already exists (In-Touch Bible Tablet since 2011, Sharper Image e-Bible) — but everyone chose **cheap LCD at ~$120–200**, none chose e-ink, and none anywhere near $900.
  2. **Daylight Computer** sells nearly the exact "calm, focused, distraction-free" transformation this market wants — at $729, secular framing — making it a positioning competitor even though it never says "faith."
  3. The real ceiling competitor is paper: Christians already pay $230–400+ for a goatskin heirloom Bible, so the $900 price is not unthinkable to this buyer — but the free YouVersion floor (1B installs) is the harder wall.
