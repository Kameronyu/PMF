## Product
General-purpose tablet line. Four current models, one-time hardware purchase (no subscription for the device itself):
- iPad (A16, 11-inch) — from $349
- iPad mini (A17 Pro, 8.3-inch) — from $499 (Wi-Fi); $649 (Wi-Fi + Cellular)
- iPad Air (M4) — 11-inch from $599; 13-inch from $799
- iPad Pro (M5) — 11-inch from $999; 13-inch from $1,299
Price band as a hardware substitute for our $900 e-ink device: $349–$799 covers the directly comparable tablets; iPad Pro sits above our price. The $349 entry iPad and $499 mini are the sharpest price anchors against a $900 device. Faith use happens entirely through third-party apps (e.g. YouVersion Bible App, free) — Apple sells no faith software or bundle.

## Reviews / VOC quick-read
- review/rating volume + rough sentiment: iPad is broadly positively reviewed as a general tablet; not faith-graded. The relevant VOC is faith users' experience of using iPad for Scripture.
- brand loyalty signal: very high Apple ecosystem loyalty; iPad is a default-owned device.
- hidden use cases / notable likes & dislikes: faith VOC is mixed. Likes — YouVersion split-view "shines on the iPad," easy verse copy/share, thousands of free reading plans and translations. Dislikes — a recurring "Bible App vs physical Bible" debate where users report switching back to a physical Bible because the iPad's notifications, apps, and connectivity pull them out of focused study (e.g. biblemomma.com "Why I Switched to Physical"). This distraction complaint is exactly the opening a dedicated, distraction-free devotional device targets.

## Notes / gaps
- Apple targets the faith market with ZERO copy — niche correctly recorded as general / not specified. Faith use is 100% buyer repurposing via third-party apps.
- Decision-relevant: iPad is the true substitute, not a positioned competitor. Its strength is the sunk-cost "I already own one" at $349–$799; its weakness — verified in faith VOC — is that the always-connected, app-laden, emissive-screen, notification-heavy device actively undermines focused devotional time. That gap (distraction-free, single-purpose, reflective e-ink) is the differentiation lane our $900 device must own, because on price/versatility iPad cannot be beaten.
- iPad Pro ($999–$1,299) sits above our $900 price; iPad / mini / Air ($349–$799) sit below — our device must justify a price premium over an already-owned cheaper general tablet purely on the focus/devotional transformation, since it cannot win on hardware specs or cost.
