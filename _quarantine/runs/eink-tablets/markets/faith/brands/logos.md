## Product
Logos Bible Study Platform — software ecosystem (desktop app, web app, mobile app), not a device. Two stacked pricing models:
- **Subscriptions (recurring):** Premium $9.99/mo or $99.99/yr; Pro $14.99/mo or $149.99/yr; Max $19.99/mo or $199.99/yr. 30-day free trial. Owners of legacy Logos packages get $1–$7/mo off.
- **One-time purchases (library + feature expansions, perpetual ownership):**
  - Library Expansions: $99.99 (Small) / $299.99 (Medium) / $499.99 (Large)
  - Feature Expansions: $199.99 (Small) / $399.99 (Medium) / $599.99 (Large)
  - Individual books/resources: ~$7.49–$274.99 (e.g. BDAG lexicon $123.74; BDAG/HALOT bundle $274.99)
  - **Master Collection: $2,789.99** (marked down from $4,649.99), "over fifteen hundred reference works"
  - Historical/legacy base packages reported $259.99 (Starter) up to ~$10,799.99 (top tier) — confidence: proxy, from third-party 2026 buyer guides.
Full price range: ~$7.49 single book → ~$2,790 Master Collection → ~$10,800 historical top library. Highest-spend digital faith buyer by a wide margin.

## Mechanism / UM
- mechanism(s) — how/why it works: A single searchable, cross-linked digital library — every commentary, lexicon, and reference work is tagged and interconnected so a verse instantly surfaces all related scholarship; original-language tagging makes Greek/Hebrew accessible to non-specialists; AI assistant collapses research time by answering natural-language questions across the whole library.
- problem-mechanism (causal story, may be shared): The Bible is "an ancient book" — distant in language, culture, and history — so an untrained reader risks studying it "poorly" or misreading it. Scholarship that resolves this is locked in expensive, scattered print volumes most people cannot afford or search.
- UM: **Feature-UM** — the integrated, fully-searchable cross-linked theological library + original-language tagging (now AI-layered). Positioned as the category-defining upgrade for serious Bible study ("#1 Bible app for transformative study"); it is an upgrade-within-category mechanism, not a unique problem cause. Not a Problem-UM (the "ancient book / hard to study" cause is generic).

The fear-based "studying it poorly" hook and the segmented-by-identity tiers are Stage 4–5 moves (new mechanism + identification), not Stage 1–2 direct-benefit claims.
