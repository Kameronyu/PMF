## Product
- **Bible App** — flagship mobile/tablet/web app. Free. Read or listen to Scripture, Bible Plans, Prayers, study with Friends, videos. 3,500+ Bible versions in 2,300+ languages.
- **Bible App Lite** — stripped variant for older phones, low storage, slow/offline connections. Free.
- **Bible App for Kids** — 41 animated Bible stories for children. Free.
- **YouVersion Connect / YouVersion Platform** — B2B/developer-facing: lets churches and developers embed Scripture content; real-time dashboards. (Not consumer-facing.)
- Price points + pricing model: **Free across the entire family of apps. No in-app purchases. No ads.** Model = nonprofit / donation-funded (see Offers/value models).

## Mechanism / UM
- mechanism(s) — how/why it works: **Reading Plans + Verse of the Day + streaks/notifications** create a structured daily prompt loop that builds the habit; **massive version/language library** removes the access barrier; **Friends/social layer** adds accountability; **audio + offline** removes the situational barrier to engagement.
- problem-mechanism (causal story, may be shared): Implicit only — "Scripture should be available and easily accessible to everyone" implies the problem is access friction (cost, language, device, connectivity). Notably the copy does NOT name phone-distraction as the problem — the opposite of the e-ink/focus framing. YouVersion's premise is that the phone IS the delivery vehicle.
- UM: **none.** No mechanism is positioned as unique differentiation. The library scale and free price are dominance facts, not positioned UMs. (If anything its "moat" is incumbency + free, not a mechanism story.)

## Reviews / VOC quick-read
- review/rating volume + rough sentiment: 13M App Store ratings at 4.9/5 — overwhelmingly positive. Universally regarded as the category standard.
- brand loyalty signal: Extremely high — record growth every month of 2024, 798K installs in a single day (Jan 5), strong recurring-donor base ("Sowers"). Cultural fixture (annual "Verse of the Year" gets press coverage).
- hidden use cases / notable likes & dislikes: Liked — breadth of versions/languages, free, offline, reading plans, audio, cross-device sync. Engagement spikes tied to anxiety/peace-seeking (top 2024 verse = Philippians 4:6 on anxiety; top searched words "love," "peace," "faith," "prayer") — signals users come to the app for comfort during stress, a need-state worth noting. Common complaints across app stores (general knowledge, not verified here) center on UI bloat / social-feed clutter and notification volume — i.e., the app has drifted from pure reading toward a feature-heavy social product.

## Notes / gaps
- This is the **price floor and category anchor**: free, 1B+ installs, no ads, no IAP. Any paid faith product ($900 e-ink tablet included) is not competing on "access to Scripture" — that war is over and YouVersion won it. Decision-relevant: the e-ink play must sell a transformation YouVersion structurally CANNOT deliver — namely *distraction-free, single-purpose* engagement. YouVersion's entire premise is the phone as delivery vehicle; it cannot frame the phone as the problem. That is the open lane.
