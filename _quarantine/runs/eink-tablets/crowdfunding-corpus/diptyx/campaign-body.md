## Campaign Body Copy (verbatim from diptyx.dev + press-quoted campaign copy)

> "Diptyx is an ESP32-based, open-source ereader. Its unique dual screen design allows it to close like a book, protecting the screens without requiring a case, perfect for reading on the go."

> "Automatic standby mode and e-ink displays enable the device to run for weeks on a single charge, while it powers up in seconds with the press of a button."

> "Fully open-source, Diptyx is easy to repair and customize, letting you read whenever and wherever you want, forever."

> "It features no form of DRM, allowing you to read books from any digital bookstore, as long as they are in EPUB format."

> "No internet connection, accounts, or cloud services are required."

Additional copy from CNX Software (quoting Crowd Supply campaign directly):
> "its unique dual-screen design allows it to close like a book, protecting the screens without requiring a case"

From Good e-Reader (Oct 27 2025, quoting campaign):
> "It features no DRM, allowing you to read books from any digital bookstore as long as they are in EPUB format. No internet connection, accounts, or cloud services are required."

> "The Diptyx firmware lets you personalize your reading experience: upload custom fonts, adjust margins, and set line spacing, all through the built-in settings menu. In addition, users are free to modify the source code itself: add Wi-Fi functionality such as an RSS reader, create unique custom games, or make an offline Wikipedia backup; the limit is your own imagination."

From TechEBlog (Oct 26 2025, paraphrasing campaign copy with creator quotes):
> "no accounts, no subscriptions, no nagging updates"
> "fits in your purse like an old favorite novel"

From Linuxiac (Dec 14 2025, quoting campaign):
> "upload custom fonts, adjust margins and line spacing, enable dark mode, and configure standby behavior"

From Hackaday (Dec 24 2025, quoting campaign / creator):
> "The displays themselves are driven with custom look-up tables to allow for low-latency updates when turning pages."

## Creator Bio

Creator: **Martijn den Hoed** (Reddit: u/spacerower). Location: Lisse, Netherlands.

From NotebookCheck (Oct 24 2025):
> "The creator, known as u/spacerower on Reddit, developed the project after their Kobo device broke. Rather than purchasing another proprietary reader, they constructed this alternative. The design emphasizes device ownership and repairability."

From TechEBlog (Oct 26 2025):
> "Den Hoed started this project when his Kobo Clara BW died on him one too many times. Instead of spending money on another locked-down reader from a large company, he decided to build his own."

From Yanko Design (Oct 28 2025):
> Creator described as building "for tinkerers, readers, and anyone who wants to truly own their digital books and the device that displays them" (Martijn den Hoed quote per Yanko Design)
