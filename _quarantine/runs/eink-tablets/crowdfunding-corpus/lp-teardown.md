## Data quality caveats (read first)

This is a teardown of pre-launch reservation LPs, not post-deposit funnels. Reasons:

1. The e-ink corpus ran **email-only** reservation, not $1 deposits. None of the 4 e-ink campaigns we have on disk ran a LaunchBoom-style refundable deposit. They captured emails on a creator-owned page and drove KS-launch-day spikes off that list. The deposit mechanic itself comes from the LaunchBoom framework, not from a copyable e-ink LP.
2. Some pre-launch LPs went dark or pivoted post-launch. theainote.com is now a retail store (was a pre-launch AI tablet site). Zerowriter Fold's LP is still pre-launch/launch-live as of capture. pre-launch.viwoods.com SSL-errors on fetch; we have only the page title and press references. Diptyx is partial.
3. The single LaunchBoom case-study URL we tried (Hooke Lav) 404'd. The $1-reservation mechanic below is drawn from LaunchBoom's own public explainer at launchboom.com/game-tips/why-the-1-dollar-reservation-helps. It is the agency that pioneered the mechanic across 100+ campaigns. Treat it as framework reference, not a specific LP rip.

Per the prompt: where a page or funnel step isn't visible, the section says so explicitly. No invented copy.

## LP 1: Zerowriter Fold (zerowriter.ink/pages/zerowriter-fold)

Status at capture: KS campaign live. "Funded in 42 minutes" already in header. So this LP serves dual duty: pre-launch reservation in the run-up, then KS driver post-launch.

### Section order

1. **Hero** + immediate Kickstarter CTA + email capture
2. Trust badges + press logos

4. Feature showcase (4 features)
5. Quick specs

12. Final CTA + email capture

### Section-by-section

**1. Hero**
- Job: Grab attention, install the buyer outcome in one line, hand off to the CTA.
- Belief + device: "This is the focused-writing device I've been looking for." Devices: identity (built for writers), novelty (paper-like e-ink + mechanical), specificity (six-inch, weeks of battery), negation (no subscriptions, no bloat).
- Verbatim: "Write anywhere. Distraction free." / "Zerowriter Fold is the paper-like word processor built for writers. Six-inch e-ink display, mechanical keyboard, weeks of battery life, and user-controllable lighting. No subscriptions. No bloat." / Scarcity overlay: "First Wave pricing, limited to 500 units" / "Save up to $90 USD, 33% off retail." / CTAs in same viewport: "Back us on Kickstarter" + "Stay updated by email."

**2. Trust badges + press logos**
- Job: Be taken seriously. Defuse "is this real" early.
- Belief + device: "Reputable outlets cover this." Devices: press authority + made-in-Canada origin marker.
- Verbatim: "Designed & Assembled in Ottawa, Canada" / "No subscriptions. Ever" / "Simple, open software" / Press logos: Financial Times, Tom's Hardware, Yanko Design, Liliputing, Good e-Reader.

**3. Product overview**
- Job: Crystallize what it IS in one paragraph so the user can decide to keep reading.
- Belief + device: "This is a deliberately limited writing-only device." Devices: identity (purpose-built), specificity, framing (modern day word processor).
- Verbatim: "The next Zerowriter" / "A writing tool that keeps work in focus." / "Fold open and get going. Zerowriter Fold is a purpose-built e-paper word processor in a slim, laptop-like enclosure." / "This was built for writers looking for simplicity and security."

**4. Feature showcase (4 features)**
- Job: Install desire by mapping each feature to a felt outcome.
- Belief + device: "Every spec exists for a reason I care about." Devices: feature-to-outcome translation, sensory cues, lifestyle imagery.
- Verbatim feature 1: "Fast 6" e-ink display with frontlight" / "Clarity without compromise. No perceptible typing delay. Gentle on the eyes, hard to put down."
- Feature 2: "Low-profile mechanical keyboard" / "A writing tool should feel good. Custom keycaps, hot-swappable Choc switches, and a standard 60% layout."
- Feature 3: "Weeks of battery life. Instant on." / "Open it and write."
- Feature 4: "Goes everywhere you do" / "It slips into your bag and comes out ready to write."

**5. Quick specs**
- Job: Hand the technical buyer the proof. Pre-empt skepticism.
- Belief + device: "These numbers check out." Device: specificity, table format.
- Verbatim: Price "$239 USD (was $329)" / Weight "900 g" / Display "6" e-ink (5" × 3.75" viewing area)" / Battery "50–100 hours" / "Any angle" hinge.

**6. Founder quote**
- Job: Personalize the company. Cheaper than a founder video.
- Belief + device: "There's a real person behind this with a point of view." Device: founder voice + value statement.
- Verbatim: "A good, reliable writing device shouldn't cost an arm and a leg. We're here to make it happen." / Attribution: "Adam, Founder, The Zerowriter Company."

**7. Philosophy (offline / zero distractions)**
- Job: Install the worldview the buyer needs to opt into to want this device.
- Belief + device: "Offline-by-default is the moral choice." Devices: rule of negation (list of "no's"), values framing.
- Verbatim: "Offline by default" / "Zero distractions. Zero exceptions." / "No WiFi required. No notifications. No AI integrations. No mandatory cloud. No subscriptions. No tracking. No browser." / "A high-trust device." / "Your writing is yours."

**8. Software trust breakdown (5 features)**
- Job: Handle the meta-objection "but what happens if the company dies."
- Belief + device: "This product survives the company." Devices: open-source community proof, anti-VC stance, specific file-management transparency.
- Verbatim: "Built on trust" / "Software that respects you." / "Buy it once, own it forever." / "No subscriptions. Venture capitalists hate us." (note the explicit anti-VC line) / "Built on Zerowriter Core, open source software the world can read and verify. Community supported, no matter what happens to us." / "Saves to micro-SD, like a camera."

**9. Company credibility / founder backstory**
- Job: De-risk vaporware. Prove they ship.
- Belief + device: "They've shipped before, they'll ship again." Devices: track record (Zerowriter Ink shipped), production capacity number, named geography, fulfillment policy.
- Verbatim: "A small team that builds great tools." / "We've done this before." / "We started in 2024 with a viral DIY project and a Raspberry Pi Zero." / "In '25 and '26, we've shipped Zerowriter Ink to writers around the world." / "Each device assembled by hand and personally tested in-house." / "Production capacity exceeds 300 units per month." / "Our first units will be ready to roll forward in late 2026. We'll be fulfilling this campaign first-in, first-out."

**10. Indie Author Program**
- Job: Subtle off-ramp + community-builder. Signals the company cares about the use case beyond the device sale.
- Belief + device: "They're part of the writing scene, not just hardware vendors." Device: adjacent offer, community signal.
- Verbatim: "Got books sitting around?" / "We're looking to purchase books from indie and traditionally published authors at a wholesale rate."

**11. FAQ (9 Qs)**
- Job: Pre-empt the deposit-stopping objections in writing.
- Belief + device: "They've thought of my exact question." Devices: candor on shipping/timing, candor on commitment, candor on price/tariff.
- Verbatim Q2: "What is the First Wave, exactly?" / "We're fulfilling on a first-come, first-serve basis. Signing up now holds your spot and your First Wave price. There's no payment required until the campaign goes live."
- Verbatim Q7: "What if I sign up but decide not to order?" / "No problem. We don't spam. No pressure, no commitment."
- Verbatim Q6 (tariff candor): "American customers: as of 2026, goods imported from Canada carry approximately a 10% tariff."

**12. Final CTA + email capture**
- Job: Close. If they scrolled to the bottom, ask for the action twice.
- Belief + device: "Now is the moment." Devices: capacity scarcity, savings specificity.
- Verbatim: "Kickstarter is live. 500 units only." / "Back us now. Save up to $90." / "First Wave pricing saves you up to $90 USD off retail, limited to 500 units, first in, first out." / "No spam. Unsubscribe at any time."

**13. Conversion popup (2 states)**
- Job: Convert the leaver. Catch the email or push to KS.
- Belief + device: "Last chance to not miss this." Devices: dual CTA (back or join list), reassurance post-submit.
- Verbatim state 1: "Live on Kickstarter. We're live. The Zerowriter Fold campaign is live on Kickstarter. First Wave pricing saves you up to $90 USD off retail, limited to 500 units." / "No thanks" dismiss option.
- Verbatim state 2: "You're on the list! We'll be in touch."

### What's notable in this LP

- Email capture is the explicit pre-launch CTA. No deposit. The "First Wave" framing is a soft equivalent: signing up "holds your spot" at the discounted tier.
- Three scarcity hooks layered: capacity (500 units), discount (33%/$90 off retail), time (first-in-first-out shipping order).
- The "no payment required until the campaign goes live" line is the desire-to-no-friction bridge. It's the email version of LaunchBoom's "fully refundable $1."
- Strong anti-VC / open-source moralism is the differentiator and ALSO the trust mechanic (the product survives the company).

## LP 5: LaunchBoom $1-reservation framework

Source: WebFetch launchboom.com/game-tips/why-the-1-dollar-reservation-helps 2026-05-25 + general LaunchBoom doc snippets from search.
Substitute: A real LP case study (Hooke Lav) 404'd. Treating this as the deposit-mechanic reference rather than an LP rip.

### The mechanic

- **Amount**: $1
- **Refund posture**: Explicitly "completely refundable." Stated upfront.
- **Value exchange**: Backer gets a bonus discount or item worth more than $1
- **Conversion-rate claim**: $1 reservers are ~30x more likely to convert at campaign launch than email-only sign-ups (LaunchBoom's own published number)

### The funnel sequence (per LaunchBoom's public explainer)

1. Paid ads (typically Meta) drive cold traffic to LP
2. LP captures email
3. Reservation offer page is presented after email (the "reservation funnel" itself)
4. Price disclosure happens here (upfront, not hidden)
5. Payment page collects the $1 deposit
6. Thank-you / VIP confirmation page
7. Campaign-launch email triggers convert reservers to backers

Key page split: email capture and deposit ask are on different pages. The deposit ask is the upsell on top of an already-converted email.

### Recommended copy / framing per the LaunchBoom article

- "Avoid words like 'exclusive' or 'limited time'" on the reservation offer. Frame the deposit as a commitment indicator, not a urgency trigger.
- Emphasize "completely refundable."
- Disclose price upfront at reservation. Don't bait-and-switch.
- Frame as mutual benefit: "a way to create a win-win for the creator and the backer."
- During-campaign add-on cost: ~$4-5 more for the same bonus that reservers got. After-campaign: the bonus is sold via standard ecom. This price ladder is the reason reservers feel they got a deal.

### What this gives us

- The deposit-mechanic answer to Kam's Part 2A (the deposit offer itself).
- The bridge wording for Kam's Part 2E (desire-to-deposit handoff): "Lock your spot. $1, fully refundable, gets you the founder-price bonus and the lowest price you'll see on this device."
- Frequency note: LaunchBoom recommends NOT using "limited time" or "exclusive." That conflicts with what the e-ink LPs in the set actually do (Zerowriter uses "limited to 500 units" heavily). Tension flagged below in Part 3.

# PART 2, Focused passes (across all 5)

## A. The reservation/deposit offer itself

How each LP framed the ask:

| LP | Ask type | Amount | Refund/credit language | What depositor gets |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Zerowriter Fold | Email only | $0 | "No payment required until the campaign goes live" | First Wave pricing (~$239 USD, save $90), 500-unit capacity slot, first-in-first-out shipping priority |
| iFlytek AINOTE 2 | Email + reservation page (higizmos.com) | Unknown (page blank) | Unknown | "Exclusive Super Early Bird Benefits" (per page title); actual benefit detail not recoverable |
| Viwoods AiPaper | Email only (per press) | $0 | Not visible | KS launch notification; pre-launch list seeded the day-1 spike |
| Diptyx | Email only (Crowd Supply "notify me") | $0 | n/a | KS launch notification |
| LaunchBoom framework | $1 refundable deposit | $1 | "Completely refundable" | Founder discount + bonus item worth more than $1; ~30x conversion lift vs. email-only |

**The convention across the e-ink set (4 of 4):** Email-only reservation, no deposit. The "what you get" is a discounted founder tier + a place in the shipping queue. The refund equivalent is "no payment required" / "no commitment" reassurance.

**LaunchBoom's claim** is that pulling $1 separates the actual buyer from the curious. The 30x figure is their attribution. The e-ink set didn't test it and converted off email alone, but the e-ink set also had hardware-specific demand drivers (existing backer lists, niche press) that consumer-electronics LaunchBoom campaigns may not have.

**Where the deposit ask lands on the page (recommended convention):**
- LaunchBoom flow: email first, deposit on the NEXT page. The LP doesn't ask for $1 in the hero. It asks for an email, then escalates.
- e-ink LP flow: email on the page directly, no escalation.

For Kam's deposit funnel, the LaunchBoom split (email then deposit) is the right convention. The LP itself should look more like Zerowriter Fold than the LaunchBoom example: rich content, strong founder/trust, scarcity layered. The deposit ask escalates after the email is captured.
