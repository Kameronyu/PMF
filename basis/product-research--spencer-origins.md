## PRODUCT SELECTION FRAMEWORK

**Winning Product 3-Factor Difficulty Model**
- Product selection difficulty = magnitude of desire × market awareness stage × market sophistication
- All three factors interact with CAC — higher on any axis = higher acquisition cost + more creative investment
- Beginner guidance: avoid problem-unaware markets — expensive education required, high CAC, slow feedback loops
- Preferred entry awareness stage: solution-aware or product-aware — audience already knows a solution exists, you just need to position better

**3-Variable Difficulty Assessment** [augments existing Brand/Product Selection section]
- Before selecting a product, score it on:
  1. Magnitude of desire (how strong is the pull?)
  2. Market awareness stage (how sophisticated is the audience?)
  3. Market sophistication (how saturated is the solution landscape?)
- Higher on all three = higher CAC and more creative investment required before break-even

**Brand Builder Criteria**
- $3K minimum capital figure — do not start ads with less; insufficient to generate reliable signal
- Personal conviction as non-negotiable filter: sell only what you believe actually works
  - Why: conviction drives persuasion quality; sustained motivation through failure iterations requires belief in the product
  - Operator who doesn't believe in their product will quit at the first 3-day losing streak

**Over-Studying Failure Mode**
- Named pattern: "Fear of action masquerades as wanting more information"
- Add to Study phase of the 6-stage loop as explicit warning
- Second-pass study (after doing the thing once) produces more insight than first-pass study — doing unlocks better learning

## PRODUCT VIABILITY RESEARCH

**Kalodata 9-Step TikTok Shop Research Workflow**
- Tool: Kalodata ($45–100/month)
- 9-step sequence:
  1. Kalodata — identify products with active TikTok Shop sales velocity
  2. Meta Ad Library — confirm product has active paid traffic (proof of economics)
  3. ITRA — trademark check (both product name AND brand name)
  4. SimilarWeb — estimate competitor website traffic
  5. Reddit / TikTok comments — confirm pain exists at volume
  6. Amazon — extract price anchor (what customers are used to paying)
  7. AliExpress reverse image search — identify COGS and multiple suppliers
  8. Google Trends — confirm sustained vs. spike demand
  9. Seasonality check — is primary market currently in-season?
- Beginner AOV ceiling: $150–170 with break-even math modeled before committing

**Multi-Signal 7-Signal Product Viability Scoring Table**
- Pass/fail scoring across 7 signals before committing to MOQ:

| Signal | Tool | Pass Condition |
|---|---|---|
| Sales velocity | Kalodata | Active, growing orders |
| Trademark clear | ITRA | No active marks on product OR brand name |
| Traffic estimate | SimilarWeb | Meaningful competitor traffic visible |
| Demand trend | Google Trends | Stable or growing (not declining spike) |
| Pain confirmation | Reddit / TikTok comments | Volume of emotional complaints |
| Price anchor | Amazon | Established price point above your COGS target |
| COGS validation | AliExpress | Margin achievable at target AOV |

- Rule: no single data point is sufficient — require 5+ passes before committing
- Trademark check: check BOTH the active marks on the product category AND the specific brand name

## VALIDATION BEFORE COMMITMENT

**Validation Before MOQ Commitment**
- Never commit to Minimum Order Quantity (MOQ) without 10–30 real purchase signals
- Negotiation tactic: negotiate MOQ down (e.g., 500 → 250 units) at slightly higher per-unit cost
  - Extra per-unit cost is cheap insurance against holding dead inventory
- Alternative validation sources:
  - Etsy US-based suppliers: 30–50 domestic units for rapid validation (faster shipping, domestic quality signal)
  - Pre-orders as intent signal (collect payment before stocking)

## OPERATING SYSTEM

**Study → Research → Create → Test → Feedback → Repeat Loop (6-Stage)**
- 6-stage operating system for continuous improvement:
  1. Study — learn frameworks, study competitors, understand market
  2. Research — avatar, desire, mechanism, competitive gaps
  3. Create — copy, creative, offer
  4. Test — run ads, collect data
  5. Feedback — analyze what happened vs. hypothesis
  6. Repeat — return to appropriate stage based on what failed
- Diagnostic fallback routing:
  - Failed Create → Test: return to Research (wrong avatar, wrong mechanism, wrong desire)
  - Still failing after Research: return to Study (wrong product, wrong market)
- Second-pass Study produces more insight after doing the thing once — doing unlocks better learning

**Claude Project Context Architecture (Multi-Phase)**
- Persistent context files maintained across all phases:
  - Brand sophistication document
  - Avatar research (verbatim quote density is the primary quality lever)
  - Product URLs
  - Market sophistication assessment
- Description field in Claude Project = system prompt (sets behavioral context for all sessions)
- Same project reused across all phases — do not start fresh each session
- Free-tier workaround: drag relevant files into individual chats manually

**7-Day Onboarding Sprint Checkpoints**
- Checkpoint 1 (Days 1–7): commit to product — no switching during sprint
- Checkpoint 2: complete customer research + avatar building (do not create before this is done)
- Revenue ladder milestones with distinct focus at each stage:
  - $0–10K/month: validate product + avatar, get first purchases
  - $10–50K/month: validate offer, find sub-avatars, stabilize ROAS
  - $50–100K/month: scale proven angle, begin hiring
- Context: Spencer spent 4 years before developing proper research methodology — compress that by doing research first, not after
