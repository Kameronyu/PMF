# Advertorial: Strategy, Structure & AI-Assisted Production

An advertorial (ad + editorial hybrid) is a conversion-critical landing page asset placed between cold traffic ads and sales pages. It increases purchase receptivity by educating readers about a problem before presenting a solution, without triggering sales resistance. Mastery of advertorials distinguishes high-volume direct response operations from average ones — this system is attributed to $1.3M/month in revenue.

---

## Advertorial Format Definition & Core Mechanics (Source: Mark Builds Brands)

An advertorial combines advertising with editorial content, presenting brand messaging in the style of a news article or impartial third-party editorial. It is created by the brand but must feel as though it was written by a journalist or independent voice.

- Appears written from the perspective of a news article or editorial publication
- Actually created by the brand, not a journalist
- Must feel like educational content, not a sales pitch
- Sells and convinces customers before presenting a formal offer
- The brand's product is woven throughout but never the overt subject of the piece

---

## Advertorial as Awareness Bridge (Source: Mark Builds Brands)

Rooted in Eugene Schwartz's levels of market awareness, the advertorial functions as a bridge between cold traffic (unaware or problem-aware buyers) and the sales page (solution/product-aware state). Cold audiences resist being sold to; the advertorial bypasses this resistance.

- Targets buyers who are not yet ready to purchase when they first encounter an ad
- Sells people on the *problem* and reframes root causes — not symptoms
- Moves the reader from unaware/problem-aware to solution-aware without an overt product pitch
- Eliminates the "being sold to" feeling, increasing receptivity to the subsequent sales page
- Structural position in funnel: Cold Ad → Advertorial → Sales Page

**Key principle:** The advertorial does not pitch the product directly. It puts the reader in a psychological state where they are seeking a solution — which the sales page then provides.

## Five-Step AI Advertorial System (Source: Mark Builds Brands)

A sequential production process treating AI as a junior copywriter requiring structured inputs, not a one-shot content generator. Most failures occur because practitioners skip steps 1–2 and jump directly to writing.

**Step 1: Comprehensive AI-Assisted Research**
- Use AI to conduct all research — do not do this manually
- Research covers: target customer pain points, root causes of problem, competitor claims, market beliefs, customer language patterns
- Output of this step feeds directly into foundational documents

**Step 2: Build Four Foundational Documents**
- Four documents are constructed from research outputs before any copy is written
- These documents define customer avatar, problem framing, solution angle, and voice/tone parameters
- These are uploaded into every subsequent AI session to maintain context consistency
- Chiefing (Step 5) is the second most important step; these documents are the most important

**Step 3: Find a Currently-Running Competitor Advertorial (Swipe-to-Structure)**
- Identify a competitor advertorial that is actively running paid traffic — active spend indicates proven conversion
- Convert competitor landing page to PDF using a web-to-PDF converter
- Goal: extract structure and flow, not content
- Upload competitor PDF alongside all four foundational documents at the start of the writing session to refresh AI context

**Step 4: Write in Chunks**
- Do not generate the entire advertorial in a single prompt due to AI token/context limitations
- Write section by section: headline, lead, body blocks, transition to offer, etc.
- AI unconsciously mirrors proven direct response formulas (e.g., "They laughed when I sat down at the piano") when given strong structural inputs
- Maintain conversation and give feedback — treat AI as a junior copywriter in a dialogue, not a vending machine

**Step 5: Chief the Copy (Review)**
- "Chiefing" = reviewing and refining the output through both AI review and personal review
- Run AI review pass: prompt AI to evaluate copy against foundational documents and awareness-bridging goals
- Run personal review: apply direct response judgment to tone, authenticity, and sales resistance
- Most critical quality gate in the process

---

## Competitor Swipe-to-Structure Method (Source: Mark Builds Brands)

A specific sub-method within Step 3 for extracting structural intelligence from proven advertorials without plagiarizing content.

- Only target advertorials with active paid media behind them — ongoing ad spend signals profitability and conversion
- Convert the full competitor landing page to PDF using a web-to-PDF tool
- Upload the PDF alongside all four foundational documents at the start of each AI writing session
- AI uses competitor structure as a flow template while generating original content based on your foundational documents
- Structural elements to extract: headline formula, lead hook type, problem framing sequence, social proof placement, transition to CTA
- AI naturally adopts proven direct response structural patterns from the swipe without copying literal language

---

**Key Metrics & Benchmarks**
- Revenue attributed to this system: $1.3M/month
- Funnel position: always between cold ad and sales page, never replacing either
- Active competitor ads = proxy signal for conversion viability of that advertorial structure

**Common Pitfalls**
- Copying a competitor's advertorial into AI and requesting a rewrite — skips all research and foundational document creation, producing non-converting output
- Writing the entire advertorial in one AI prompt — token limitations degrade quality; chunk-based production is required
- Treating AI as a one-command tool rather than a junior copywriter requiring context, conversation, and iterative feedback
- Skipping the chiefing step — unreviewed AI output will contain tone mismatches and weak transitions even with strong inputs
- Selecting competitor swipes from ads that are no longer running — no active spend means unverified conversion performance
- Targeting symptom-level problems instead of root causes — reduces the reader's receptivity shift from problem-aware to solution-seeking state
