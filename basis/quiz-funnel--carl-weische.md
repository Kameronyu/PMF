# Quiz Funnel Architecture

A quiz funnel replaces static landing pages with an interactive diagnostic sequence that maps pain, builds personalization, pre-handles objections, and extracts micro-commitments before presenting a dynamically personalized sales page. Used in ecommerce (especially supplements, haircare, skincare) to increase AOV, LTV, and conversion rates.

---

## Section 1: Pain Diagnostic Sequence (Source: Carl Weische)

Five-question opening that builds a precise avatar profile while emotionally engaging prospects through their problem. Each question serves a specific psychological function.

1. **Q1 — Visual Identifier**: Age question with avatar images for easy self-identification. Reduces cognitive load and initiates engagement.
2. **Q2 — Pain Location**: Where they experience the problem. Grounds the funnel in their specific reality.
3. **Q3 — Pain Specification**: Yes/no branching to narrow exact pain type. Increases perceived diagnostic precision.
4. **Q4 — Pain Severity**: Quantifies intensity. Surfaces how acute the problem is for segmentation and copy.
5. **Q5 — Pain Timeline**: Asks when changes first started. Triggers urgency by reminding the prospect the problem is progressing.

All five answers feed forward into the final diagnosis slide and the sales page personalization.

---

## Section 2: Desired Situation + Social Proof Pivot (Source: Carl Weische)

After pain mapping, the funnel pivots from problem-focused to solution-focused framing. The pivot question asks what results the prospect expects from treatment — this surfaces their purchase motivation and primes them for the offer.

Immediately after, a social proof slide (e.g., millions of satisfied customers) is shown, directly tying their stated desired outcome to proven results. This is the first social proof exposure in the funnel, making it land with maximum relevance rather than appearing as generic testimonials.

---

## Section 3: Individualization Questions (Source: Carl Weische)

Two questions collect product-relevant personal attributes (e.g., hair type: straight/wavy/curly; hair length: short/medium/long). The explicit function is to make the prospect feel the solution is being built specifically for them rather than mass-produced.

Individualization data collected here is referenced again on the sales page ("recommended based on your quiz results"), creating narrative continuity between funnel and purchase moment.

---

## Section 4: In-Funnel Objection Pre-Handling (Source: Carl Weische)

A behavioral question (e.g., "How much time do you spend on hair daily?") surfaces the time-commitment objection. Immediately following, an info card neutralizes it before the prospect reaches the product page: *"A busy schedule doesn't have to stop you from looking and feeling years younger."*

Core principle: objections identified as common on the sales page are cleared inside the quiz funnel, reducing friction at the point of purchase. The sales page then does not need to work as hard to overcome resistance.

---

## Section 5: Causation Education + Final Diagnosis Slide (Source: Carl Weische)

Two questions educate on root causes of the problem:
- Genetic causation: "Does hair loss run in your family?"
- Lifestyle causation: "How often do you experience stress?"

This builds authority by framing the brand as diagnostically sophisticated. The section closes with a **personalized diagnosis slide** that aggregates all prior answers — age, pain severity, pain timeline, genetic/lifestyle causation — into a named result (e.g., "Stage 2 Hair Loss — caused by genetic predisposition and high stress levels"). The named diagnosis makes the solution feel evidence-based and medically credible.

---

## Section 6: Six-Month Transformation Plan Framing (Source: Carl Weische)

A transformation plan slide takes the current month and projects six months into the future, stating: "Based on your answers, we expect you to see visible results by [future month]."

This framing accomplishes two goals before the prospect sees any pricing:
1. **Pre-sells multi-month supply**: Prospect is already thinking in terms of a six-month commitment.
2. **Increases AOV**: When they reach the bundle selection, the six-month option feels necessary to reach the result they were just promised.

The slide frames the plan as "the last plan you'll ever need," adding finality and reducing comparison-shopping impulses.

---

## Section 7: Yes Street + Tension Building (Source: Carl Weische)

The final pre-sales section uses sequential micro-commitment questions leveraging consistency bias:
1. "Are you determined to finally stop your hair loss?" — emotional commitment
2. Reinforce root cause education to solidify diagnosis credibility
3. "Does a simple hair care routine sound good?" — practical commitment

A **loading screen animation** simulates individualized solution generation ("Analyzing your profile…"). This creates anticipation, reinforces personalization perception, and builds excitement before the sales page reveal.

Prospect arrives at the sales page already having said yes multiple times, already committed to a six-month plan, and in a state of anticipatory excitement.

---

## Personalized Quiz Results Sales Page (Source: Carl Weische)

The sales page is structured to feel like the delivery of an individualized medical result, not a product listing.

**Above-the-fold elements (in order):**
- "Hair profile matched" and "Here's your growth plan" — language frames it as a result, not an offer
- Quiz-derived answers displayed to reinforce personalization
- "Bundle and save" framing leads — AOV optimization from first scroll
- Social proof + desired situation benefits immediately visible
- Urgency element showing simultaneous purchasers ("X people are looking at this right now")

**Bundle selection architecture:**
- Best-value bundle pre-selected and placed in center position
- Labeled "Recommended based on your quiz results" — personalization callback
- Subscribe/save option pre-selected to capture LTV
- Six-month supply framed as the duration required to hit the transformation promise made in the funnel

---

## Advanced Model: Personalized Supplement Formulation (Source: Carl Weische — Bionic Model)

Extension of the quiz funnel into mass-customization manufacturing. Entry point is either a long-form quiz OR blood test submission; both feed into an individual profile. Output is a custom supplement formulation unique to each customer.

Value proposition: eliminates over-supplementation and under-targeting vs. generic mass-market products. Celebrity equity (e.g., Ronaldo as shareholder) provides brand authority overlay. Represents the highest-differentiation version of the quiz funnel model — the quiz data doesn't just personalize the sales page, it determines the actual product formulation.

---

**Key Metrics & Benchmarks**
- Six-month supply framing is established before the prospect sees any pricing — AOV lift is structural, not persuasive
- Bundle pre-selection and subscribe pre-selection both occur at page load — default effect drives both AOV and LTV simultaneously
- Social proof is introduced at the exact moment the prospect has stated their desired outcome — relevance maximizes credibility impact
- Objection neutralization occurs inside the funnel, reducing the sales page's persuasion workload

**Common Pitfalls**
- Introducing social proof too early (before desired situation is stated) reduces its relevance and impact
- Failing to carry quiz data through to the sales page breaks personalization continuity and reduces conversion
- Not pre-selecting the recommended bundle or subscribe option leaves AOV and LTV on the table
- Generic loading screens without "analyzing your profile" framing waste the tension-building opportunity
- Placing objection handling only on the product page instead of pre-handling it inside the funnel leaves avoidable friction at the purchase decision point
- Skipping the transformation plan framing means the six-month supply appears expensive rather than necessary
