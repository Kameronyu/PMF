# Copywriting: Frameworks, Principles & AI-Assisted Production

Copywriting is the foundational skill driving all e-commerce and direct response results — every winning ad, funnel, and sales page is built on copy. These frameworks span classical direct response principles, structural frameworks, and modern AI-augmented workflows.

---

## Mass Desire Channeling (Source: Eugene Schwartz / Breakthrough Advertising)

Desire cannot be created by marketers — only channeled. The marketer's job is to locate pre-existing desire in the market and amplify it. Advertorials, listicles, and VSL pages function as desire-channeling mechanisms, warming cold traffic before the sales pitch. This principle from Breakthrough Advertising (~$500/copy) underpins all modern direct response frameworks and influenced every major marketing book written since.

---

## Levels of Awareness & Unique Mechanism (Source: Schwartz, Breakthrough Advertising)

Market awareness levels determine how to open and structure any marketing message. Awareness ranges from completely unaware → problem-aware → solution-aware → product-aware → most aware. The unique mechanism is the specific, differentiating reason a product works differently from all alternatives. These two concepts are the structural foundation for all modern sales copy architecture.

---

## Six Types of Sales Message Leads (Source: Michael Masterson, Great Leads)

Six distinct lead types cover the full range of sales message openings, each matched to a different awareness level and offer type. Pairs directly with Schwartz's awareness levels for maximum effectiveness. Mastery of these lead structures is prerequisite to writing high-converting long-form copy.

## Four Foundational Documents System (Source: Mark Builds Brands)

Before any copy is written, build four documents that serve as the persistent knowledge base for all marketing and AI prompting:
1. **Research Document** — 20–60 pages of customer insights from forums, Reddit, and social media (8–20 min AI deep research process using Gemini 2.5 Pro)
2. **Avatar Document** — target customer profile with verbatim pain-point quotes (e.g., "I can't afford another huge bill"), demographics, goals
3. **Offer Brief** — funnel architecture, awareness levels, unique mechanisms, consciousness levels, all copy ingredients
4. **Necessary Beliefs Document** — the specific beliefs a prospect must hold to purchase; marketing is reframed as belief-change work (shifting limiting → empowering beliefs)

All four documents are uploaded into a custom Claude project as persistent context. AI output quality is directly proportional to input quality. Once built, documents enable rapid generation of VSLs, ads, advertorials, and landing pages. Poor AI output = inadequate prompting, not tool failure.

## Claude AI Funnel Copywriting System (Source: Mark Builds Brands)

Claude (Opus 4.1 for generation, Sonnet 3.5 for production copy) is the preferred LLM for creative copywriting. A dedicated Claude "project" stores foundational documents and instructions persistently. Workflow: upload all four foundational docs + competitor swipe files → generate outline first → expand to full draft. Simple conversational prompts suffice once context is loaded. Sales page copy for a product can be produced in under 30 minutes. Competitor sales pages downloaded as PDFs and uploaded to ChatGPT enable deep research prompt extraction of buyer psychology and copywriting frameworks.

## AI-Augmented Hook & Ad Script Generation (Source: Mark Builds Brands)

**Hook Generation:** Load foundational documents into Claude; generate multiple hook variations via conversational prompts. Use Go Full Page browser extension to capture full competitor product pages as PDFs → feed into ChatGPT for psychological framing extraction → instruct AI to generate hooks based on proven market messaging. "You can literally steal the exact headlines and hooks that other offers have used that have generated millions."

**Ad Script Generation via GetHooked AI:** Input product description, target audience, and creativity score (0.7 recommended) → AI rewrites proven competitor transcripts for your product. Creativity score 0.7 balances proven structure with customization.

**Copy Chiefing:** After AI generation, read copy aloud multiple times or use ElevenLabs AI voice tool to identify friction points. Copy must flow like a "slippery slide with no bumps." AI review checks alignment with necessary beliefs, research doc, and copywriting principles; personal review checks natural flow and logical progression.

## Seven-Point Hook Checklist (Source: Mark Builds Brands)

Score any hook against seven elements; more checkmarks = higher performance. No single element is mandatory.
1. Curiosity — open loops
2. Pain point callout
3. Solution promise
4. Specificity — exact numbers and timeframes
5. Credibility/authority
6. Clear timeframe for results
7. Simplicity

*Example: "What happens to your body when you eat beef organs for 30 days" scores 4/7 (curiosity, solution promise, specificity, simplicity).*

## Three Hook Archetypes for Video Ads (Source: Mark Builds Brands)

1. **Problem-Solution:** "Here's how I fixed my dog's health problem in just [time]" / "If your dog has this symptom, stop giving them XYZ and try this instead"
2. **Surprise/Discovery:** "I can't believe what the vet said about this supplement" / "The shocking ingredient in most supplements that's harming your pet"
3. **Expert Authority:** "As a vet for 10 years, here's the only supplement I give my dogs"

All hooks focus on the problem being solved, never the product itself.

## PIG Method — Punch in the Gut (Source: Chris Haddad via Mark Builds Brands)

Trigger an intense low-energy emotional response (shame, anger, fear, despair) as early as possible, in the fewest words possible. Effectiveness scales inversely with word count. Particularly potent in women's markets (skincare, anti-aging, beauty). Example: "Is that a gray hair?" — 5 words triggering fear of aging. Example: "I love that you're confident enough to go without makeup" — triggers insecurity without overt aggression. A PIG-method-driven funnel generated $465,000 in revenue in 90 days.

## Four Elements of High-Converting Advertorial Headlines (Source: Mark Builds Brands)

Headline accounts for 90% of advertorial performance in split testing. Four simultaneous elements required:
1. **Self-Interest** — address "what's in it for me" directly to the target audience
2. **News Appeal** — frame like breaking news or a news article
3. **Curiosity** — use trigger words: "this," "why," "new"
4. **Quick & Easy** — present solution as fast and effortless (humans seek the laziest path)

---

## Advertorial Body Copy Structure (Source: Mark Builds Brands)

Six-step sequential structure taking readers from problem-aware to purchase-ready:
1. Unique mechanism of the problem (root cause framing)
2. Amplify pain (list specific symptoms, make pain tangible)
3. Introduce product with social proof and credibility
4. Competitor comparisons (show why alternatives fail)
5. Expert/doctor testimonials (third-party authority)
6. Outcome focus (sell what the product does, not what it is)

---

## Unique Mechanism Framing in Ad Body (Source: Mark Builds Brands)

Ad body (1–2 minutes) must cover two areas: (1) unique mechanism — why this product differs from competitors and why past attempts/competing solutions have failed; (2) how it works — specific process or ingredients. Both sections must connect to tangible customer outcomes, not abstract features. "People buy outcomes."

---

## Eight-Element Sales Page Framework (Source: Mark Builds Brands)

1. **Headline** — benefit-driven, must include 3–4 of 7 checklist elements
2. **Urgency** — time-limited offers create buying pressure
3. **Scarcity** — limited availability increases perceived value
4. **Guarantee/Risk Reversal** — eliminate customer risk
5. **Unique Mechanism** — explains why this solution works when others failed
6. **Social Proof** — testimonials that overcome objections or install empowering beliefs
7. **FAQ Section** — handles remaining objections
8. **Credibility Markers** — market-specific signals (not always doctors or celebrities)

---

## Benefit-First Product Copy Framework (Source: Mark Builds Brands)

Structure: one powerful benefit-driven sentence → three bullet points highlighting key outcomes. Mine Amazon reviews for real customer language. Use ChatGPT to refine. Write at fifth-grade reading level. Lead with outcome, never product specifications or features.

---

## Voice of Customer (VOCC) Copywriting (Source: Mark Builds Brands)

Copy must match how the target demographic actually speaks in daily life. AI-generated copy is detectable when it doesn't match customer language. The competitive advantage in AI-assisted copy is the human editor's ability to refine AI output to sound authentic — not prompting skill. Deep familiarity with customer communication patterns is the differentiating factor.

---

## Marketing Angle Differentiation for Saturated Markets (Source: Mark Builds Brands)

The same physical product can be marketed to entirely different customer segments using different pain/desire angles, creating untapped sub-markets within saturated categories. Example: same product marketed for weight loss vs. nerve pain relief targets different buyer segments. Zero cost to implement — requires only creativity and audience insight. Each distinct reason customers buy is a separate angle with its own addressable market.

---

## Facebook Ad Copy Compliance Rules (Source: Mark Builds Brands)

Facebook's AI analyzes text sentiment, not just keywords. Violations and penalties:
- **Aggressive transformation claims** (e.g., "lose 20 lbs in 2 days without diet or exercise") → ad rejection
- **All-caps writing** → not rejected but triggers artificially inflated CPM/ad costs as punishment
- **Profanity** → rejected; bleeped curse words are acceptable
- Primary text (caption above creative) is the second most important ad element

---

## Long-Form Direct Response Static Ads (Source: Mark Builds Brands)

High-performing current format: long-form direct response copy on a static image, built around an emotional story anchored in classical direct response principles (hook, agitation, solution, proof, CTA). Combines emotional narrative with proven structural frameworks.

**Common Pitfalls**
- Writing about product features instead of customer outcomes
- Skipping foundational documents and prompting AI without context (produces generic, low-converting copy)
- Using all-caps in Facebook ads (triggers cost inflation, not rejection — easy to miss)
- Making aggressive transformation claims that trigger Facebook AI rejection
- Consuming copywriting resources without implementing (applied knowledge beats theoretical knowledge)
- Neglecting copy in favor of product/design — copy is the most critical and most neglected e-commerce element
- Editing AI output without knowledge of VOCC — output sounds inauthentic to target demographic
- Overlooking angle differentiation in saturated markets — competing on product instead of messaging
