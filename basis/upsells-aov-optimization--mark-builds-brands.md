# Upsells & AOV Optimization

AOV (Average Order Value) optimization maximizes revenue per transaction by capturing incremental spend from customers already in a buying state, reducing effective CAC and increasing LTV without requiring additional acquisition spend.

## Post-Purchase OTO (One-Time Offer) (Source: Mark Builds Brands)

The moment immediately after payment is the highest emotional buying state a customer will ever be in — higher than pre-purchase browsing, higher than cart stage. Inserting an OTO page between payment confirmation and the thank-you page captures this peak state. The customer has already committed psychologically and financially; friction to an additional purchase is at its minimum.

**Implementation:**
1. Customer completes checkout and payment is collected
2. Redirect to OTO page (NOT thank-you page)
3. Present either: (a) a higher-priced item in the same category, or (b) a larger quantity of the same product at a volume discount
4. After OTO accept or decline, redirect to thank-you/confirmation page

**OTO Offer Types:**
- **Upgrade offer**: More expensive, premium version of what they just bought (higher margin, higher perceived value)
- **Quantity bundle**: More of the same product at a reduced per-unit price (increases volume, deepens commitment, reduces churn/reorder friction)

The acquisition cost is already sunk. Incremental revenue from an OTO has near-zero additional CAC, making every accepted OTO disproportionately profitable. Correctly executed OTOs do not damage customer relationships because the offer is presented after trust is established via completed purchase — the customer is not being asked to commit before they feel safe.

---

**Key Metrics & Benchmarks**
- OTO placement: strictly post-payment, pre-thank-you page — timing is the critical variable
- Two viable OTO structures: premium upgrade or bulk quantity; both exploit the same psychological peak-buying-state mechanism
- Incremental revenue per customer from OTOs carries near-0% additional CAC, making OTO conversion rate directly translatable to margin improvement

**Common Pitfalls**
- Sending customers directly to a thank-you page after purchase — wastes the highest-value moment in the customer journey
- Placing OTOs pre-purchase (on cart or checkout pages) — increases friction before commitment is made, reducing primary conversion rate
- Offering unrelated products as OTOs — breaks continuity of the buying decision and reduces acceptance rates; OTO must be logically connected to the just-purchased item
- Skipping OTOs entirely due to fear of seeming "pushy" — misunderstands customer psychology at post-purchase moment; a relevant offer is not relationship-damaging when timed correctly
