## HOOK ARCHITECTURE

**Hook as Slippery Slope (Open Loop Compounding)**
- Each line must create an open loop that makes the next line necessary
- Mechanism chain: stop scroll → open loop → partial info → new open loop → delayed resolution → pull into next section
- Pattern-interrupt alone stops scroll but fails at 2–3 seconds — open loops sustain past 7 seconds
- Every sentence serves one purpose: create the need to hear the next sentence
- Test: after each line, ask "would a viewer stop watching here?" If yes, add an open loop

**Hooks as Angle Communication Vehicles (with Worked Examples)**
- Hooks execute the angle — they are not the angle itself
- The angle is the sub-niche + pain/desire combo + positioning; the hook is how you communicate that angle in 3 seconds
- Direct always outperforms indirect/clever, especially for juniors
- Three direct-translation worked examples:
  - Angle: reduces swelling → Hook: "14 hours, no swelling"
  - Angle: fills like a meal → Hook: "This is a meal, not a protein shake"
  - Angle: stops snapping → Hook: "5 minutes to install, no more snapping"
- AI can generate sub-avatar hooks at scale but requires avatar profile as input — product brief alone produces generic output

## HOOK FORMULAS

**New Mechanism + Authority Stack Hook Formula**
- Formula: [unfamiliar named mechanism] + [revolution language] + [authority figure abandoning their solution] + [open loop]
- Each element breakdown:
  - Unfamiliar term = curiosity ("I've never heard of that")
  - Revolution language = FOMO ("I might be missing something")
  - Authority trashing their own product = implied superiority without making a direct claim
  - Accidental discovery framing = third open loop ("how did they find this?")
- Worked example: "The hollow fiber revolution that's making cardiologists trash their own compression socks happened completely by accident."
- Mirror avatar visually while delivering the audio hook — visual-audio congruency compounds effect

**Push-Pull Hook Structure**
- Push (alarming negative statement) + Pull (immediate positive resolution)
- Hard rule: complete the full push-pull within 5–10 seconds — do not extend the negative premise
- Dragging the negative premise extends anger/anxiety, not engagement
- Worked examples:
  - Scam reframe variant: "I think I just got scammed by every supplement brand for 10 years — until I found this."
  - Unexpected side-effect variant: "The weird side effect nobody talks about from [product category] is actually [desirable outcome]."
- Visual-audio congruency required: visuals must not contradict the emotional valence of the audio

**Ease-Based Hook Positioning**
- Leads with effortlessness to address self-doubt as the primary rejection barrier
- Structure: quantify effort minimally + state absence of friction
- Requires believable mechanism to avoid skepticism — ease without mechanism = unbelievable
- Worked example: "No toothbrush needed... try this for 30 seconds daily."
- When to use: primary barrier is implementation difficulty, not disbelief in result
- If doubt about the result is the barrier, use a mechanism or proof hook instead

**Desire Visual Hook Strategy**
- Open with a visual scene that embodies the viewer's core desire implicitly — without stating it
- Desire implied through action/scenario — bypasses rational resistance before it activates
- Why: explicit desire statement triggers the "I've heard this before" reflex; showing the desire as lived experience doesn't
- "Surface desires produce weak hooks — desire must be deep and real"
- Failure mode: using desire visual without sufficient avatar research → visuals resonate with the wrong audience

**Behavior-Specific Headline Principle**
- Headlines describe what the avatar is visibly DOING (observable behavior), not what they feel (emotional state)
- Test question before writing: "What are they actually doing right now?" not "How do they feel?"
- Behavioral precision triggers self-selection and suppresses unqualified clicks
- Visceral verbs compound the effect: "chugging" vs. "drinking," "devouring" vs. "eating"
- Worked examples:
  - "Constantly chugging water instead of eating"
  - "Eating toddler-sized portions but still gaining weight"
  - "Constantly chewing gum to avoid eating"
- Generate multiple variants per behavior — these are the highest-performing hook type for sub-avatars

## HOOK MECHANICS

**Negative Curiosity Distinction** [augments Mark's Hook section]
- Negative curiosity creates an information gap ("what are they about to tell me is wrong?") rather than a direct threat
- Distinction from fear hooks: fear hooks target self-preservation; negative curiosity targets intellectual dissonance
- Most effective at higher awareness levels where viewer has already tried solutions and failed — they're primed to hear "what you've been told is wrong"
- Examples: "The reason your collagen supplements aren't working (and what actually does)" vs. "Your skin is under attack"
- Use fear hooks for problem-aware audiences; use negative curiosity for solution-aware and product-aware audiences
