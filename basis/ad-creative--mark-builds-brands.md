# Ad Creative: Complete Framework Reference

Ad creative is the single highest-leverage variable in paid advertising performance, accounting for 90–95% of ad success across all major platforms. Creative quality now functions as both the message and the targeting mechanism, making it the primary competitive lever regardless of advertiser budget size.

---

## Creative Is the Targeting Principle (Source: Mark Builds Brands)

In 2025, Facebook's Andromeda AI analyzes every visual and textual element of an ad — flowers, mountains, ad copy sentiment, skin tone, gender, age, background — and uses this data to infer and target the correct audience automatically. Traditional media buying targeting is 10–20% or less of advertising success; creative does the remainder. High CPC is a creative problem, not a targeting problem. Improving creative quality is the primary lever for improving all soft metrics. This principle applies across Facebook, TikTok, Snapchat, Google, and YouTube — the advertiser with the best creative wins regardless of budget.

---

## Hook-First Creative Framework (90% Rule) (Source: Mark Builds Brands)

The hook — first 3–8 seconds of video or the opening visual of image ads — accounts for 90% of ad success. The hook is an audio-visual experience composed of three interdependent components:

- **Copy (text/spoken words):** ~80% of hook success. Example: "This device cured my bad posture in less than one week." Most viewers watch without sound, making text-driven copy critical.
- **Scroll-stopper visual (clip):** ~15% of hook success. Must be extremely high-curiosity footage matched congruently to the copy.
- **Audio (voiceover/music):** ~5% of hook success. AI voiceover, background music, or spokesperson. Least important but still required.

All three components must work in unison — failure of any single component destroys the entire hook. Always test minimum three hook variants against identical body and CTA.

---

## Hook Pre-Validation Traffic Campaign Test (Source: Mark Builds Brands)

Before investing in video production, validate hooks cheaply:
1. Create a traffic campaign (not conversion campaign)
2. Load 1 ad set with exactly 10 individual ads — white square box, black text displaying the hook copy in quotes, no video
3. Set automated rule: turn off each ad at 1,000 impressions
4. Run until all 10 ads are turned off
5. Hook with highest CTR wins and goes into the actual video ad

**Total test budget: maximum $50–$100.** Eliminates costly production risk before hook viability is proven.

## Winning Ad Anatomy: 5-Part Structure (Source: Mark Builds Brands)

Full video ad length: **60–90 seconds**, structured as:
1. **Hook (first 3–5 seconds):** highest-leverage section
2. **Body:** explains how the product uniquely solves the prospect's problem; more selling is better when done correctly
3. **First CTA (mid-ad):** kept simple, explicitly tells viewers how to buy — placed mid-ad to capture ready-to-buy viewers
4. **Testimonials/Social Proof section:** targets skeptical viewers who did not click first CTA; include before/after results, celebrity associations, media features, customer counts
5. **Implied close:** multiple CTAs throughout capture viewers at different decision points

## Four-Step Video Ad Creation Process (Swipe-Script-Source-Edit) (Source: Mark Builds Brands)

1. **Swipe:** Find winning competitor ads via GetHooked/Get Hooked (enhanced Facebook Ads Library). Filter: 4–5 star rating, currently running, English, under 60 seconds, video format, minimum 7-day runtime, minimum 5 usages in ad library. Target competitor brands with 300,000+ monthly website visits and 30+ active ads running.
2. **Script:** Rewrite competitor script using Claude 3.7 Sonnet for your specific product. Preserve proven structure; do not copy directly. Research by downloading competitor sales pages as PDFs and uploading to ChatGPT with deep research prompts.
3. **Source content:** AI UGC (Make UGC AI, $7–10/video), B-roll from Gridbank or Runway V3, custom product footage (ship to Fiverr creator or film with iPhone, $100–300/ad).
4. **Edit:** CapCut (free) or Fiverr editors (~$50–100/ad). Raw, minimally edited content with basic cuts and captions outperforms over-produced content. Excessive emojis and effects reduce performance.
5. **Bonus — Scale:** Increase spend on winning creatives once validated.

**Never steal competitor content one-for-one.** Facebook metadata detection and AI detection in 2025 cause account shutdowns. Original content achieves better CTR and day-one profitability.

## AI-Powered Video Ad Creation System (Source: Mark Builds Brands)

**Tool stack by function:**
- **Scripts/hooks:** Claude 3.7 Sonnet (outperforms other models in split tests)
- **B-roll footage:** Runway V3 (nightmare/dream scenario footage)
- **AI UGC:** Make UGC AI ($7–10/video) or Cling AI (image-to-video animation)
- **Product shots:** ChatGPT for mockups → Kling AI image-to-video to animate
- **Full video generation:** Sora 2 (maximum 15-second clips, portrait orientation, up to 3 simultaneous generations; mobile app supports custom characters for cross-clip consistency)
- **Realistic static images:** Higgsfield Soul model (most realistic image generation currently available)

**Sora 2 workflow:** Storyboard script into 15-second segments → generate Claude-written prompts per segment → generate in Sora 2 → assemble in editor. Full process executable in under one hour.

**Target hook rate: 50%+** for video ads.

## Static Image Ad Framework (Source: Mark Builds Brands)

Image ads are superior for early-stage scaling: full batch of 20 creatives producible in under one hour using AI tools, no editors or designers required. Creative fatigue only becomes relevant at $10K–$50K spend per creative, which most brands never reach. Lower CPMs than video ads in many cases.

**Two hook types for static image ads:**
- **Transformation hook:** before/after journey from problem state to dream state
- **Nightmare-only hook:** focuses solely on problem state without resolution; trickier but powerful (example: overweight person viewing scale displaying "you're fat")

**Research-first production process (Four-Document Foundation):**
1. Capture successful competitor's full sales page
2. Use ChatGPT to generate: customer avatar, operational brief, necessary beliefs document, research compilation
3. Feed these documents + reference image from Get Hooked into ChatGPT
4. Ask ChatGPT to write the image generation prompt (do not write prompts manually — AI-written prompts are significantly more detailed and effective)
5. Generate using Higgsfield (Soul for realistic UGC, Flux Context Max for animated style, GPT Image for text-on-creative)

**Sources for winning image models:** Get Hooked with filters: 4–5 star, image format, still running, minimum 7 days, English, Facebook/Instagram, 3+ creative usages, brand library 100+ ads.

## High-Volume Creative Testing Framework (Source: Mark Builds Brands)

- **Minimum volume:** 20–50 new creatives per week (not counting copy variations) for baseline product validation; top brands test 20–30 creatives per day (~1,000/month)
- **Win rate target:** 30–50% (3–5 profitable ads per 10 tested)
- **ROAS declines gradually when creative production slows**
- Most competitors test 2–3 creatives/week; testing 10–30+ creatives/week creates dominant competitive advantage

**80/20 Allocation (Pre-Andromeda):** 80% iterations of proven winners, 20% entirely new concepts. New concept creatives must be capable of sustaining $50K–$100K+ in spend.

**Flipped Post-Andromeda:** 80% new concepts, 20% variations (sometimes 100% new concepts). New concepts = new formats (VSL → static with long-form copy, VSL → short-form podcast ad), new buying angles, new audience segments. Variations = new hook on existing video, new thumbnail, new lead — small adjustments.

---

## Creative Diversity Testing Strategy: Post-Andromeda (Source: Mark Builds Brands)

Andromeda groups similar creatives and diminishes their individual reach. Load 25–50 genuinely distinct creatives per ad set (up from old recommendation of 3–5). All 25–50 must be unique concepts — 50 variations of the same video will be grouped by Andromeda, collapsing reach. 20 truly unique home-run creatives outperform 300 similar creatives.

## Ad Format Selection (Source: Mark Builds Brands)

- **Video ads:** superior for scaling from zero to $1K/day due to stability and scalability; use for primary conversion campaigns
- **Image ads:** faster iteration, lower CPM, no editor/designer required, superior for early-stage testing and angle validation
- **Retargeting:** simple static image ads with strong offer outperform creative-heavy executions; warm audiences need offer clarity, not creative novelty
- **Founder ads:** direct-to-camera from company founder creates immediate emotional connection and trust signals polished creative cannot replicate
- **Interview-format ads:** mirror high-performing organic social media content to reduce ad fatigue; translating organic-winning formats to paid increases paid performance

---

## Minimalist Editing Philosophy (Source: Mark Builds Brands)

Three required elements for a functional video ad: good script, compelling scroll-stopper opening, clear AI voiceover. Raw content with basic cuts and captions outperforms over-produced content. Excessive emojis and effects reduce performance. Failed ads still provide valuable data for iteration — testing is a core assumption, not a failure mode.

**Key Metrics & Benchmarks**
- Hook accounts for 90–95% of ad success
- Copy = ~80% of hook success; visual = ~15%; audio = ~5%
- Hook window: first 3–8 seconds
- Hook pre-validation test: $50–100 maximum, 10 variants, 1,000 impressions each
- Target hook rate (video): 50%+
- Full video ad length: 60–90 seconds
- Weekly creative testing minimum: 20–50 (beginners); 20–30/day for top brands (~1,000/month)
- Target win rate: 30–50% (3–5 profitable per 10 tested)
- Post-Andromeda creatives per ad set: 25–50 unique concepts (previously 3–5)
- Post-Andromeda concept/variation split: 80% new concepts / 20% variations
- New concept creative must sustain $50K–$100K+ in spend
- Creative fatigue threshold for image ads: $10K–$50K spend per creative
- Minimum competitor website visits for validation: 300,000/month
- Minimum competitor active ads for validation: 30
- Custom content production cost: $100–300/ad
- AI UGC cost (Make UGC AI): $7–10/video
- Fiverr editing cost: $50–100/ad
- Media buying as share of ad success: 10–20% or less
- Revenue attributed to image-first system: $44K/day
- Sora 2 clip maximum: 15 seconds; max simultaneous generations: 3

**Common Pitfalls**
- Stealing competitor content one-for-one → account shutdowns, payment holds, Facebook metadata/AI detection
- Over-producing creatives (excessive emojis, effects, polish) → lower performance than raw content
- Adjusting targeting instead of creative when CPC is high → wasted optimization effort
- Testing fewer than 20 creatives/week → insufficient data for product/angle validation
- Loading 25–50 variations of the same asset post-Andromeda → Andromeda groups them, collapsing individual reach
- Continuing old 80/20 variation-heavy testing post-Andromeda → underperformance vs. concept-first allocation
- Reinventing creative from scratch without competitor research → introduces unnecessary variables
- Relying on a single winning creative → ROAS decline when it fatigues with no replacement pipeline
- Testing only volume without improving win rate → diminishing returns on spend
- Skipping four-document research foundation before image ad generation → non-converting creatives despite quality visuals
- Writing AI image generation prompts manually instead of using AI to write them → significantly less detailed and effective outputs
