# Landing Pages: Conversion Frameworks & Architecture

Landing pages are single-purpose conversion assets where copy, media, and design interact to move visitors toward a specific action. Understanding the hierarchy of these elements determines where to invest optimization effort.

## Three-Element Conversion Model (Source: Mark Builds Brands)

Landing pages consist of exactly three components: copy (words), media (images/videos/GIFs), and design/aesthetics. These elements are not equal — copy and media are the primary conversion drivers; design only matters when it creates friction in the sales process. If copy and media are correctly optimized, design becomes irrelevant to conversion outcomes.

**Priority order for optimization:**
1. Copy — the core persuasion layer; must be researched from actual customer language
2. Media — visual proof, demonstration, and emotional resonance
3. Design/aesthetics — only address if it introduces friction (slow load, illegible text, confusing layout)

The single most important factor across all three elements is **clarity**. Confused minds do not buy. Every element on the page must reduce cognitive load, not increase it. Customer research (now acceleratable with AI tools) is the prerequisite for copy that converts, because effective copy mirrors the customer's own language back at them.

**Key Metrics & Benchmarks**
- No specific conversion rate benchmarks provided in source frameworks; optimization priority is copy > media > design
- Clarity of value proposition is the primary lever for conversion improvement

**Common Pitfalls**
- Over-investing in design/aesthetics before copy and media are validated
- Writing copy from the brand's perspective rather than the customer's researched language
- Treating all three elements as equally important rather than following the conversion hierarchy
- Adding visual or layout complexity that increases friction instead of reducing it
