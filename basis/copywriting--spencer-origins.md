## VOICE OF CUSTOMER RESEARCH

**Command+F Verbatim Verification**
- Before publishing any copy, run Command+F on all research documents for every assumed customer word
- If a word doesn't appear in research, remove it from copy
- Worked example: "hydrated" appears zero times in research; customer actually says "smoother and less dry"
- Prevents plausible-but-wrong word choices that feel right but miss resonance
- Apply to every adjective, verb, and descriptor before finalizing

**Casual Referral Language Extraction**
- Elicit how customers describe the product to a friend — not in complaint/review language
- Prompt to customers: "Make this less salesy and more like you're just telling a friend about it"
- Indicators of authentic referral language: triggering problem, uncertainty, specific time references, social proof framing
- Example: "I've had it for 8 months, everyone stops and asks me about it" — natural social proof with specificity, no pain framing
- Tactic: text actual customers unprompted and capture their response
- Capture this register separately from Amazon/Reddit reviews — it's a different linguistic mode

## AVATAR CONSTRUCTION

**Core Five Avatar Categories + Revenue Scale Thresholds**
- Avatar-building priority order: (1) Desires, (2) Experiences, (3) Emotions, (4) Behaviors/Habits, (5) Demographics (last — demographics are a byproduct of the above)
- Scale guidance:
  - $0–100K: 1 core avatar, go deep
  - $100K–500K: same avatar, better execution — don't expand yet
  - $500K+: 2–3 avatars
  - $1M+: expand surface area further
- Comfort (hoodie brand) benchmark: 10,000+ videos/week at $500M+ attributed to desire/behavior targeting, not demographics

**Behavior-Based Avatar Targeting + Ego Mechanism**
- Calling out what people DO is more powerful than who they ARE demographically
- Behaviors tied to identity → ego purchasing (they buy because it confirms who they are)
- Inverse rule: the more specific the avatar, the weaker supporting elements need to be — specificity does the heavy lifting
- Hook example: "Are you scrolling Instagram looking at mom body transformations?" — behavior callout, not demographic

**Sub-Avatar Research Method (400+ Comment Signal)**
- Break broad audience into 2–4 behavioral sub-avatars
- Search Reddit per sub-avatar's specific pain/behavior combo
- Comment count signal: 400–620+ comments = high pain intensity thread worth mining
- Full process:
  1. Define core desire
  2. Identify 2–4 sub-avatars behaviorally
  3. Reddit search per sub-avatar's exact pain/behavior
  4. Document verbatim quotes
  5. Feed raw quotes into Claude → generate 3–5 headlines per angle
  6. Select and refine
- Example sub-avatars for appetite-control product: The Dieter, The Water Drinker, The Bored Eater

**Desire-to-Headline 12×2 Matrix**
- 12 desire sources: 6 mass instincts (Health/Survival, Status, Sex/Relationships, Comfort, Control, Belonging) + 6 technological problem categories (complexity, overwhelm, fragility, maintenance, incompatibility, obsolescence)
- 2 forces of change: Style Trend OR Mass Education
- 12 × 2 = 24 total headline combinations per product
- Worked dog harness examples: each instinct × each force = distinct angle
- Use as generative scaffold to exhaust all angles systematically before selecting

**Identity Label Technique**
- Name a specific identity label the customer uses to describe themselves
- Weak: "busy mom" — Specific: "the mom who already ordered Halloween costumes in August"
- Specificity-drives-resonance chain: relatability → trust → purchase
- More examples: "drop shipper," "last-minute kind of person," "marketing nerd"
- Rule: use the identity label they give themselves, not the one you assign them

## MECHANISM WRITING

**Mechanism Villain Framework**
- Name the specific biological/chemical/structural villain as root cause
- Structure: Name villain → competitors only fix symptoms → product blocks source
- Rule: re-explain the mechanism each time it's mentioned — never assume carry-through
- Worked example: DPP4 enzyme destroys GLP-1 ("fullness hormone") — product blocks DPP4
- Supporting data: 58% GLP-1 increase, 25% fewer calories in 7 days, 90 women 12-week study
- Frames product as defender/guardian, not just a better version of existing solutions

**[Feature → HOW it works → Functional Outcome] 3-Step Chain** [augments Weische's Unique Mechanism section]
- Formula: [Feature name] → [HOW it works mechanically] → [Functional Outcome for customer]
- Worked examples:
  - "Stainless steel interior" → resists bacterial biofilm → water stays fresh for 72 hours
  - "CarePod" → UV-C light chamber → kills 99.9% of bacteria without chemicals
  - "Peptide 47D" → signals collagen synthesis at receptor level → visible results in 14 days
- The HOW step is what competitors skip — it's the persuasion engine

**New Information as Mechanism** [augments Mark's New Information section]
- Deep research (not AI-generated) to find non-obvious factual education that reframes the problem
- Verified-fact requirement — surface-level education fails; must be genuinely non-obvious
- AG1 benchmark: $5M clinical investment → positioned as clinically-backed → $600M business
- 30–67% partial awareness rationale: use counterintuitive hooks because 30–67% of audience has heard a concept but doesn't fully understand it
- Credibility-destruction test: if 67%+ of audience already knows it, don't claim it's a discovery
- Worked example: REM sleep — most know the term, few understand architecture → education opportunity
- Soup brand: IBS-specific information → $13K spend → $90K revenue following month

## COMPLIANCE & VALENCE

**Valence × Intensity Emotional Scaling**
- Two-axis framework for copy safety and effectiveness:
  - Valence: positive (aspiration) vs. negative (fear/threat)
  - Intensity: mild → extreme
- High intensity + low valence (extreme negative) = fear-mongering → Meta ban + Shopify removal + brand destruction
- Family member test: would you show this ad to a family member you care about?
- Acceptable: "Complete darkness may support long-term memory health"
- Unacceptable: "Brain health emergency — protect yourself now"
- Rule: stay within the acceptable quadrant — high enough intensity to motivate, positive enough to avoid compliance flags

**Lower Valence Copy Template** [augments Weische's Pain/Fear Hook section]
- "You might have this problem" (self-assessment trigger) vs. "You will get this result" (aspirational frame)
- Self-assessment explanation: viewer applies the condition to themselves, triggering internal confirmation — more powerful than being told
- When to use: audiences who are skeptical of direct claims, or when direct pain callout risks fear-mongering flags
- The "might" frame is often safer for Meta compliance while maintaining resonance

**Implied Claim Technique**
- State the verifiable literal fact; let the customer's brain complete the logical chain to the claim you can't make directly
- "Cardiologists are trashing their own compression socks because of this mechanism" → implies superiority without claiming it directly
- Compliance benefit: avoids superlative claim issues (no "best," "most effective," etc.)
- Works because the implied conclusion feels like the viewer's own reasoning, not a pitch

## PERSUASION STRUCTURES

**Show Don't Tell Script Principle**
- Every benefit claim needs a corresponding behavioral scene
- Telling: "Eating 1,200 calories is easy with this product"
- Showing: "I have breakfast and I'm still not even hungry by dinner. Sometimes I just skip lunch."
- Why: Showing lets viewer draw their own conclusion rather than being pitched — reduces sales resistance
- Copy-editing rule: for every claim, write the behavioral scene that makes the claim unnecessary

**Escalation Technique**
- Exaggerate benefit to comedic or dramatic extreme to make it memorable and shareable
- Must stay tied to real benefit — escalation without grounding = lying
- Examples:
  - Easy-to-clean humidifier → "If Mr. Clean invented a humidifier"
  - Hair growth product → "Take too many and you turn into Chewbacca"
- Reference: Harmon Brothers style
- Most brands present claims flatly — escalation creates standout in a flat landscape

**Framework-Level Thinking (Steal Frameworks, Not Words)**
- Identify the psychological structure a winning hook operates on → re-express entirely in different words
- Never copy word-for-word — map the structure, rebuild entirely
- Example: "I think I just got scammed" = [negative event] + [stakes established] + [curiosity gap opened]
  → Re-express: "My doctor was wrong for 10 years" = [authority invalidated] + [stakes] + [curiosity gap]
- Cross-niche stealing: the further from your niche, the safer and more novel the transplant
- Process: watch competitors → extract framework → rebuild in your context

**Contrast Headline Formula** [augments Mark's Headline section]
- Formula: [difficult/unpleasant thing] is [easy/achievable] with [mechanism/product]
- Creates cognitive dissonance → viewer asks "how?" → engagement follows
- Example: "Losing 30 lbs is now as easy as making one swap in your morning routine"
- The contrast between the expected difficulty and claimed ease is the hook mechanism

**Sequential CTA Closing Stack (6-Step Order)** [augments Mark's Closing/CTA section]
- Step order:
  1. Transformation vision (paint the after-state)
  2. Mechanism distinction (why this, not others)
  3. Social proof bridge (others like them succeeded)
  4. Guarantee (de-risk the decision)
  5. Price anchor (reframe cost against alternative)
  6. Brand tagline (identity stamp)
- Third-person framing for guarantee copy: "Thousands of customers have..." vs. "We guarantee..." — reduces pitch perception
- Each step must logically flow from the previous — don't reorder

## LANGUAGE & WORD CHOICE

**Power Word Substitution + Repetition Rules**
- Replace neutral language with high-stakes power words throughout entire ad
- Substitution pairs:
  - "issue" → "scammed"
  - "popular" → "viral"
  - "misleading" → "chronically lying"
- Repetition rule: say the highest-value word twice verbally + show visually 4 times
- Urgency phrase: "Good luck" implies competition + scarcity simultaneously without claiming either directly

**Price-Per-Unit Value Reframe**
- Break total price into smallest meaningful unit
- $X for 17 meals = $2.65/meal vs. the typical $10 meal
- Pair with quality justifier in same sentence: "$2.65/meal AND 40 grams of protein"
- Applies to subscriptions, multi-unit products, and recurring-use items
- Rule: always pair the low unit price with a quality signal — low price alone reads as low value

## AI WORKFLOW

**Hemingway App + Grade 6 Threshold** [augments Mark's Readability section]
- Tool: hemingwayapp.com
- Target: Grade 6 reading level for all direct response copy
- Benchmark: one Claude rewrite pass can move Grade 8 → Grade 4
- Exact Claude rewrite prompt: "Rewrite this at a sixth grade level. For context, here is the original question I am answering."

**Two Claude Inputs Not in Standard Workflow** [augments Mark's Claude Workflow section]
- Input 1: Screenshotted ad comments — feed as images into Claude. Comments contain objections + buying reasons in customer language that reviews don't capture (more spontaneous, more emotional, more specific to your exact ad)
- Input 2: "Where did I mess up?" reverse-review prompt after completing draft. Prompt Claude to attack the draft as a skeptical customer — surfaces logic gaps before launch
