# Optional formulas — anatomy + a worked example each

Reach for one when it fits the angle. None is a required slot order; the source's attempt
to force a fixed sequence is exactly what produced same-y hooks. Use these to vary, not to
standardize.

## New-Mechanism + Authority Stack
[unfamiliar named mechanism] + [revolution language] + [authority abandoning their solution] + [open loop].
- unfamiliar term → curiosity; revolution language → FOMO; authority trashing their own
  product → implied superiority without a direct claim; accidental-discovery → a third loop.
- Example: "The hollow fiber revolution that's making cardiologists trash their own compression socks happened completely by accident."

## Push-Pull
[push: alarming negative] + [pull: immediate positive resolution]. Complete within 5–10s;
do not drag the negative.
- Example: "I think I just got scammed by every supplement brand for 10 years — until I found this."

## Ease-Based
[quantify effort minimally] + [state absence of friction]. Requires a believable mechanism.
Use when the barrier is implementation difficulty, not disbelief in the result.
- Example: "No toothbrush needed... try this for 30 seconds daily."

## Desire-Visual
Open on a visual scene that embodies the viewer's core desire implicitly — shown as lived
experience, never stated (stating it triggers "I've heard this before"). Needs avatar
research or it resonates with the wrong audience. Surface desires make weak hooks; go deep.

## Behavior-Specific Headline
Describe observable behavior, not emotion — "what are they actually doing right now?"
Visceral verbs compound it; behavioral precision self-selects the qualified viewer.
- Examples: "Constantly chugging water instead of eating" · "Eating toddler-sized portions but still gaining weight."
