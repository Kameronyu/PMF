# Hooks — write the line that stops the scroll

A hook executes an **angle** (sub-niche + pain/desire + positioning) in three seconds —
it is not the angle itself. Your job is the *wording* a viewer feels. Lead from the
specimens below; reach for a formula only if it helps; keep it short and direct.

## Start here — real winning specimens (study these before writing)

These are preserved verbatim from the source. They steer you harder than any rule.

- **Push-Pull (scam reframe):** *"I think I just got scammed by every supplement brand for 10 years — until I found this."*
  — alarming negative, then immediate resolution; the betrayal emotion buys attention before any claim.
- **New-Mechanism + Authority:** *"The hollow fiber revolution that's making cardiologists trash their own compression socks happened completely by accident."*
  — unfamiliar term (curiosity) + authority abandoning their own solution (implied superiority) + "by accident" (a third open loop).
- **Direct angle→hook translations** (direct beats clever, especially at scale):
  *"14 hours, no swelling"* · *"This is a meal, not a protein shake"* · *"5 minutes to install, no more snapping"*
- **Ease-Based:** *"No toothbrush needed... try this for 30 seconds daily."*
  — quantify effort minimally + state the absence of friction; needs a believable mechanism or it reads as too-good.
- **Behavior-Specific headlines** (describe what the avatar is visibly DOING, with visceral verbs):
  *"Constantly chugging water instead of eating"* · *"Eating toddler-sized portions but still gaining weight"* · *"Constantly chewing gum to avoid eating"*

More specimens, with the angle each executes, are in `references/specimen-bank.md`.

## Craft targets (steer by these; they are not a rulebook)

- **Momentum.** Every line exists to make the next line necessary — an open loop the
  viewer must close by continuing. One dead line and they're gone.
- **Direct over clever.** State the angle plainly; wit that hides it is a leak.
- **Specificity over superlative.** A concrete number, named outcome, or timeframe beats
  "fast" / "amazing." Enter the desire the reader already has; don't announce it.
- **Behavioral precision.** Name what they're *doing*, not what they *feel* — "what are
  they actually doing right now?" Visceral verbs ("chugging," "devouring") compound it.
- **Fewest words that keep the meaning.** Draft, then cut every word that adds no new
  information or pull.

## Optional patterns (reach for one; never force a fixed slot order)

Push-Pull · New-Mechanism+Authority · Ease-Based · Desire-Visual · Behavior-Specific.
Each is a shape to borrow, not a required sequence. Anatomy and a worked example for each
is in `references/formulas.md`.

## Contract rules (these DO bind — they pick the shape, not the words)

- **Awareness routing:** fear hook for *problem-aware*; **negative curiosity** ("the
  reason your X isn't working — and what does") for *solution-aware* and *product-aware*.
- **Push-Pull timing:** complete the full push→pull within **5–10 seconds**; do not drag
  the negative premise (that extends anxiety, not engagement).
- **Ease needs a mechanism:** lead on ease only when the barrier is implementation
  difficulty; if the barrier is disbelief in the result, use a mechanism/proof hook.
- **Visual–audio congruency:** for video, the visual must not contradict the audio's
  emotional valence or subject.

## How to use this with the grader

Generate several diverse hooks (vary the formula and the angle; don't iterate one toward
"safe"). Then hand them to **kb-hooks-score** — a separate skill, ideally a different
model — for the gates and the pairwise-vs-specimen judgment. Do not grade your own
output here; the writer that judges itself optimizes toward "looks like a hook," not
"stops the scroll." The banned-phrase / round-number / length checks live there, not here.

## Self-test (is this a real hook draft?) — any NO, revise

- Does the first line buy attention before any product claim, and make line two necessary?
- Is it direct — the angle legible in one read — and as short as the meaning allows?
- Does it carry at least one concrete specific (number, named outcome, or timeframe)?
- Did you produce several diverse candidates rather than one polished safe line?
