## DESIRE FRAMEWORK

**Two Forces That Create Desire**
- 2 permanent forces (always present):
  1. Mass Instincts — hardwired biological drives
  2. Mass Technological Problems — friction created by technology adoption
- 2 forces of change (time-dependent):
  1. Style Trends — social/cultural shifts in what's desirable
  2. Mass Education — information that changes what people want or fear
- Mass Education example: a published GLP-1 risk report would collapse Ozempic demand overnight — information is the mechanism, not the product

**Six Mass Instincts (Expanded)** [augments Weische's Mass Market Desires section]
- Full list (priority ranked — top 3 are "powerhouses for scaling"):
  1. Health / Survival
  2. Status
  3. Sex / Relationships
  4. Comfort
  5. Control
  6. Belonging
- Money satisfies instincts 1–5 simultaneously — explains why money is the universal desire proxy
- Previous 4-instinct models are incomplete — Comfort and Control are distinct, high-revenue instinct categories

**Permanent vs. Trending Desire Strategic Split** [augments Weische's Desire section]
- Build core business on permanent desires (Health, Status, Sex — always present)
- Use trending desires for sprint acceleration (seasonal, cultural moments)
- iPhone example: taps all three permanent desires simultaneously (status + health/productivity + relationships)
- Valentine's Day / crypto examples: trending — strong sprint window, no long-term dependency
- Rule: trending desire = ad campaign; permanent desire = product category and brand positioning

**Desire Evaluation Framework (Scope → Urgency → Staying Power)**
- Three-axis ranked model for pre-commitment desire evaluation:
  1. Scope (most important): how many people feel this desire? (market size)
  2. Urgency: how badly do they want it now? (conversion speed)
  3. Staying Power: will they want it again? (LTV / repeat purchase)
- Worked example: "blackout a nursery one time" = low Staying Power = single-purchase product = bad for LTV
- Drug addict urgency analogy = maximum urgency benchmark (useful for calibrating "is this really urgent?")
- Assessment sequence: Scope first (is the market big enough?) → Urgency (can we convert cold?) → Staying Power (can we build a brand?)

**Two-Axis Desire Scoring Model** [augments Weische's Desire/Scoring section]
- Intensity × Scope as pre-commitment viability filter
- High intensity + small scope = niche product (viable but ceiling)
- Low intensity + large scope = commodity (requires massive reach to make unit economics work)
- High intensity + large scope = scaling product
- Research signal phrases that indicate desire (not just problem): "I was looking for X," "I was in need of X"

**Spending Patterns as Revealed Desire Signal** [augments Weische's VoC section]
- Where money is currently being spent = the strongest signal of actual desire (not stated preference)
- Strongest signal because it's revealed behavior, not survey response
- Use to validate: if no competing products exist with meaningful revenue, the desire may not be strong enough to support ad spend

**"I Wish" Attainability Caveat** [augments Weische's VoC section]
- Verify that a stated "wish" is actually attainable before building a campaign around it
- "I wish I could fly" ≠ addressable desire
- "I wish I could fall asleep in under 10 minutes" = addressable desire (mechanism can plausibly deliver)
- Rule: desire must be both real AND achievable by your mechanism

## SEASONAL & TEMPORAL PSYCHOLOGY

**Seasonal Spending Cycle Awareness**
- Monthly calendar tied to dopamine cycles, financial reality, and social context:
  - Late Aug / Sep: 15–30% resistance increase (financial tightening after summer — not a creative problem)
  - October: Halloween/fall spending spike
  - November/December: gifting-mode requires full messaging frame shift
  - January: 35–40% health supplement surge
  - February: Valentine's Day — $27.5B 2025 record
  - July (not September): Back-to-school — 67% of purchases by end of July
- Implication: poor performance in late Aug/Sep = seasonal effect, not necessarily funnel failure — do not make permanent decisions from seasonal data

**Desire Calendar by Month**
- Full month-by-month table: dominant desire + biological driver + cultural event + spend data
- Inverse use: if primary market is in-season, competitors are flooding that desire → target the opposite desire
  - Example: January health surge = opportunity for comfort/indulgence brands targeting "recovery from restriction"
- Key pitfalls: never pull December health brand spend as benchmark; run back-to-school ads in July not September

## AVATAR CONSTRUCTION

**Sub-Avatar Construction Framework (Core Five Categories)**
- Five-category layering to build one testable sub-avatar:
  1. Desires (required core — start here)
  2. Demographics
  3. Emotions
  4. Behaviors / Habits
  5. Product Experiences (always include outcome they expect)
- Each unique combination across these categories = one testable sub-avatar
- Revenue benchmarks: $0–100K = 1 core avatar; $100K–$1M = 2–3; $1M+ = several
- Label sub-avatars using audience's own language: "night shifters," "elderly early nighters," "the mom who already ordered Halloween costumes"

**Emotion Layer: Primary + Secondary Classification** [augments Weische's Dual-System section]
- Primary emotions: joy, sadness, fear, disgust, anger, surprise
- Secondary emotions (what customers report): anxious, frustrated, overwhelmed, defeated
- Always unpack secondary emotions to their underlying primary cluster
  - "Overwhelmed" → primary = fear + disgust
  - "Frustrated" → primary = anger
- Recommendation: desire-based avatars are primary; emotions are sub-avatar refinement layer (not the foundation)

**Five-Category Reddit Research Protocol for Sub-Avatars**
- After desire is identified, search four remaining categories independently on Reddit:
  1. Emotion — "feeling [emotion] about [category]"
  2. Behavior — "people who [specific behavior]"
  3. Demographic — "[demographic] struggling with [category]"
  4. Product experience — "[product category] results" / "did [product] work for you"
- Capture self-identifying labels from each category
- Feed raw Reddit quotes into Claude for headline generation
- Worked output: "Magnesium got you sleeping longer. This gets you sleeping deeper." — arose from emotion + product experience combo mining

**Customer Comment Mining for Avatar Discovery**
- 5-step closed-loop process for turning a Facebook comment into a validated sub-avatar:
  1. Contact commenter (DM or email from comment thread)
  2. Record call
  3. Transcribe call
  4. Feed transcript to Claude for pain language extraction
  5. Validate AI-generated pain language directly back with customer before using in copy
- Warning: AI may guess "throbbing pain" when real language is "pins and needles and stabbing pains" — validation step is non-negotiable
- One validated comment spawned a new avatar → dedicated creative → 5.5 ROAS within 48 hours

## DESIRE RESEARCH OPERATIONS

**Desire Hunting Research Protocol** [augments Weische's VoC section]
- 1-hour time cap per research session — prevents over-studying without action
- Three-column spreadsheet structure: Desires/Wants/Needs | Customer Language Snippets | Rejected Ideas (what didn't pass Command+F verification)
- "Do not paraphrase" as operational rule — verbatim only
- Desired state must be captured in their words: "smoother and less dry" not "hydrated"
