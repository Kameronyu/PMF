# Deep Market Analysis Framework

Runs after a transformation-niche pair (NTP) has passed the four market-selection gates. The pair is already qualified. The job now is to extract the raw material that builds the funnel: the proven sales messaging to deploy, and the funnel container to deploy it in, calibrated to the awareness state of the buyer you are actually selling to.

This flow is built for a crowdfunding launch, but the structure generalizes.

---

## The core structure: two lenses, one pool, one merge

You are not building two competitor lists. You run **two extraction lenses** over one competitor pool, then **merge** their outputs into the deployable plan.

- **Structure lens** — extracts the funnel container and buyer-awareness positioning.
- **Messaging lens** — extracts the proven sales angles, claims, and emotional frames.
- **Merge** — pours the messaging from the messaging lens into the container from the structure lens, calibrated to your buyer's real awareness state.

**Which lens applies to a source is keyed to transformation match, not source type.**

| Source | Structure lens | Messaging lens |
|---|---|---|
| Crowdfunding project, in your transformation | Yes (primary — same container) | Yes |
| Crowdfunding project, off your transformation | Yes (structure only) | **No — do not take their specific messaging** |
| DTC brand, in your transformation | Partial (different container, weight lower) | Yes (primary — battle-tested on real spend) |

The crowdfunding-vs-DTC distinction only tilts *which lens is richer* for that source. Crowdfunding projects are the strongest structure source because they solve the problem unique to your launch: selling a promise, not a product. In-transformation DTC brands are the strongest messaging source because their angles are proven against people spending real money on a real, shipping product.

---

## Why crowdfunding structure is its own analysis

A crowdfunding backer is in a structurally unusual state: they are buying a promise of a product that does not exist yet. A normal DTC funnel never has to carry "will this actually ship" credibility. A crowdfunding funnel's central job is exactly that. So the structure lens has to capture trust-and-shipping scaffolding that has no equivalent in standard DTC teardown. This is why you study crowdfunding projects for the container even when their transformation is nothing like yours — the container solves a problem the messaging sources never had to.

---

## STRUCTURE LENS — extract from crowdfunding projects (as comparable as possible)

For each comparable recent successful project, collect:

**Funnel sequence.** What each page does, in what order. Ad → pre-launch capture → project page → pledge → confirmation, and what work each step performs.

**Offer mechanic.** Deposit, tiered pledges, early-bird structure, founder pricing. What is the hook that makes a backer commit now rather than wait.

**Urgency and scarcity construction.** Batch limits, price-step deadlines, founder-batch caps, countdane. How is "now" manufactured when the product ships months out.

**Trust and shipping scaffolding.** This is the load-bearing, crowdfunding-specific part. The risks-and-challenges section, timeline credibility, founder story, manufacturing transparency, update cadence. How they answer "will this ship" before the backer asks it.

**Awareness positioning.** How much the page assumes the backer already knows. How much problem-education runs before the ask. This reads the buyer's awareness state, which the merge step needs.

Decision this feeds: the container you build, and the specific backer-credibility moves you borrow. **Off-transformation projects contribute all of the above and none of their messaging.**

---

## MESSAGING LENS — extract from in-transformation brands (crowdfunding and DTC)

For each brand selling your transformation, or your transformation-category, to your niche:

**Claims, tagged by type.** What transformation they promise. Tag each direct / enlarged / mechanism / enhanced so they aggregate in the merge.

**Products and mechanisms.** What they sell and the reason-why they attach to it.

**Niche, precisely.** Who exactly they target. Confirms the proven spend is proven for your niche, not an adjacent one.

**Angles and emotional framing.** How the pitch is framed in ads and on-site. This is the proven persuasion you are here to harvest. Collect the angle regardless of the awareness temperature the competitor ran it at — a winning angle is collected once, then warmed or cooled at deployment. Do not throw away a proven cold angle because you intend to deploy warm.

**Awareness level they sell to.** What awareness state their messaging targets. Needed so the merge knows how far each angle has to be shifted for your buyer.

**Sophistication signals.** Extraordinary identification on-site, UGC review volume, press coverage, celebrity endorsement. Reads competitor strength and the table-stakes proof level the market now expects.

**Estimated revenue.** Confirms this is a brand worth borrowing from.

**Offer and bundle structure.** What they bundle, what the offer looks like, price points. What the market is conditioned to pay for this transformation.

**Brand loyalty signals.** Repeat-purchase language, community, subscription.

**Review mine.** Hidden use cases, what they like, what they don't, who they actually are. **Treat review mining as a primary angle-discovery source, not just a differentiation check.** The winning deployable angle often lives in what reviewers care about, not in competitor marketing (speed-as-mechanism was found in reviews, not ads). This sits parallel to studying competitor ads, not after it.

Decision this feeds: the proven angles and claims you deploy, and the whitespace you own.

---

## THE MERGE — the actual deliverable

Everything above is raw material. The merge is the testable plan. Produce:

**Proven angle set.** The battle-tested angles and claims from the messaging lens, ranked by evidence strength.

**Dead ground.** The saturated claims to avoid. Count claim frequency across the pool — 5+ competitors making the same claim marks it dead, discount it regardless of copy quality. Note: this claim count is *not* re-litigating market entry (that was settled at selection Gate 3). Here it maps where messaging goes — what to avoid — not whether to proceed.

**Whitespace.** The open claims you can own. What is left after the dead ground is removed, cross-referenced against your UM and your review-mined angles.

**The container.** The funnel structure and backer-credibility scaffolding from the structure lens.

**Awareness calibration.** Identify your crowdfunding backer's actual awareness state (read off the structure lens's awareness positioning). Then specify how each borrowed angle is warmed or cooled to land in that state, inside that container. Awareness is a property of deployment, not of the collected angle — the same angle deploys at different temperatures for different awareness states.

The merge output is one congruent plan: these proven angles, with these claims, avoiding this dead ground and owning this whitespace, deployed in this crowdfunding container, calibrated to a backer at this awareness state. That is what gets built and tested.

---

## Separate output: channel-edge finding

Not part of either lens or the merge. While collecting, note which in-transformation competitors are NOT scaling on a given channel (e.g. not on Meta). A channel where proven-transformation demand exists but strong competitors are absent is a first-mover distribution edge. This feeds channel selection, not page construction. File it separately so it does not contaminate the messaging-and-structure work.

---

## What changed from the working draft

- The "keep claims under 5" rule was a *selection* filter (Gate 3) imported into *deep analysis*. Removed as an entry test. The same claim count now serves a different job here: mapping dead ground vs whitespace, not deciding entry.
- Warm-vs-cold is a deployment property, not a collection property. You do not hunt for temperature-matched competitor messaging. Collect proven angles regardless of temperature; warm or cool them at the merge.
- The "evaluate market" in-progress section was filed as a third collection bucket. It is not — it is the start of the merge step (synthesis of the two lenses), not a third source to collect from.
- Review mining was buried under "differentiate." Promoted to a primary angle-discovery source running parallel to competitor-ad study.
